Ethical Implications of Genetic Engineering in Humans


Genetic engineering, or the modification of an organism’s inherited characteristics, has been a topic of widespread debate and exploration. In particular, human genetic engineering has sparked both curiosity and concern over the potential repercussions. In the realm of human gene-editing, several crucial issues emerge, demanding a broader public discourse on the matter.

The basic premise of gene-editing technology lies in the ability to isolate and alter specific DNA sequences. Early gene-editing methods include chemical and radiation exposure, which induce mutations that can lead to genetic changes. However, more recent advancements employ molecular techniques such as CRISPR-Cas9, a revolutionary tool for precise gene editing. With this technology, scientists are capable of repairing or adding genetic material within a living organism.

While gene-editing holds immense potential for treating genetic diseases, curing hereditary disorders, and even enhancing human abilities, the resulting implications go beyond the science itself. The tangle of moral, legal, social, and psychological questions becomes increasingly complex.

Firstly, the issue spans across the definitions of “human” and “person.” As gene-editing becomes more widespread, lines that distinguish us physically may blur or become arbitrary. Consequently, these questions challenge us to examine the essence of human identity and what it means to be a “person.”

Secondly, questions of fairness arise when considering the government’s or insurance companies’ potential requirement for genetic documentation. The ever-present possibility of genetic enhancement raises issues of social equality, healthcare access, and societal fairness in a world with such disparate capabilities.

Thirdly, gene-editing has the potential to assert eugenics and social engineering on an unprecedented scale. The implications may lead to a dystopian world, as seen in movies like “Gattaca,” where inferior beings are left behind while genetically superior beings flourish. Moreover, ethically charged goals like curing genetic diseases may be overshadowed by society’s focus on optimizing traits like intelligence or physical prowess.

Moreover, the psychological consequences are often overlooked. Performing gene-editing on embryos can lead to a child-specific design or even the devaluation of non-enhanced children. Our perception of parenthood and family could potentially shift significantly with such practices.

The growing presence of private companies offering genetic enhancement adds another layer of complexity. The iPrivacy, Genetics, and Human Identity project by the Center for Democracy & Technology emphasizes the importance of addressing privacy and informational risks linked with genetic data.

Lastly, the accessibility aspect plays a vital role in the discussion. The price of gene-editing continues to fall, yet, it remains inaccessible for many, contributing to growing wealth disparities.

It’s crucial to note that human genetic engineering involves substantial change, implying any comparison to current biotechnological interventions is inadequate. Moreover, Gene-editing technologies have the potential to address countless global health issues. However, without proper guidelines and legislation, the technology may lead to more significant, unintended problems.

With every advancement comes a responsibility to consider its implications thoroughly. The possibilities and potential of human gene-editing technologies are immense, and so too must be our efforts to grapple with the resulting implications.

So, while the idea of a genetically modified future may feel almost unfathomable, perhaps that’s the point. The conversation we need to have around human gene-editing needs to be at the level of our understanding and beyond. We must begin to consider the implications and understand that it is not only about the presence of the technology but about how we decide to utilize it.

Thus, with both excitement and trepidation, we move forward into a world of unprecedented genetic possibilities. How we navigate this new path will determine what kind of future awaits us.
