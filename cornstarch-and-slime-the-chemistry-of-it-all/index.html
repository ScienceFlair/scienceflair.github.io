Cornstarch and Slime: The Chemistry of it All


Ever wonder what makes slime so gooey and intriguing? Looking for a fun, science-filled activity for an afternoon spent at home? Let’s dive into the chemistry of using cornstarch to create that notorious and squishy slime we’ve all come to love!

What is Cornstarch?

Cornstarch is a fine, white powder made from the corn grain. This incredibly abundant and versatile ingredient is used in a myriad of products, from cooking and baking, to household cleaning supplies and personal care items. But beyond its culinary uses, cornstarch has the innate ability to create an unconventional material when combined with water—slime!

The Science Behind Cornstarch Slime

When we mix cornstarch with water, a fascinating chemical reaction occurs. Cornstarch molecules are actually made up of thousands of glucose units bonded together, forming a type of polysaccharide. These molecules have massive sizes and structures, making hydrophobic (water-repelling) parts that don’t easily dissolve in water.

When cornstarch is added to water, the starch molecules start to thicken the water and bind together. This process effectively thins out the surface tension of the water, making it easier for everything from fingers to beads and even paintbrushes to glide across its surface. This is what we see and feel as the gooey consistency of slime!

To achieve the proper slime texture, we’ll need to mix just the right proportions of cornstarch and water. Too much cornstarch and you may end up with a thick, hard-to-mix clump. Too little, and it won’t reach that ideal viscosity. But don’t worry, once you’ve nailed the perfect ratio, you’ll be rewarded with hours of squishy fun!

Making Cornstarch Slime

Before we jump into directions, let’s gather some items to make your very own slime:

  • 1 cup of cornstarch
  • ½ cup of water
  • (Optional) Food coloring or liquid watercolor paint
  • (Optional) Glitter or small beads for added texture
  • Bowl or container
  • Spoon or mixing utensil
  • Rubber gloves or plastic spatula

Now that you have everything you need, let’s combine our ingredients:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the cornstarch and any additional dry ingredients, such as food coloring or glitter. Be sure to combine them thoroughly.

  2. Slowly add water, stirring continuously with a spoon or your mixing utensil. As the cornstarch and water begin to combine, the texture will quickly change from dry, crumbly powder to a thick, gooey mixture.

  3. Depending on the texture you prefer, continue adding water a little at a time, stirring until you achieve your desired consistency. Note that once you’ve reached the perfect slime mixture, adding too much more water can thin it out, causing it to lose its adhesion.

  4. (Optional) If you find your slime to be too sticky, don the rubber gloves or grab a plastic spatula and knead the slime for a few minutes until it reaches a smoother, more pliable texture.

Wrap-Up: The Chemistry of Cornstarch Slime

The science behind cornstarch slime lies not just in the ingredients themselves, but in the marvelously messy experiment you get to conduct in your kitchen, making it a worthy lesson in chemistry and a fun bonding experience with friends and family. The next time you find yourself exploring the marvels of the world of chemistry or crafting a simple, sticky masterpiece, remember the simple magic of mixing cornstarch and water—the chemistry of it all!
