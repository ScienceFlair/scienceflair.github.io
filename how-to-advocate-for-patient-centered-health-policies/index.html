How to Advocate for Patient-Centered Health Policies


The way healthcare systems operate is constantly evolving, and for good reason. Patients have long been calling for meaningful change, from making primary care more accessible to improving end-of-life care for all individuals. However, positive regression typically trickles down through years of failed attempts and energetic employee advocacy efforts. Although not easy, working in partnerships, patients and healthcare professionals can thrive as change agents to strengthen the wellbeing of communities nationwide. Taking the Lead in Shaping Healthcare Policies As patients take charge of their own medical decisions, choosing optimal treatments and being vocal about reform, it's essential that they get the attention of top policymakers and actively engage with the health system in tangible ways. Federal, state, and local policymakers make crucial decisions that directly impact health outcomes and insurance coverage for medical care. When patients amplify their voices, fostering community-informed legislation can emerge and deliver higher quality, more sustainable care to an increasingly elderly population.
