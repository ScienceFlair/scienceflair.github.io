The Best Data Science Podcasts to Listen to in 2024


Podcasts have become essential for professionals and hobbyists alike to stay ahead in fast-evolving industries. For those in data science, there’s no better way to keep up with trends, techniques, and tools than by tuning into some carefully curated podcast channels. Whether you’re new to the field or a seasoned expert, podcasts offer insights into machine learning, artificial intelligence, analytics, and the people shaping the data revolution—all while fitting neatly into your commute, gym time, or even your coffee breaks. Below, we take a deep dive into some of the best data science podcasts to add to your playlist in 2024, covering both technical content and broader discussions around the field.

1. Data Skeptic

“Data Skeptic” continues its reign as one of the most beloved podcasts in the data science community. Hosted by Kyle Polich, this podcast strikes a balance between in-depth discussions on technical methodologies and accessible narratives for those who aren’t well-versed in data science. The episodes frequently dive into topics like machine learning, deep learning, and the role of data in everyday life. With a mix of interviews, thought pieces, and topic-specific series, it brings a wide range of issues to light, from algorithmic bias to the application of AI in art and culture. It’s a fantastic choice for practitioners looking to fine-tune their knowledge and casual listeners who simply want to grasp the basics of data science concepts.

2. The Data Engineering Podcast

Successful data science often starts with strong data engineering foundations, and this podcast explores the importance of building and maintaining robust data pipelines. Hosted by Tobias Macey, “The Data Engineering Podcast” digs into the challenges that come with data management, storage, and transformation. It features insightful interviews with software developers, data engineers, and industry leaders who discuss real-world implementations and solutions to thorny engineering problems. This podcast is perfect for listeners who are passionate about topics like distributed databases, ETL processes, or the nitty-gritty of big data systems like Apache Spark and Kafka.

3. SuperDataScience

Created by Kirill Eremenko, a renowned data scientist and instructor, “SuperDataScience” is an excellent podcast option for anyone looking to deepen their understanding of data science while staying current with industry trends. The show features interviews with top experts from various fields such as artificial intelligence, business intelligence, and deep learning. In addition to discussing cutting-edge tools and techniques, this podcast dives into the career paths of data professionals, offering actionable advice for those entering or advancing in the field. Its approachable style and helpful resources make it a must-listen for anyone looking to build or sharpen their data science toolkit in 2024.

4. Not So Standard Deviations

Hilary Parker and Roger D. Peng bring a refreshing flair to the data science podcast landscape with “Not So Standard Deviations.” This show is ideal for those who appreciate more conversational and relatable discussions about data science in practice. Hilary and Roger often highlight common pitfalls, share best practices, and discuss how data science is applied in real-world situations. While the podcast is often lighthearted, it doesn’t shy away from diving into nuanced concepts such as reproducible research, the philosophy of science, or ethical data practices. “Not So Standard Deviations” is particularly popular among statisticians and those who value the art behind the numbers.

5. Women in Data Science (WiDS) Podcast

The “Women in Data Science (WiDS) Podcast” is a vibrant platform that pushes forward inclusion and diversity in the domain of data analytics and AI. This podcast, led by Margot Gerritsen, offers thought-provoking conversations with data scientists, engineers, and other domain experts who share their journeys, challenges, and expertise. Not only does it celebrate the contributions of women, but it also offers listeners insights into fascinating topics like the intersection of climate science and data, neural network advancements, or how data can drive social good. WiDS is an inspiring listen for everyone in the field and serves as a great reminder of the power of representation in STEM.

6. Linear Digressions

If you’re the kind of learner who thrives on bite-sized episodes filled with complex, stimulating ideas, “Linear Digressions” is an outstanding choice. Even though the show concluded its production in 2021, its content remains highly relevant for data enthusiasts and practitioners in 2024. Hosted by Ben Jaffe and Katie Malone, the podcast excels at breaking down advanced machine learning techniques, such as NLP models and reinforcement learning, into digestible and relatable segments. Whether you’re working on data-driven projects or just geeking out over the latest AI applications, the archives of Linear Digressions remain a treasure trove.

7. Data Stories

For a podcast that bridges data science and storytelling, look no further than “Data Stories.” Hosted by Enrico Bertini and Moritz Stefaner, this podcast focuses on data visualization and the importance of making data comprehensible for broad audiences. The hosts engage with experts across various industries to discuss how complex datasets can be presented visually and impactfully. This podcast is particularly valuable for listeners interested in visual storytelling, user interface design, and methods to communicate data in compelling, human-centered ways. Whether you’re an aspiring data artist or a visual analytics professional, “Data Stories” delivers fascinating insights that inspire creativity.

8. The AI Alignment Podcast

This podcast may focus primarily on artificial intelligence, but its relevance in the data science community is undeniable, particularly when addressing the ethics and societal implications of machine learning. “The AI Alignment Podcast,” hosted by the Future of Life Institute, invites thought leaders to explore the challenges of maintaining human-alignment in advanced AI systems. Topics often include the philosophy behind decision-making models, potential risks in autonomous systems, and strategies for creating an equitable relationship between humans and machines. If you’re passionate about the long-term future of AI-powered data solutions, this podcast is an eye-opening resource.

9. Chai Time Data Science

Hosted by Sanyam Bhutani, “Chai Time Data Science” is a highly engaging podcast that covers a wide range of subdomains within data science, from deep learning to Kaggle competitions. What makes this podcast unique is its friendly and informal approach, reminiscent of conversations shared over a cup of chai. Sanyam interviews a diverse group of guests—researchers, industry veterans, and community contributors alike—providing listeners with practical insights, personal experiences, and career advice. True to its name, the show is warm, inviting, and a treat for anyone looking to explore the human side of data in technological contexts.

10. Data Science at Home

“Data Science at Home,” run by Francesco Gadaleta, is a podcast geared toward data scientists and engineers keen on subjects like scaling AI solutions, edge computing, and the latest in neural network architectures. The episodes are concise yet dense with technical expertise, making this an excellent resource for practitioners focused on production-grade AI systems. The show often explores topics like federated learning, data privacy in machine learning, and distributed training frameworks. If you’re a listener who appreciates a focus on hands-on technologies and implementation strategies, this podcast offers an abundance of value.

Honorable Mentions

The Gradient Podcast

This series delves into the frontier of machine learning research, inviting authors of recent studies and thought leaders to discuss cutting-edge advancements.

Practical AI

For listeners aiming to enhance their AI development skills, “Practical AI” focuses on the tangible applications of machine learning and artificial intelligence tools.

Wrapping Up

With so much innovation happening in data science, staying informed has never been more important or challenging. These podcasts provide a wealth of knowledge and insights from leading experts, catering to audiences of varying skill levels and interests. By incorporating a mix of technical deep dives, industry updates, and human stories, these podcasts ensure you’ll stay connected to the ever-changing world of data science. Tuning into any of these channels is like having a front-row seat as the data-driven evolution unfolds in 2024 and beyond.
