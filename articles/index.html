Understanding how the body maintains this balance during exercise entails a deep dive into both biochemistry and physiology. The Chemistry of Acid-Base Balance At the heart of acid-base balance lies the concept of pH, a measure of how acidic or basic a solution is. The scale ranges from 0 (very acidic) to 14 (very basic), with 7 being neutral. Healthy human blood typically maintains a pH between 7.35 and 7.45. Deviations from this range can impact bodily functions, leading to fatigue, impaired recovery, and potentially serious health conditions. Acids are substances that donate hydrogen ions (H+) in a solution, while bases accept these ions. Our bodies produce various acids during metabolic processes, particularly lactic acid during high-intensity exercise. The accumulation of lactic acid can lead to increased H+ concentration, lowering the pH and creating a more acidic internal environment.
