These massive storms are characterized by strong winds, heavy rainfall, and a distinct circular motion. Understanding the anatomy of a tropical cyclone can help us comprehend the science behind these natural disasters and better prepare for their impact. Formation and Development Hurricanes begin as tropical disturbances, which are clusters of thunderstorms that form over the ocean. When favorable conditions are present, such as warm sea surface temperatures and low wind shear, the disturbance can intensify into a tropical depression. As the depression strengthens, with sustained winds reaching 39 miles per hour (63 kilometers per hour) or more, it evolves into a tropical storm. Finally, if the storm’s sustained winds exceed 74 miles per hour (119 kilometers per hour), it is classified as a hurricane. ...
