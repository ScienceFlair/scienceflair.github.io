DNA methylation is a vital process that plays a crucial role in regulating gene expression and determining cell fate in molecular biology. It involves the addition of a methyl group to the DNA molecule, specifically at cytosine residues in a CpG dinucleotide context. This modification is carried out by DNA methyltransferases (DNMTs) and is a fundamental mechanism for gene silencing, genomic imprinting, and X-chromosome inactivation. The Importance of DNA Methylation DNA methylation is a dynamic process that has significant implications for cellular development and function. It acts as a heritable epigenetic mark that can be stably transmitted through cell divisions, allowing for the maintenance of gene expression patterns in different cell types.
