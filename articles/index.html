As global temperatures rise due to human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation, the effects of Arctic amplification are becoming increasingly apparent, with far-reaching implications for the Arctic and beyond. Arctic amplification is driven by several feedback mechanisms that magnify the warming effect in the polar region. One of the primary factors is the albedo feedback loop, where melting ice and snow expose darker surfaces that absorb more sunlight, leading to further warming and ice melt.
