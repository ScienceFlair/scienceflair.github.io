Diaphragm: A large, dome-shaped muscle separating the chest cavity from the abdominal cavity, essential for breathing. The primary function of the respiratory system is to facilitate the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide between the air and the bloodstream. Oxygen from inhaled air diffuses into the blood, while carbon dioxide, a waste product of cellular respiration, diffuses out of the blood and into the lungs.
