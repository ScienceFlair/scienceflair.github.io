Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) Quantum Chromodynamics, a fundamental theory in the realm of particle physics, describes the strong force that holds quarks together to form protons, neutrons, and other hadrons. The theory is based on the concept of “color charge,” which is analogous to the electric charge in the theory of Quantum Electrodynamics (QED). In QCD, quarks carry one of three possible “colors” (red, green, or blue), while antiquarks carry the corresponding anticolors (antired, antigreen, or antiblue).
