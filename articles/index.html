Huntington’s disease is caused by a mutation in the huntingtin gene (HTT), located on chromosome 4. This mutation involves a repeated sequence of DNA known as a CAG trinucleotide repeat. In individuals with Huntington’s disease, this repeat is expanded, leading to the production of a mutant huntingtin protein.
