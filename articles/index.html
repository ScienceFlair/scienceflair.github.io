Consisting of a circular disk painted white and black, the Secchi disk is lowered into the water until it disappears from view. While agriculture is essential for sustaining human life by providing food and resources, its impact on freshwater ecosystems cannot be overlooked. In this blog post, we will explore the multifaceted effects of agriculture on limnology and freshwater ecosystems, shedding light on both the positive and negative aspects of this relationship. One of the most significant impacts of agriculture on freshwater ecosystems is nutrient loading. The excessive use of fertilizers in agricultural practices leads to the runoff of nitrogen and phosphorus into nearby water bodies. These nutrients, while beneficial for crop growth, can have detrimental effects on freshwater ecosystems. Excessive nutrient input can cause eutrophication, a process in which excessive plant and algal growth depletes oxygen levels in the water, leading to the death of aquatic organisms. Eutrophication poses a significant threat to the balance and health of freshwater ecosystems, disrupting the natural food web and biodiversity. ...
