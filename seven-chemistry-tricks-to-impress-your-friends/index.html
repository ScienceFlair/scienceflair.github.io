Seven Chemistry Tricks to Impress Your Friends


Chemistry is a fascinating subject that often gets a bad reputation for being boring or complicated. However, with a little creativity and curiosity, chemistry can reveal itself to be a truly awe-inspiring and entertaining subject. In this blog post, we will explore seven chemistry tricks that you can effortlessly perform to astound your friends and audiences while highlighting the beauty of science.

  1. Sweet and Visual Chemical Reactions: Citric Acid and Baking Soda One of the most visual and engaging chemistry tricks is using citric acid and baking soda to create an explosive reaction. Start by adding a few tablespoons of citric acid to a glass of water, stirring until it is completely dissolved. Then, get a small cup of baking soda and carefully mix it with some dish soap. Show your audience the individual ingredients, and then, slowly pour the baking soda mixture into the glass of citric acid water. Marvel at the fizzy bubblestorm that forms, and enjoy the moreish lemony smell that fills the room.

  2. The Magic of Magnetism: Chromatography on a Paperclip Chromatography is a method used to separate and identify components of a mixture. However, with a playful twist, you can demonstrate chromatography using nothing more than a paperclip and a colored ink pen. Dip the paperclip in a pen and let it dry. Afterward, draw a circle on a strip of paper (could be a simple sticky note) using the same ink, and fold the strip so that the inked part touches the magnetized paperclip. Gently pour a small amount of water over the folded paper and watch the ink separate as it travels down the paper with the help of the magnetized iron in the paperclip.

  3. Melting Ice Instantly: The Power of Leidenfrost Effect The Leidenfrost effect occurs when a liquid comes into contact with a significantly hotter surface, turning it into a hot vapor layer that allows the liquid to float and seemingly defy gravity. To impress your friends with this amazing effect, simply dip a spoon in water and quickly slide it over a flame on a stove. Be careful not to burn yourself, and enjoy the mesmerizing sight of the water on the spoon instantly vaporizing and creating a freezing sensation.

  4. Batteries From Oranges: The Magic of Electrochemistry Electrochemistry is a branch of chemistry that deals with the relationship between electricity and chemical reactions. You can demonstrate this exciting science using two oranges, copper pennies, and an LED light. First, slice the oranges in half and insert a copper penny in each. Connect the two oranges to a small LED light using wire and battery holders (don’t mix the terminals). While the different chemical components in the orange create electricity, the LED light will light up, showcasing the power of electrochemistry in a fun and interactive way.

  5. Color-Change Phenomena: pH Testing with Flowers Another great way to demonstrate chemistry through color-change phenomena is by testing various flowers and plants’ pH levels. Start by picking a few flowers with deep-colored petals, like roses. Use a dropper to extract some of the flower’s water and check its pH level with pH test strips. Most flowers will have a pH level around 4-5, making them acidic. You can then ask your friends to predict what would happen if you add a small amount of baking soda to the floral water, and watch their faces light up in surprise when they see the water’s pH change.

  6. Making Milk Curdle: The Science of Hydrochloric Acid To create an opaque, lumpy, and slightly scary milk mixture, all you need is some skim milk and hydrochloric acid. In a cup of milk, add a few drops of food coloring for extra visual effect, and then slowly add a dropper full of hydrochloric acid. Watch the milk curdle and turn into a gooey mess. Afterward, bring out a teaspoon of baking soda, which neutralizes the acid and makes the curdled milk reappear as liquid. Teach your friends about the science behind this trick (proteins in milk reacting with hydrochloric acid) in an engaging manner.

  7. Gummy Bear Osmosis Experiment: The Power of Concentration Osmosis is the movement of solvent molecules through a selectively permeable membrane from a less concentrated solution to a more concentrated one to equalize the solute concentrations on the two sides of the membrane. To demonstrate this interesting concept, have your friends select a gummy bear. Then, fill a container with water and a number of gummy bears equal to the number of friends participating. Add as much sugar as possible to the water to create a concentrated solution, and then leave a gummy bear in each for about an hour. Afterward, show the initial gummy bear and the one soaked in sugar water. Watch in amazement as the soaked gummy bear grows in size, displaying the power of osmosis!

Conclusion Falling in love with chemistry has never been so fun, and we hope you enjoyed exploring these seven chemistry tricks as much as our team did. Share your newfound knowledge and excitement with friends, and watch as they, too, are intrigued by the depth and beauty of chemistry. There’s never a dull moment when it comes to combining everyday materials, a dash of creativity, and a drop of scientific curiosity.

Happy experimenting and educating!
