Innovative Approaches to In Vivo Dosimetry in Radiotherapy


In the realm of radiotherapy, the precise delivery of radiation doses to cancerous tissues while sparing healthy surrounding tissues is crucial for successful treatment outcomes. In Vivo Dosimetry plays a pivotal role in ensuring the accuracy and safety of radiation therapy by measuring the actual dose received by the patient during treatment. Traditional methods of dosimetry involve calculations based on treatment plans, but the real-time monitoring provided by In Vivo Dosimetry offers a more comprehensive understanding of the delivered dose and enables adjustments to be made promptly if needed. Let’s delve into some innovative approaches to In Vivo Dosimetry that are shaping the landscape of radiotherapy.

1. Electronic Portal Imaging Device (EPID)

EPIDs are flat-panel detectors mounted on the linear accelerator machine that capture images of the treatment field before or during radiation delivery. These images can be used to verify the position of the patient and the treatment field, as well as to reconstruct the delivered dose. EPIDs provide a real-time assessment of the dose distribution and can detect errors such as patient positioning discrepancies or machine malfunctions.

2. Optically Stimulated Luminescence Dosimeters (OSLDs)

OSLDs utilize aluminum oxide crystals that store radiation energy when exposed to radiation. After irradiation, the OSLDs can be read using a special reader to determine the absorbed dose. OSLDs are small, reusable, and tissue-equivalent dosimeters that offer high accuracy and sensitivity in measuring radiation doses. They are commonly used for patient-specific dosimetry verification.

3. Semiconductor Diode Detectors

Semiconductor diode detectors are small electronic devices that convert radiation energy into an electrical signal. These detectors are placed on the patient’s skin or within body cavities to measure the dose received during treatment. Semiconductor diodes provide fast response times and high spatial resolution, making them ideal for verifying dose delivery in critical areas.

4. Radiochromic Film

Radiochromic film is a specialized film that changes color in response to radiation exposure. The degree of color change is correlated with the absorbed dose, allowing for dose mapping in two or three dimensions. Radiochromic film offers high spatial resolution and can be used for complex dose verification in various treatment techniques, including intensity-modulated radiation therapy (IMRT) and stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT).

5. Wireless Electronic Dosimeters

Wireless electronic dosimeters are small, wearable devices that continuously monitor the radiation dose received by the patient in real-time. These dosimeters transmit data wirelessly to a central monitoring system, allowing for immediate dose tracking and analysis. Wireless electronic dosimeters enhance patient safety by alerting healthcare providers to any deviations from the prescribed dose.

In conclusion, the field of In Vivo Dosimetry in radiotherapy continues to evolve with innovative technologies that improve the accuracy, efficiency, and safety of radiation treatments. By incorporating advanced dosimetry approaches such as EPIDs, OSLDs, semiconductor diode detectors, radiochromic film, and wireless electronic dosimeters, healthcare providers can optimize treatment delivery and ensure optimal outcomes for cancer patients. Embracing these cutting-edge dosimetry tools paves the way for enhanced quality assurance and personalized care in the field of radiation oncology.
