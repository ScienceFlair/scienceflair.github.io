The Best Herpetology Conferences and Events to Attend


For herpetologists, both seasoned researchers and aspiring students, conferences, symposiums, and workshops offer a unique opportunity to exchange knowledge, form collaborations, and explore the latest advancements in the study of reptiles and amphibians. These events are cornerstones of professional development, bringing together biologists, ecologists, conservationists, and educators from diverse backgrounds to share expertise and innovations.

In this guide, we’ll dive into some of the most impactful herpetology conferences and events worldwide, detailing what makes them special, who they cater to, and why you should consider attending.

The Society for the Study of Amphibians and Reptiles (SSAR) Annual Meeting

The Society for the Study of Amphibians and Reptiles (SSAR) is one of the leading organizations dedicated to herpetology, and their annual meeting is highly anticipated by professionals and enthusiasts alike. The SSAR meeting provides a dynamic platform for sharing cutting-edge research, forging collaborations, and networking with like-minded individuals.

Highlights:

  • Diverse Topics: The conference covers a wide array of topics, from ecological studies and conservation strategies to physiology, phylogenetics, and behavior.
  • Networking Opportunities: With attendees ranging from students to senior researchers, the SSAR meeting fosters connections across academic and professional levels.
  • Workshops and Symposia: These events often include specialized workshops that allow attendees to focus on niche fields within herpetology, such as population modeling or environmental DNA (eDNA) techniques.

This annual event is deeply rooted in academic rigor while also being an inclusive space for growing professionals, making it a must for anyone serious about reptiles and amphibians.

Joint Meeting of Ichthyologists and Herpetologists (JMIH)

One of the largest gatherings for professionals in herpetology and ichthyology, the Joint Meeting of Ichthyologists and Herpetologists (JMIH) is a premier event for scholars, practitioners, and educators in the field. Organized collaboratively by SSAR, the Herpetologists’ League, and the American Society of Ichthyologists and Herpetologists, JMIH is a comprehensive event that attracts participants from across the globe.

Highlights:

  • Interdisciplinary Approach: By combining ichthyology and herpetology, the conference fosters innovative discussions and cross-discipline partnerships.
  • Student-Focused Sessions: Dedicated opportunities for students to present their work and receive feedback from experts in the field.
  • Outstanding Presenters: World-class speakers and panelists share their insights on major challenges and discoveries in herpetology.

The JMIH is a vibrant event that bridges multiple specialties, making it ideal for those whose interests span the broader biological sciences.

European Herpetological Society (SEH) Congress

For those based in or interested in European herpetology, the biennial congress of the European Herpetological Society (SEH) is a key event. The SEH provides a critical platform for herpetologists across Europe to share research specific to reptile and amphibian species in the region, while also addressing global conservation issues.

Highlights:

  • Regional Focus: Discussions include topics relevant to European ecosystems, populations, and species.
  • Conservation-Driven: Special attention is given to current threats, such as climate change, habitat destruction, and invasive species.
  • Collaborative Environment: Networking opportunities with leading European researchers and conservationists.

This congress is perfect for attendees who are passionate about herpetology as it pertains to Europe, but it’s equally valuable for global researchers seeking insights into biodiversity and conservation.

World Congress of Herpetology (WCH)

The World Congress of Herpetology (WCH) is unmatched in scale and scope, offering a global perspective on herpetological research. Held every four years, this event attracts thousands of participants, from leading herpetologists to students embarking on their careers.

Highlights:

  • Global Participation: Attended by experts, researchers, and organizations from all corners of the globe.
  • Comprehensive Programming: Sessions cover everything from taxonomy and molecular biology to applied conservation, public policy, and education.
  • Field Excursions: Many WCH events include optional field trips, allowing participants to explore local habitats and conduct on-site research.

The WCH is not just an academic conference—it is a celebration of herpetology worldwide, providing unparalleled opportunities to engage with the global community.

Amphibian Conservation Research Symposium (ACRS)

The Amphibian Conservation Research Symposium (ACRS) is dedicated solely to issues affecting amphibians, making it an indispensable event for those focused on the world’s frogs, salamanders, and caecilians. Hosted across different locations each year, ACRS brings together researchers and conservation practitioners to address amphibian-specific challenges.

Highlights:

  • Amphibian Expertise: A specialized event for those deeply invested in amphibian biology and preservation.
  • Collaborative Atmosphere: Creates connections between researchers, conservation organizations, and policymakers.
  • Action-Oriented: Emphasis on actionable solutions to reverse amphibian declines.

The ACRS is ideal for professionals who want to dive deep into amphibian conservation while interacting with a community passionate about protecting these vital species.

Regional Herpetological Associations and Events

In addition to international conferences, herpetologists can benefit immensely from regional meetings and events that cater to specific local issues and species. Some examples include:

  • Australian Society of Herpetologists (ASH) Annual Meeting: Focused on unique challenges and species found in Australia’s ecosystems.
  • African Amphibian Working Group (AAWG): A forum for advancing knowledge and conservation efforts across Africa.
  • Asian Herpetological Conferences: Bringing together researchers who specialize in the extraordinary biodiversity of Asia.

These smaller-scale, regionally focused events are vital for creating localized networks and exploring research that directly affects the surrounding environment.

Why You Should Attend Herpetology Conferences

Whether you are an academic researcher, a conservation practitioner, or simply a passionate individual intrigued by reptiles and amphibians, attending herpetology conferences is an invaluable experience. Here are some of the key benefits:

  1. Networking: Conferences connect you with mentors, peers, and collaborators who can help propel your career or project forward.
  2. Professional Development: Access to cutting-edge research, methodologies, and tools can deepen your expertise and enhance your work.
  3. Sharing Your Work: Presenting your findings can increase your profile within the community and provide constructive feedback.
  4. Inspiration: Engaging with new ideas and perspectives often fuels motivation and creativity.

Given the wide range of conferences and events available, there’s something for every herpetologist, regardless of their level of expertise or area of interest.

Tips for Maximizing Your Conference Experience

  • Plan Ahead: Review the program and decide which sessions and workshops align with your goals.
  • Engage Actively: Ask questions during sessions, discuss ideas with fellow attendees, and participate in social events.
  • Take Notes: Keeping a detailed record of key insights and contacts ensures that valuable information doesn’t get lost.
  • Follow Up: After the conference, reach out to individuals you connected with to maintain relationships and explore potential collaborations.

By preparing thoughtfully and participating fully, you can transform any herpetology conference into an unforgettable and highly productive experience.

Herpetology conferences are more than just academic gatherings; they are vibrant hubs where the past, present, and future of reptile and amphibian science converge. Whether you’re focused on advancing conservation efforts, uncovering evolutionary mysteries, or developing educational programs, these events offer the resources, connections, and inspiration to achieve your goals. Dive into the world of herpetology events, and you’ll find opportunities to deepen your knowledge, expand your network, and make a meaningful impact in the field.
