Nuclear Engineering Conferences and Workshops to Attend in 2024


Nuclear engineering continues to be a dynamic and rapidly evolving field that blends advanced physics, cutting-edge technology, and global policy considerations. As the industry navigates pivotal challenges such as climate change mitigation, nuclear safety, and advanced reactor development, staying updated through professional gatherings has never been more critical for engineers, researchers, and professionals in related domains. In 2024, a rich calendar of conferences and workshops offers unmatched opportunities to network, learn, and contribute to the future of nuclear technology.

This comprehensive guide explores the most notable nuclear engineering conferences and workshops taking place around the world in 2024, highlighting their key themes, target audiences, and what attendees can expect.

Why Attend Nuclear Engineering Conferences & Workshops?

Conferences and workshops serve as a vital nexus for exchanging ideas, presenting research findings, and fostering collaborations. They provide a platform for:

  • Showcasing Innovation: Discover the latest research outcomes, technological advancements, and novel reactor designs.
  • Professional Development: Engage in training sessions and workshops to upgrade technical skills and understand regulatory frameworks.
  • Networking: Connect with peers, industry leaders, government representatives, and academia to build strategic partnerships.
  • Policy and Safety Discussions: Participate in dialogues on nuclear safety, security, waste management, and non-proliferation measures.

With that backdrop, let’s dive into the key nuclear engineering events scheduled for 2024.

1. International Topical Meeting on Nuclear Reactor Thermal-Hydraulics (NURETH 2024)

When: August 18–22, 2024
Where: Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA
Focus Areas: Thermal hydraulics, multiphase flow, reactor safety, advanced reactor technologies

NURETH stands out as the premier event focusing on the thermal-hydraulic behavior of nuclear reactors. It draws researchers and engineers who specialize in fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and safety simulation in reactor systems. The program includes technical sessions featuring cutting-edge computational models, experimental research, and new methodologies to address reactor cooling challenges.

Attendees can expect thematic workshops on passive safety systems and small modular reactors (SMRs), making it an ideal venue for those involved in the design and safety assessment of next-generation reactors.

2. International Conference on Nuclear Engineering (ICONE 32)

When: July 7–11, 2024
Where: Shanghai, China
Focus Areas: Reactor physics, fuel cycle, radiation protection, and material science

ICONE is a flagship global conference organized by the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) and brings together a broad spectrum of nuclear engineering topics. This event is particularly beneficial for professionals engaged in reactor design, materials development, and nuclear fuel cycle optimization.

Highlights in 2024 include keynote sessions by pioneers in nuclear fusion and presentations on advanced cladding materials that improve safety margins. The conference also hosts a dedicated career development workshop tailored for early-career engineers entering the nuclear sector.

3. European Nuclear Conference (ENC 2024)

When: May 6–9, 2024
Where: Madrid, Spain
Focus Areas: Policy, safety, decommissioning, radioactive waste management

As one of Europe’s most comprehensive nuclear events, ENC provides a platform to discuss both technical and socioeconomic aspects of nuclear energy. The conference encompasses sessions on nuclear regulatory frameworks, decommissioning strategies for aging reactors, and the circular economy approaches for radioactive waste.

ENC 2024 will feature a special panel on the role of nuclear power in Europe’s path to carbon neutrality, highlighting evolving regulatory trends and public engagement strategies.

4. International Conference on Advances in Nuclear Instrumentation, Measurement Methods and their Applications (ANIMMA 2024)

When: June 2–6, 2024
Where: Marseille, France
Focus Areas: Instrumentation, nuclear measurement, monitoring technologies, and automation

ANIMMA focuses on innovations in nuclear instrumentation, which lies at the heart of reactor monitoring and safety assurance. The conference showcases advances in sensor technologies, data acquisition, and automation tools used in nuclear plants and research facilities.

In 2024, the workshop sessions will emphasize artificial intelligence and machine learning applications for anomaly detection and predictive maintenance in nuclear systems. Professionals involved in control systems and instrumentation will find this event particularly valuable.

5. American Nuclear Society (ANS) Winter Meeting & Nuclear Technology Expo

When: November 11–14, 2024
Where: Orlando, Florida, USA
Focus Areas: Nuclear science, power generation, fusion, nuclear policy

This annual gathering by the ANS is one of the largest and most diverse nuclear engineering events in North America. The Winter Meeting encompasses paper presentations, panel discussions, and an expansive expo floor showcasing the latest in nuclear technology.

2024’s program includes in-depth sessions on fusion reactor developments, nuclear cyber security, and public communication strategies to enhance acceptance of nuclear power. The associated workshops on nuclear project management and licensing also provide practical knowledge for industry professionals.

6. International Symposium on Nuclear Fuel Cycle and Reactor Technology (NFCRT 2024)

When: October 15–18, 2024
Where: Tokyo, Japan
Focus Areas: Fuel cycle innovation, reprocessing, waste minimization, reactor optimization

NFCRT zeros in on the integral relationship between nuclear reactors and their fuel cycles. With a growing need for sustainability, this symposium presents research on advanced fuel designs, closed fuel cycles, and waste reduction techniques.

A unique feature of NFCRT 2024 is its collaborative sessions with nuclear fuel manufacturers and utility operators to discuss real-world challenges and deployment strategies.

7. International Conference on Radiation Shielding (ICRS 2024)

When: September 9–13, 2024
Where: Cape Town, South Africa
Focus Areas: Radiation shielding materials, radiation transport, shielding design

ICRS offers a focused forum on radiation shielding technologies critical to protecting people and the environment from ionizing radiation. Experts discuss advances in novel shielding materials, computational methods for radiation transport, and safety optimization strategies.

Given the growing emphasis on mobile nuclear systems and space applications, the 2024 program includes sessions on lightweight shielding solutions for aerospace technologies.

8. Workshop on Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) and Advanced Reactors

When: April 22–24, 2024
Where: Toronto, Canada
Focus Areas: SMR design, licensing, market strategies, and deployment pathways

As small modular reactors gain momentum due to their scalability and potential for enhanced safety, workshops dedicated to SMRs provide a deep dive into technical, regulatory, and economic considerations. This focused three-day workshop brings together industry stakeholders, regulators, and researchers to discuss reactor designs ranging from light water SMRs to molten salt and fast reactors.

This event is ideal for developers and government agencies planning the commercialization and deployment of these advanced systems.

Tips for Maximizing Conference and Workshop Attendance

Attending a conference can be overwhelming without proper planning. Here are some practical tips to help you get the most out of your participation:

  • Preparation: Review the program beforehand and select sessions aligned with your interests. Plan to attend keynote talks and panel discussions to gain the broadest insights.
  • Networking: Bring plenty of business cards or digital contact info. Engage actively with speakers during Q&A sessions or after presentations.
  • Workshops: Make the most of hands-on workshops and training sessions to build practical skills.
  • Follow-up: Reach out to new contacts and collaborators promptly after the event to keep conversations alive.
  • Documentation: Take notes and gather conference materials to revisit and share knowledge with your team.

The Future of Nuclear Engineering Conferences

Looking ahead, the nuclear engineering community is increasingly integrating virtual and hybrid formats to broaden accessibility and sustainability. Events in 2024 will continue this trend, allowing global participation from remote regions. These inclusive formats enhance cross-border collaboration and knowledge sharing, especially important in a field as globally significant as nuclear energy.

Moreover, as the space nuclear industry and fusion energy research grow, expect specialized conferences focusing on these frontiers to emerge, complementing the established gatherings covered here.

Conclusion

The year 2024 offers a rich slate of nuclear engineering conferences and workshops, vital for anyone involved in this multifaceted discipline. Whether your focus is reactor design, thermal hydraulics, fuel cycles, instrumentation, policy, or emerging technologies such as SMRs and fusion, there is an event tailored to your expertise and interests.

Engaging in these professional gatherings connects you to the latest developments and thought leaders while strengthening the foundations necessary for innovation and safety in nuclear energy. Mark your calendar, prepare strategically, and take full advantage of these opportunities to propel your career and contribute to the advancement of nuclear engineering.
