How did our solar system come to be, and what role did our star, the Sun, play in its formation?

Black holes, the most mysterious and enigmatic inhabitants of the cosmic neighborhood, how did they come to be?

Not merely a science fiction myth but very real existents, black holes are the final frontier of space exploration. Possessing such incredible gravitational force that not even light can escape their pull, black holes represent the pinnacle of cosmic accumulation and entropy.

Although not yet fully understood, the formation and evolution of black holes are believed to follow the birth-death-rebirth process of stars. When massive stars exhaust their nuclear fuel, they collapse under gravity’s power, forming either neutron stars or black holes. In the case of black holes, the process takes an extraordinary turn.

Through the relentless force of gravity, these celestial bodies briefly condense into Singularity, with infinite density and zero volume - essentially the most compact object imaginable. This Singularity, over cosmic timescales, gives rise to the supermassive black hole found at the center of galaxies, including our own.
