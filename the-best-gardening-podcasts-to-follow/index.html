The Best Gardening Podcasts to Follow


Gardening is more than just a hobby; it’s a passion that connects us to nature, provides relaxation, and offers the joy of watching plants grow and thrive. Whether you’re a seasoned gardener or a newcomer to the world of plants, podcasts have become one of the most enjoyable ways to gain gardening insights. They offer expert advice, helpful tips, plant care strategies, and even community support to help gardeners grow their skills. With a variety of formats, from interviews with horticulturists to in-depth discussions on specific plants, gardening podcasts can help you cultivate your gardening knowledge.

In this post, we’ll explore the best gardening podcasts to follow. Each of these offers unique content that will enrich your gardening journey, making it even more rewarding. Let’s dive in!

1. The Gardener’s Workshop Podcast

If you’re looking for practical gardening advice and tips that are rooted in real-world experiences, The Gardener’s Workshop Podcast is an excellent choice. Hosted by Lisa Mason Ziegler, a well-known flower farmer and gardening expert, this podcast focuses on the essentials of growing flowers and vegetables, organic gardening, and small-scale farming.

Lisa brings in a wide range of guests from all areas of gardening, including flower farming, permaculture, and seed saving, making it a must-listen for anyone passionate about sustainable gardening practices. Whether you’re interested in starting a flower farm or growing your own food at home, this podcast covers a wide range of topics to help you succeed in your gardening journey.

Why listen:

  • Expert advice from experienced professionals in the industry
  • Practical tips for beginners and advanced gardeners alike
  • Emphasis on organic and sustainable gardening practices

2. The Modern Gardener

If you’re seeking a podcast that merges modern gardening techniques with eco-friendly solutions, The Modern Gardener podcast might just be what you’re looking for. Hosted by gardening experts Emily and Matt, this show takes a fresh approach to gardening, highlighting the intersection between contemporary gardening trends and environmental sustainability.

Listeners will learn about new gardening tools, eco-conscious garden designs, and how to grow plants in urban settings. From vertical gardens to container planting, this podcast helps urban gardeners maximize space and resources while still cultivating lush, beautiful gardens.

Why listen:

  • A modern take on traditional gardening techniques
  • Emphasis on eco-friendly gardening
  • Great for urban gardeners and those with limited space

3. The Plant Based Podcast

The Plant Based Podcast is perfect for those who want to deepen their knowledge about plant care while fostering a deeper connection with nature. Hosted by Michael and Ellen, two passionate horticulturists, this podcast covers everything from houseplants to rare garden species, with a particular focus on plant health, propagation, and care.

Listeners can expect in-depth conversations with experts, lively discussions about the latest trends in gardening, and practical advice on nurturing plants at all stages of their life. Whether you’re just getting started with houseplants or are a seasoned gardener looking to expand your knowledge, The Plant Based Podcast will provide valuable insights.

Why listen:

  • In-depth discussions with experts on plant care
  • Focus on plant propagation and health
  • Great for both houseplant enthusiasts and outdoor gardeners

4. Epic Gardening

Epic Gardening, hosted by Kevin Espiritu, has earned a reputation for being one of the most accessible and helpful gardening podcasts available. Kevin’s podcast is known for its simplicity and educational value, making gardening seem less intimidating, especially for those just starting out.

From container gardening to growing herbs and vegetables, Epic Gardening offers easy-to-follow tips, tricks, and tutorials that will help listeners achieve success in their gardening endeavors. With hundreds of episodes covering topics like soil health, pest control, and growing food at home, this podcast is a goldmine for aspiring gardeners.

Why listen:

  • Perfect for beginners and intermediate gardeners
  • Practical and straightforward advice
  • Focus on growing food at home and creating sustainable gardens

5. The Dirt on Plants

If you’re someone who enjoys diving deep into plant science, The Dirt on Plants podcast is for you. Hosted by Dr. Sarah Gardner and Dr. Jessica McCumber, two horticultural experts, this podcast provides detailed information on the biology of plants and the science behind plant growth.

Listeners will learn about plant anatomy, root systems, soil health, and the latest scientific research on plant care. While it’s perfect for those who enjoy the technical side of gardening, it’s also an excellent choice for anyone interested in learning more about how plants function and thrive in different environments.

Why listen:

  • Ideal for those interested in plant science and biology
  • Offers a deeper understanding of how plants grow and thrive
  • Perfect for gardeners who want to understand the “why” behind plant care

6. Grow, Cook, Eat, Repeat

This podcast, hosted by Jennifer Ebeling, focuses on growing your own food and using it to create delicious meals. Grow, Cook, Eat, Repeat takes listeners through every step of the process, from planting your garden to harvesting your crops, and then turning those ingredients into mouth-watering dishes.

Whether you’re interested in growing fruits, vegetables, or herbs, this podcast offers practical advice and tasty recipes that will help you make the most of your home-grown produce. It’s an excellent choice for gardeners who want to experience the full cycle of growing and cooking with homegrown ingredients.

Why listen:

  • Combines gardening with cooking, making it both informative and fun
  • Offers advice on growing a variety of edible plants
  • Provides recipes to help you use your home-grown produce

7. Gardeners’ World Podcast

For gardeners who enjoy hearing from the UK’s most well-known horticulturalists, the Gardeners’ World Podcast is a must-listen. Hosted by BBC’s Monty Don and other guest gardeners, this podcast brings a wealth of gardening expertise straight to your ears.

Each episode features tips on gardening techniques, plant recommendations, and seasonal advice. It’s packed with inspiration for gardeners looking to transform their outdoor spaces, whether you’re creating a flower garden, a vegetable plot, or a serene outdoor sanctuary. The podcast often ties in with the popular TV show Gardeners’ World, so it’s perfect for those who already enjoy watching the show.

Why listen:

  • Hosted by well-known UK gardeners and horticultural experts
  • Features a mix of practical tips and inspirational stories
  • Great for gardeners looking to expand their skills and knowledge

8. The Sill’s Plant Parent Podcast

For those who want to become true “plant parents,” The Sill’s Plant Parent Podcast is a fantastic option. Hosted by Christopher Satch, this show offers a deep dive into the world of houseplants and offers expert guidance on how to care for indoor plants.

Each episode features interviews with plant experts, as well as practical tips on everything from watering techniques to choosing the right plants for your home environment. Whether you’re dealing with a thriving plant collection or trying to save a struggling plant, this podcast has something for everyone.

Why listen:

  • Focuses on indoor plant care and houseplants
  • Offers advice for both beginners and experienced plant owners
  • Features expert interviews and plant care tips

9. The Organic Gardener Podcast

For those who want to garden in harmony with the environment, The Organic Gardener Podcast is a valuable resource. Hosted by Jack Spirko, this podcast explores organic gardening techniques that are both sustainable and effective. Jack focuses on topics like permaculture, soil health, composting, and how to grow food without relying on chemical pesticides or fertilizers.

The Organic Gardener Podcast is perfect for gardeners who want to learn how to create a self-sustaining garden that works with nature, not against it. It also offers insights on the importance of biodiversity and protecting the environment through responsible gardening practices.

Why listen:

  • Emphasis on organic and sustainable gardening methods
  • Offers practical advice on growing food naturally
  • Great for gardeners who want to minimize their environmental impact

10. The Houseplant Coach Podcast

For plant lovers who want to master the art of houseplant care, The Houseplant Coach Podcast, hosted by Nancy, is a must-follow. This podcast is dedicated to helping listeners keep their indoor plants healthy, happy, and thriving.

Nancy covers a wide range of topics, from plant problems like pests and diseases to advice on repotting, pruning, and propagation. Each episode is designed to help listeners gain the confidence they need to take care of their plants and nurture their indoor gardens.

Why listen:

  • Focuses specifically on houseplant care
  • Offers solutions to common plant problems
  • Helps listeners build confidence in plant care

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re an experienced gardener or just starting your green thumb journey, these podcasts offer something for everyone. From gardening basics and plant science to food gardening and sustainable practices, each of these podcasts provides valuable insights and expert advice to help you grow as a gardener.

Podcasts are a great way to stay informed and inspired while on the go, allowing you to learn new techniques and hear from experts who can help you improve your gardening skills. Whether you’re looking to grow a vegetable garden, create a lush flower garden, or care for your indoor plants, these podcasts are sure to enhance your gardening experience. Happy gardening!
