Exploring Non-Newtonian Fluid Dynamics: From Oobleck to Blood Flow


Non-Newtonian fluids are a fascinating branch of fluid dynamics that defy the conventional laws of physics established by Sir Isaac Newton. Unlike Newtonian fluids, which have a constant viscosity regardless of the applied force, non-Newtonian fluids exhibit variable viscosity and can change their flow behavior under different conditions. In this blog post, we will delve into the intriguing world of non-Newtonian fluids, from the curious substance known as Oobleck to the complex dynamics of blood flow.

Oobleck: A Non-Newtonian Wonder

Let’s start by discussing Oobleck, a non-Newtonian fluid that can be easily made at home using household ingredients. Oobleck is a mixture of cornstarch and water, and its unique properties make it a popular science experiment for both children and adults. When you apply force to Oobleck, such as squeezing it or stomping on it, it behaves like a solid, resisting the external pressure. However, when left undisturbed, it flows like a liquid, allowing objects to sink into it.

The reason behind this behavior lies in the nature of the cornstarch particles suspended in the water. When force is applied, these particles align and lock together, creating a network that resists deformation. This phenomenon is known as shear thickening, where the viscosity of the fluid increases with applied stress. Conversely, when the force is removed, the particles relax and slide past each other, resulting in a decrease in viscosity and a return to liquid-like behavior.

Shear Thinning: From Ketchup to Blood

Another type of non-Newtonian fluid behavior is shear thinning, where the viscosity of the fluid decreases as the applied stress or shear rate increases. A common example of shear thinning is the behavior of ketchup. Have you ever struggled to pour ketchup out of a bottle, only to have it suddenly flow out in a quick stream? This is due to the shear thinning properties of ketchup.

In the case of blood, the flow behavior is also non-Newtonian. Blood is a complex mixture of cells, proteins, and plasma, and its viscosity varies depending on the shear rate. At low shear rates, such as when blood is flowing through capillaries, it behaves more like a viscous fluid. However, at high shear rates, such as when blood is pumped rapidly through arteries, it exhibits shear thinning behavior, allowing it to flow more easily.

Understanding the non-Newtonian behavior of blood is crucial in various medical applications, such as designing artificial organs, developing drug delivery systems, and diagnosing cardiovascular diseases. By studying the flow dynamics of blood, scientists and engineers can gain insights into the intricate workings of our circulatory system and develop innovative solutions to improve human health.

Conclusion

Non-Newtonian fluid dynamics offer a captivating glimpse into the complexity of fluid behavior. From the playful Oobleck to the intricate dynamics of blood flow, these fluids challenge our intuitive understanding of how liquids and solids behave. By exploring the unique properties and behaviors of non-Newtonian fluids, we can unlock new possibilities in fields ranging from materials science to medicine.

So, the next time you encounter a substance that defies your expectations, remember that there is a fascinating world of non-Newtonian fluid dynamics waiting to be explored. Whether it’s the mesmerizing dance of Oobleck or the life-sustaining flow of blood, these fluids remind us of the inherent beauty and complexity of the natural world.
