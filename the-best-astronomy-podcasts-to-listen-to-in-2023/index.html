The Best Astronomy Podcasts to Listen to in 2023


If you’re passionate about the cosmos, stargazing, and the mysteries of space, astronomy podcasts are a fantastic way to explore the universe from the comfort of your own home. Podcasts offer an accessible and immersive way to stay updated on the latest space discoveries, space missions, celestial events, and all things astronomical. Whether you’re an amateur skywatcher or a seasoned astronomer, there is a podcast out there for everyone. In this post, we’ll dive into some of the best astronomy podcasts to listen to in 2023, showcasing a mix of well-established shows and newer, unique gems that are worth checking out.

1. StarTalk Radio

StarTalk Radio is a long-standing staple in the world of science podcasts. Hosted by renowned astrophysicist Neil deGrasse Tyson, this show brings together humor, insightful discussions, and engaging celebrity guests to explore the wonders of space. It covers everything from black holes and quantum mechanics to the latest space explorations and science fiction.

What sets StarTalk apart is its ability to explain complex scientific concepts in an easy-to-understand way. Tyson’s ability to make deep space topics accessible to the general public has made StarTalk a must-listen for anyone interested in astronomy.

Why Listen:

  • Hosted by Neil deGrasse Tyson, one of the most well-known astrophysicists.
  • Engaging and entertaining discussions.
  • Celebrity guests and expert scientists weigh in on various topics.
  • A great mix of humor and science.

2. Planetary Radio

If you’re looking for an in-depth, thought-provoking podcast that covers space exploration and science, Planetary Radio by The Planetary Society is a perfect choice. This podcast takes listeners on a journey through space, with interviews from experts and scientists at the forefront of the latest space missions. From the exploration of Mars to the search for exoplanets, Planetary Radio provides thoughtful analysis of the developments in space science and exploration.

This podcast is known for its dedication to deep dives into specific topics, often featuring guests who are involved in current space research, including NASA engineers and planetary scientists. The show also provides updates on upcoming space missions and their significance to the greater understanding of the universe.

Why Listen:

  • Hosted by Mat Kaplan, a long-time space exploration journalist.
  • Focuses on space missions and scientific breakthroughs.
  • Interviews with space scientists and astronauts.
  • Provides insight into space exploration technologies and mission objectives.

3. The Infinite Monkey Cage

The Infinite Monkey Cage is a BBC Radio 4 podcast that brings together scientists, comedians, and other experts to discuss science in an approachable and engaging manner. While it covers all areas of science, it regularly delves deep into astronomy and astrophysics. Hosted by physicist Brian Cox and comedian Robin Ince, the show is a delightful mix of humor and rigorous scientific exploration.

In each episode, Cox and Ince break down complex space topics, such as dark matter, quantum physics, and the potential for life on other planets, all while maintaining a light-hearted tone. The Infinite Monkey Cage invites listeners to think about the universe in new and often unexpected ways.

Why Listen:

  • A fun blend of science and comedy.
  • Hosted by Brian Cox, a renowned physicist, and Robin Ince, a popular comedian.
  • Tackles tough questions and astronomical concepts in an entertaining manner.
  • High-quality guests from diverse fields.

4. Orbital Path

For those interested in the philosophical and existential side of space exploration, Orbital Path offers a unique approach to astronomy. Hosted by astronomer and science communicator Michelle Thaller, this podcast delves into the deeper questions about our place in the universe. Thaller interviews experts from various fields to discuss everything from the search for extraterrestrial life to the future of space travel and the societal impacts of exploring space.

With its reflective tone and insightful commentary, Orbital Path invites listeners to ponder not just the science behind space, but the emotional and ethical considerations of space exploration.

Why Listen:

  • Hosted by Michelle Thaller, an accomplished NASA astronomer.
  • Explores the philosophical and ethical implications of space exploration.
  • Engages listeners in thought-provoking discussions on life beyond Earth.
  • A more reflective and contemplative astronomy podcast.

5. Astronomy Cast

Astronomy Cast is a fantastic resource for anyone looking to learn about the fundamental concepts of astronomy and space science. Hosted by Fraser Cain and Dr. Pamela Gay, this show takes listeners on a guided journey through the universe. Whether you’re interested in the inner workings of our solar system, the latest advancements in space exploration, or the science behind cosmic phenomena, Astronomy Cast has you covered.

What makes this podcast particularly special is the way it breaks down complex subjects into manageable chunks. Each episode is dedicated to a specific astronomical topic, with discussions that are accessible to newcomers while still offering enough depth for seasoned space enthusiasts.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on educational content and deep dives into astronomy.
  • Hosted by Fraser Cain (of Universe Today) and Dr. Pamela Gay (a professional astronomer).
  • A well-rounded resource for beginners and experts alike.
  • Clear explanations of complex topics.

6. Cosmic Vertigo

Cosmic Vertigo is a relatively new podcast that is rapidly gaining attention for its unique approach to space science. Hosted by astrophysicist Dr. Janna Levin, this show looks at the vast and strange nature of the universe, bringing attention to the curious phenomena that defy explanation. From black holes and gravitational waves to the nature of time itself, Cosmic Vertigo explores the fundamental questions about the universe that still puzzle scientists.

Levin’s captivating storytelling style and her deep understanding of the cosmos make this podcast an intellectual treat for those interested in exploring the unknowns of the universe.

Why Listen:

  • Hosted by Dr. Janna Levin, a leading astrophysicist.
  • Covers mysterious and unexplained cosmic phenomena.
  • Offers a unique, inquisitive approach to space science.
  • A great podcast for listeners who enjoy exploring the unknown.

7. The Cosmic Companion

The Cosmic Companion takes a different approach by focusing on the intersection of science and popular culture. Hosted by Christian Ready, this podcast dives into the latest news from the world of space exploration, while also drawing connections to the way space is portrayed in TV, movies, and literature. The show discusses how science fiction reflects our hopes, dreams, and fears about space, and how real-life astronomy and space exploration often mimic the ideas first presented in sci-fi.

In addition to space news, The Cosmic Companion regularly features interviews with leading scientists and space professionals who share their insights on the future of space exploration.

Why Listen:

  • Explores both real and fictional space stories.
  • Hosted by Christian Ready, an experienced science communicator.
  • Features interviews with space experts and scientists.
  • Engaging mix of space science and pop culture.

8. SpaceTime with Stuart Gary

SpaceTime with Stuart Gary is an excellent daily podcast for anyone who wants to stay up-to-date with the latest news from the cosmos. Stuart Gary brings listeners daily updates on space missions, discoveries, and developments in the world of astronomy. The podcast covers everything from planetary science and space exploration to the search for extraterrestrial intelligence.

One of the standout features of SpaceTime is its quick and concise format. The podcast offers listeners the latest news in an easily digestible format, making it perfect for people who want to get their daily dose of astronomy without investing too much time.

Why Listen:

  • Daily updates on space news and discoveries.
  • Concise and easily accessible format.
  • Provides the latest in astronomy, space exploration, and technology.
  • Ideal for listeners who want to stay informed on space happenings.

9. AstroPod

AstroPod is another podcast that brings a fresh perspective to space exploration. Focused on discussing current astronomical research, technological innovations, and breakthroughs in space science, AstroPod features interviews with scientists, researchers, and engineers working on cutting-edge space missions.

The show provides insightful discussions on the role of technology in space exploration, from satellite imaging and space telescopes to the development of new spacecraft and propulsion systems.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on the technological side of space exploration.
  • Features interviews with leading space professionals and researchers.
  • Explains the science behind the technology shaping the future of space travel.

10. Eyes on the Sky

Eyes on the Sky is an astronomy podcast that is perfect for amateur astronomers and stargazers. Hosted by a seasoned astronomy enthusiast, this podcast offers practical tips for observing the night sky. Each episode highlights specific stars, planets, and constellations visible in the upcoming week, providing listeners with a guide to what they can see from their own backyards.

In addition to practical observing advice, Eyes on the Sky also delves into the science behind the celestial events, offering explanations of phenomena like meteor showers, eclipses, and planetary conjunctions.

Why Listen:

  • Ideal for amateur astronomers and skywatchers.
  • Provides practical advice for observing celestial events.
  • Focuses on stargazing and understanding the night sky.

Conclusion

Astronomy podcasts offer a rich and engaging way to connect with the cosmos. Whether you’re looking for educational content, the latest space news, or a thoughtful discussion of the philosophical implications of space exploration, there is a podcast for every type of listener. In 2023, these astronomy podcasts will continue to provide fascinating insights, expert interviews, and lively discussions, helping listeners of all backgrounds deepen their understanding of the universe. Tune in, expand your knowledge, and let the stars be your guide!
