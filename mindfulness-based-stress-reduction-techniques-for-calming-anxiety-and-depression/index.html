Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction: Techniques for Calming Anxiety and Depression


Stress, anxiety, and depression these days are like an umbrella term for the adrenal fatigue and mental turmoil that most individuals face in their daily lives. Harvard Health Publishing emphasizes the importance of stress reduction, explaining that chronic stress can exacerbate both physical and mental health issues. As the title suggests, this blog post will outline various techniques derived from Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) - a therapeutic approach that combines meditation, body awareness, and yoga to manage symptoms of anxiety and depression.

1. Mindfulness Meditation:

Mindfulness meditation refers to a method of training the mind to stay on the present moment. In other words, it’s like a conscious exercise of acknowledging and accepting the thoughts, emotions, and bodily sensations that arise in a given moment without engrossing too much into their meanings. While there is no singular formula to perform mindfulness meditation, it is generally practiced by focusing on your breathing and gently refocusing when your mind starts to wander.

To practice mindfulness meditation, try the following steps:

  1. Find a quiet and comfortable space: It could be a corner of your room, a park, or even a busy cafe (ideally, a place where you would feel least disturbed)
  2. Sit comfortably: You may want to cushion yourself onto the floor, use a chair, or sit cross-legged - whichever position you feel more comfortable and relaxed in
  3. Set a timer: Begin with 10 to 15 minutes and gradually increase your duration
  4. Close your eyes: This helps in uprooting distractions and aids in focusing on internal sensations
  5. Observe your breath: Pay undivided attention to your breath as it flows in and out of your nostrils or feel the rise and fall of your chest
  6. Acknowledge wandering thoughts: When your focus drifts to other thoughts, gently bring it back to your breath without dee-judging the thought

2. Mindful Breathing:

Mindful breathing, as the name suggests, is the practice of being aware of your breathing pattern. Every time you inhale and exhale, take a moment to enjoy, savor, and appreciate the sensation of air filling your nostrils or the rise and fall of your chest and abdomen.

Here’s a simple exercise:

  1. Find a comfortable seating position: Sit on a chair or on the floor, depending on what feels comfortable for you
  2. Place one hand on your upper chest and the other on your abdomen: These hands will sense your breathing
  3. Breathe deeply and slowly: Inhale through your nose and exhale through your mouth
  4. Feel the rise and fall of your chest and abdomen: As you do so, focus on the sensation of cool air entering and warm air leaving your nostrils
  5. Gently refocus on your breath: Whenever your mind starts wandering or becomes distracted, refocus it onto your breath

3. Body Scan Meditation:

A body scan is another mindfulness technique that enables you to tap into your internal landscape, transforming the emotional pain associated with stress, anxiety, and depression into a sensation that can be managed. To complete a body scan, shower your attention over various areas of your body- from your toes to the crown of your head- and feel physical sensations such as heat, pressure, or tightness.

Try this exercise:

  1. Find a quiet, dimly-lit space and sit or lie down: You may use a yoga mat floor or lie on your back on a bed
  2. Close your eyes: Focus on your breath here
  3. Start with your toes: Begin sensing any sensations or emotions and try to remain non-judgmental
  4. Move upward slowly: As you progress, systematically scan each limb, torso, and head
  5. Stay in the present moment: If you encounter any painful or difficult emotions, don’t try to resist or dismiss them – simply acknowledge them and let them go as you focus on the next area

4. Yoga and Mindful Movement:

Mindful movement, especially yoga, complements mindfulness meditation by incorporating physical postures, controlled breathing, and relaxation techniques. The American Psychological Association acknowledges the potential of yoga in reducing stress, anxiety, and depression by helping individuals to stay in the present moment.

Here’s a quick start for beginners:

  1. Warm up: Before starting yoga, start by stretching in a warm-up routine
  2. Choose gentle yoga poses: Simple poses such as Cat-Cow stretch, a standing forward bend, seated forward bend, and child’s pose work in elongating the back, releasing tension, and engendering a peaceful state of mind
  3. Practice rhythmic breathing: As you hold each pose or move from one pose to another, practice rhythmic breathing to encourage body awareness and a linking experience
  4. Finish with a restorative pose: Wind down your yoga session with a child’s pose or corpse pose (Savasana) to cultivate relaxation and inner calm

5. Mindful Eating:

Mindful eating, as the name suggests, entails being aware of the sensory experience of eating. This practice promotes self-awareness, presence, and appreciation for food. Here’s how to practice mindful eating:

  1. Prepare food mindfully: Take time to gratefully select and wash your vegetables or fruit
  2. Serve food on a clean plate: Free from clutter to help you focus only on your food
  3. Eat slowly and savor the aroma, taste, flavor, and texture of the food: Avoid multi-tasking – either sit at a table, away from the TV, laptop, or phone
  4. Observe your hunger and fullness signals: Allow your stomach to guide you on portion size

Integrate these MBSR practices into your daily routine to cultivate an awareness of present feelings and create a space for emotional, cognitive, and physical healing. While MBSR techniques often require time and consistency, their ability to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression make them a valuable tool in your self-care arsenal.
