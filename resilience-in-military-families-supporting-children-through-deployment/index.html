Resilience in Military Families: Supporting Children through Deployment


Military families face unique challenges, particularly when a loved one is deployed. While deployments are a necessary part of military life, they can be stressful and disruptive, especially for children. However, military families are incredibly resilient, and with the right support systems in place, children can navigate the challenges of deployment successfully. In this blog post, we explore the importance of resilience in military families and discuss strategies for supporting children through the deployment process. Deployment is a reality for many military families, often involving long periods of separation from loved ones. For children, this separation can be difficult to understand and cope with, leading to feelings of sadness, anxiety, and uncertainty. Additionally, children may experience changes in routine, caregiver roles, and family dynamics during deployment, further adding to their stress.

The Role of Resilience

Resilience is the ability to adapt and bounce back from adversity, and it plays a crucial role in helping military children cope with the challenges of deployment. Resilient children are better able to manage stress, regulate their emotions, and maintain positive relationships with family members and peers. Building resilience in military children can help them navigate the ups and downs of military life and emerge stronger and more capable of facing future challenges.

Strategies for Supporting Children through Deployment

Open Communication

Communication is key to helping children understand and cope with deployment. Military parents should have open and honest conversations with their children about the upcoming separation, reassuring them of their love and support. Children should be encouraged to ask questions and express their feelings, and parents should provide age-appropriate explanations about where their deployed loved one is and what they are doing.

Maintaining Routines

Maintaining consistent routines can provide a sense of stability and predictability for children during deployment. Military parents should strive to keep daily routines as normal as possible, including mealtimes, bedtime rituals, and extracurricular activities. Consistent routines help children feel safe and secure, even in the absence of their deployed parent.

Seeking Support

Military families should take advantage of the support services available to them, both on and off base. Military installations often offer a variety of resources for families facing deployment, including counseling services, support groups, and youth programs. Additionally, community organizations and non-profit groups may provide additional support and assistance to military families during deployment.

Encouraging Connections

Maintaining connections with the deployed parent is essential for children’s well-being during deployment. Technology makes it easier than ever for families to stay in touch, whether through video calls, emails, or letters. Encourage children to communicate regularly with their deployed parent and involve them in special activities or rituals, such as sending care packages or recording video messages.

Fostering Resilience

Finally, parents can help foster resilience in their children by modeling positive coping strategies and problem-solving skills. Encourage children to identify their strengths and find healthy ways to manage stress, such as exercise, hobbies, or spending time with supportive friends and family members. By building resilience in their children, military parents can empower them to navigate the challenges of deployment with confidence and resilience.

Conclusion

Deployment can be a challenging time for military families, particularly for children. However, with the right support systems in place, children can successfully navigate the ups and downs of deployment and emerge stronger and more resilient. By maintaining open communication, consistent routines, and connections with their deployed parent, military families can help children cope with the challenges of deployment and thrive in the face of adversity. Together, we can support military children and families as they demonstrate resilience and strength in the face of deployment.
