How to Prepare for the Fundamentals of Engineering Exam


The Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam is a crucial step for anyone aiming to pursue a career in engineering. Whether you’re a recent graduate or an experienced professional, preparing for the FE exam can be a challenging yet rewarding journey. In this post, we’ll break down effective strategies, tips, and best practices to help you prepare thoroughly for the exam, boost your confidence, and increase your chances of success.

Understand the Structure of the FE Exam

Before diving into preparation, it’s essential to understand what the FE exam entails. The exam is designed to assess your knowledge of fundamental engineering concepts. It’s the first step toward becoming a licensed professional engineer (PE).

The FE exam consists of 110 multiple-choice questions, and the topics covered vary depending on the engineering discipline you choose. The four main disciplines are:

  1. Chemical Engineering
  2. Civil Engineering
  3. Electrical and Computer Engineering
  4. Mechanical Engineering

Each of these disciplines has its own specific content outline, so knowing which area you’re focusing on is crucial. For most students, the exam is divided into two sections:

  • General Engineering Topics: These include mathematics, ethics, engineering economics, and others that apply to all fields of engineering.
  • Discipline-Specific Topics: These questions focus on the specialized knowledge of your chosen field.

Knowing the format and topics covered will help you prioritize your study time and focus on areas that are more likely to appear on the exam.

Set a Study Schedule and Stick to It

One of the keys to successful FE exam preparation is effective time management. Setting a structured study schedule is critical, as the exam requires both breadth and depth of knowledge. Here’s how to create a solid plan:

  1. Identify Your Timeline: Ideally, start preparing for the FE exam 3-6 months before the test date. This gives you enough time to study without overwhelming yourself.

  2. Break Down the Syllabus: Review the FE exam syllabus and identify key topics. Make a list of the topics you need to cover and prioritize the ones that are most difficult for you. Tackling difficult subjects first will ensure you don’t run out of time.

  3. Set Realistic Goals: Break your study plan into manageable weekly goals. For example, aim to cover certain chapters or topics each week. Ensure that you’re balancing time between general engineering topics and discipline-specific areas.

  4. Schedule Review Sessions: Plan periodic reviews in your study schedule. These should occur every couple of weeks to help reinforce what you’ve learned and identify areas of weakness that need more attention.

  5. Track Your Progress: Keep track of your progress throughout the study process. If you fall behind or need to adjust, don’t hesitate to modify your schedule to stay on track.

Use a Variety of Study Materials

There is no single source of information that will cover all the content you need to know for the FE exam. Using a combination of study materials will provide a well-rounded understanding of the topics.

1. FE Review Books

One of the most important study resources is a comprehensive FE review book. These books typically provide a breakdown of the exam’s subject areas, detailed explanations, and practice questions. Some popular FE review books include:

  • FE Review Manual by Michael R. Lindeburg
  • Fundamentals of Engineering Exam Review by Donald G. Newnan
  • FE Mechanical Review by Michael R. Lindeburg (for mechanical engineering)

These books often come with practice exams that simulate the format of the actual exam, allowing you to get a sense of the test-taking experience.

2. Online Resources and Courses

There are numerous online courses and study platforms that offer FE exam prep. Some provide structured study plans, while others offer self-paced learning. Online resources like FE Review Courses, NCEES (National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying), and platforms like Coursera or Udemy can provide both video lessons and practice tests. These are excellent tools for reviewing concepts and practicing problem-solving.

3. Practice Tests

Taking practice tests is one of the most effective ways to prepare. Not only do practice exams familiarize you with the format of the actual test, but they also allow you to assess your strengths and weaknesses. The more practice exams you take, the better you’ll become at managing time and handling question types you may not be familiar with.

You can find free practice questions from various study websites or purchase full practice exams from companies like NCEES or School of PE. Regularly taking practice tests can help you improve your test-taking strategies and pinpoint areas that require more attention.

4. Study Groups and Forums

Study groups can be incredibly helpful for sharing insights, asking questions, and discussing difficult topics. Whether in person or online, study groups allow you to learn from others’ experiences and gain a deeper understanding of the material.

You can also explore online engineering forums like Engineering.com or Reddit’s FE Exam Preparation subreddit. These platforms often feature discussions on specific exam topics, study strategies, and tips from other candidates who have recently taken the exam.

Master the Fundamentals of Mathematics

Mathematics is the backbone of the FE exam, regardless of which engineering discipline you focus on. Most of the exam will test your ability to apply mathematical principles in practical engineering scenarios. Here’s how to sharpen your math skills:

  1. Review Key Concepts: Make sure you have a strong grasp of basic algebra, calculus, differential equations, linear algebra, and statistics. These concepts are essential for solving problems efficiently on the exam.

  2. Understand Formulas and Units: You’ll need to recall and apply a variety of engineering formulas. Create flashcards or a formula sheet to keep track of the most important ones. Additionally, ensure that you are comfortable with both the SI (metric) and imperial systems of measurement.

  3. Practice Problem-Solving: Mathematics on the FE exam is about more than just memorizing formulas. You must practice applying them to real-world engineering problems. Use the practice tests and review books to focus on mathematical problem-solving.

Focus on Time Management

The FE exam consists of 110 questions to be completed within a set time frame of 6 hours. This means effective time management is critical to ensuring you can complete the exam without feeling rushed. Here are some time management strategies for the FE exam:

  1. Allocate Time Per Question: Aim to spend about 1.5 to 2 minutes per question. This will give you enough time to read and answer most questions efficiently.

  2. Skip Difficult Questions: If you encounter a particularly challenging question, don’t dwell on it. Skip it and come back later if you have time. Spending too much time on a single question could cause you to miss easier questions.

  3. Use the Calculator Wisely: You’ll be allowed to use a calculator during the exam, and it’s essential to know how to use it efficiently. Practice using your calculator during your study sessions to ensure you’re not wasting time looking up functions during the test.

  4. Take Breaks: During the exam, you’ll be allowed a short break. Use it to stretch, relax, and refresh your mind. A short break can improve your focus for the remainder of the test.

Stay Calm and Confident

The FE exam can be intimidating, especially when you’ve spent months preparing for it. However, staying calm and confident during the test is just as important as the preparation itself. Here are some tips to manage stress and perform well on exam day:

  1. Get Enough Rest: A good night’s sleep before the exam will help you feel refreshed and sharp. Avoid staying up late studying the night before.

  2. Eat Well: Eat a healthy meal before the exam to ensure your brain has the fuel it needs to focus. Avoid heavy or greasy foods that may make you feel sluggish.

  3. Arrive Early: Plan to arrive at the test center early so you have plenty of time to settle in. This will help you avoid unnecessary stress and give you a few minutes to relax.

  4. Breathe: If you start to feel overwhelmed during the exam, take a few deep breaths to calm your nerves. Focus on the present moment and tackle each question one step at a time.

Conclusion

Preparing for the Fundamentals of Engineering exam is no small feat, but with the right strategy, resources, and mindset, you can achieve success. Start by understanding the exam structure, setting a realistic study schedule, and using a variety of study materials to ensure a well-rounded preparation. Regular practice, mastering the fundamentals of mathematics, and managing your time efficiently will help you build confidence and perform your best on exam day.

Above all, remember that the FE exam is a critical step in your engineering career. By putting in the effort now, you’re taking a significant leap toward becoming a licensed professional engineer. Stay focused, stay organized, and most importantly, believe in your ability to succeed. Good luck!
