Top Epidemiology Conferences You Should Attend in 2023


The field of epidemiology continues to play a pivotal role in understanding and addressing public health challenges. From the global impacts of pandemics to the emergence of new disease surveillance techniques, staying up-to-date with the latest research, methodologies, and innovations is essential for professionals and researchers alike. Conferences provide a valuable opportunity to connect with peers, share cutting-edge findings, and foster collaborations that can shape the future of public health.

As we navigate 2023, many prestigious gatherings are set to bring together leading experts, practitioners, policymakers, and academics in the epidemiology domain. These conferences present opportunities to learn from thought leaders, advance professional knowledge, and contribute to impactful discussions. Below, we highlight some of the top epidemiology conferences you should consider attending in 2023 to stay ahead in this ever-evolving field.

1. World Congress on Epidemiology and Public Health

One of the most anticipated events in the epidemiology world, the World Congress on Epidemiology and Public Health, offers an unparalleled platform to delve into innovative research and pressing public health concerns. This conference addresses topics such as global disease burden, advancements in epidemiological tools, and solutions for public health inequalities. Keynote speakers from around the globe often share insights into emerging health threats, epidemiologic surveillance systems, and the effective use of big data in public health research.

Attendees can expect workshops, panel discussions, and symposiums focusing on diverse regions and their unique public health challenges. Whether you’re a novice in the field or a seasoned expert, the World Congress on Epidemiology and Public Health is a must-attend event in 2023 for learning and networking.

2. International Epidemiological Association (IEA) World Congress of Epidemiology

The International Epidemiological Association’s (IEA) World Congress of Epidemiology is revered for its scientific rigor and the depth of topics covered. This prestigious event only occurs every few years, making its 2023 iteration particularly significant. The conference brings together an interdisciplinary audience, including biostatisticians, epidemiologists, public health practitioners, and policymakers.

The focus of this year’s congress will include the impacts of social determinants of health, precision medicine, and the intersection of epidemiology with artificial intelligence and machine learning. The event also emphasizes creating dialogues around ethical challenges in research, particularly in underrepresented populations. Engaging presentations and hands-on workshops help ensure participants leave equipped with actionable knowledge to influence their practice.

3. European Congress of Epidemiology (EURO-EPI)

The European Congress of Epidemiology is one of the leading events on the continent, offering a comprehensive overview of epidemiological research and practice. EURO-EPI 2023 will focus on key issues such as the impacts of climate change on health, infectious disease modeling, and the integration of sustainable health systems in Europe. It also facilitates a platform for showcasing collaborative projects that bring together academia, non-governmental organizations, and government bodies.

Unlike many other conferences, EURO-EPI places a strong emphasis on mentorship, with tailored sessions for early-career researchers and student attendees. If you’re looking to share your work, gain feedback, or find collaborators for your next research project, this conference offers plenty of opportunities.

4. Global Infectious Diseases Congress

With the continued global focus on infectious diseases, the Global Infectious Diseases Congress provides an excellent platform for exploring topics such as antimicrobial resistance, vaccine development, and pandemic preparedness. This conference brings together leaders in infectious disease epidemiology to discuss strategies for combating new and emerging pathogens while reflecting on lessons learned from recent global crises.

Attendees can look forward to thought-provoking discussions on the integration of artificial intelligence in disease tracking, the analysis of genetic epidemiology, and the role of community-driven interventions in infectious disease control. This event is especially valuable for professionals working in epidemiology research, public health leadership, and clinical practice.

5. Society for Epidemiologic Research (SER) Annual Meeting

The SER Annual Meeting has long been a cornerstone of epidemiological education and collaboration in the United States. This conference is renowned for its focus on methodologic advances in epidemiology and its relevance across various domains, including chronic disease, health disparities, and maternal and child health.

One of the highlights of the SER Annual Meeting is the rich programming tailored explicitly for researchers at different career stages. From early-career development sessions to advanced statistical methodology workshops, the event ensures it caters to a broad academic and practical audience. For those interested in improving their technical skills and networking on a personal level, this conference stands out as a key event in the calendar year.

6. Asia-Pacific Epidemiology Conference

The Asia-Pacific Epidemiology Conference is a prominent platform for addressing region-specific issues while maintaining a global perspective on epidemiological innovation. This year’s conference will cover emerging health concerns in the Asia-Pacific region, ranging from vector-borne diseases to the non-communicable disease epidemic. Topics on digital epidemiology, health equity, and telehealth’s impact on epidemiological practices are also expected to feature prominently.

The event has a unique emphasis on empowering epidemiologists in resource-limited settings, offering accessible platforms for discussing practical surveillance and intervention strategies. For professionals interested in learning from and contributing to epidemiology in diverse contexts, this conference provides valuable insights.

7. American College of Epidemiology (ACE) Annual Meeting

The ACE Annual Meeting is tailored to professionals who aim to integrate evidence-based epidemiology into public policy and decision-making. Known for fostering engaging discussions on contemporary issues, the 2023 meeting will explore themes related to health informatics, the role of technology in public health, and the ethical considerations surrounding data-sharing and privacy in epidemiological studies.

The ACE also emphasizes presentations and workshops that provide actionable knowledge, making it a perfect setting for translating research findings into meaningful policy recommendations. Attending this conference can provide fresh ideas to address complex public health challenges effectively.

8. Canadian Society for Epidemiology and Biostatistics (CSEB) Biennial Conference

The CSEB Biennial Conference is a leading event for epidemiologists, biostatisticians, and public health researchers across Canada and internationally. The 2023 conference will focus on leveraging population health data, health systems resilience, and the impacts of environmental factors on chronic and infectious disease epidemiology.

This event is also well-known for its student-friendly approach, offering dedicated networking sessions, poster presentations, and travel awards. It’s a great platform for early researchers to build connections while learning from leading figures in the field.

9. Epidemiology Congress of the Americas

Held every five years, the Epidemiology Congress of the Americas is one of the most comprehensive conferences focusing on health disparities and epidemiological science across North, Central, and South America. The event gathers epidemiologists, statisticians, and public health advocates to address critical topics, such as climate and health, rural health disparities, and indigenous health programs.

The 2023 conference promises new discussions on cross-border collaborations and innovative approaches in disease prevention and health promotion. If you’re looking for a truly interdisciplinary event, this congress is unparalleled in its scope and influence.

10. Middle East and North Africa Epidemiology Forum

The Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region is confronted with unique epidemiological challenges, including conflict-driven health crises, the increasing burden of non-communicable diseases, and the impacts of urbanization on public health. The MENA Epidemiology Forum is an essential platform to explore public health strategies tailored to this region’s context.

The forum focuses on practical solutions that leverage local expertise while integrating global best practices. It also places emphasis on collaborative networks, making it an excellent destination for professionals interested in working on global-health projects with a significant regional impact.

Why Attend Epidemiology Conferences in 2023?

Attending an epidemiology conference in 2023 offers invaluable opportunities to stay informed about the latest developments, exchange ideas with peers, and discover innovative solutions that can significantly impact health outcomes. Moreover, conferences foster professional and personal growth, providing an environment where attendees can build meaningful connections and engage in interdisciplinary learning.

For researchers, these events often serve as a springboard for groundbreaking work, enabling discussions that can guide future studies and grant proposals. For practitioners, they offer actionable insights into the application of epidemiologic practices in real-world settings. Whether you’re interested in infectious diseases, non-communicable conditions, health inequalities, or methodologies, there’s a conference this year tailored to your interests and needs.

With such a diverse range of conferences on the calendar for 2023, there’s no shortage of opportunities to immerse yourself in the dynamic and impactful world of epidemiology. Mark your calendars, submit your abstracts, and prepare to be inspired by the wealth of knowledge and collaboration these gatherings foster.
