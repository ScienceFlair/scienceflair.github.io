In this blog post, we will explore the fascinating world of plant stomata and delve into the physiology behind their crucial roles in gas exchange and water regulation. Anatomy of Stomata Stomata are tiny openings on the epidermis, the outer layer of plant tissue. They consist of two specialized cells, known as guard cells, that surround a pore. The guard cells play a vital role in controlling the opening and closing of stomata, thereby regulating gas exchange and water loss. When the guard cells are turgid, the stomata open, allowing gases to enter and exit.
