Mycotoxins are toxic compounds produced by certain types of fungi. They can contaminate a wide range of agricultural products, including cereals, nuts, spices, dried fruits, and coffee beans. Exposure to mycotoxins can have serious health implications for humans and animals, making it essential to understand the dangers associated with these toxic fungi. What Are Mycotoxins? Mycotoxins are secondary metabolites produced by molds and fungi. These toxic compounds can develop in crops both before and after harvest, depending on the environmental conditions. Common molds associated with mycotoxin production include Aspergillus, Penicillium, and Fusarium species.
