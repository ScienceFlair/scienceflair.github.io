As the world’s population continues to grow, the demand for sustainable and healthy seafood options is on the rise. However, the growth of aquaculture also poses significant environmental and ecological risks, particularly in regards to aquatic invasive species. Aquatic invasive species (AIS) are non-native species that are introduced to an ecosystem, often through human activity, and can cause significant harm to the environment, economy, and human health. In the context of aquaculture, AIS can be introduced through the movement of infected or contaminated stock, equipment, and ballast water.
