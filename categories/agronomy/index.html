In acidic soils (low pH), elements such as aluminum, manganese, and iron can become more soluble and potentially toxic to plants, while essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium may become less available for uptake. Conversely, in alkaline soils (high pH), deficiencies of micronutrients such as iron, zinc, and copper may arise, affecting plant growth and development.
