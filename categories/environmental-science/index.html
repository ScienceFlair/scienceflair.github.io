The Effects of Air Pollution on Respiratory Health

Air pollution has emerged as one of the most pressing public health challenges of our time. With rapid industrialization, urbanization, and increased vehicular traffic, the air quality in many regions of the world has deteriorated significantly. This decline poses serious risks to respiratory health, affecting millions globally. Understanding the relationship between air pollution and respiratory ailments is crucial for developing effective public health policies and personal preventive measures. What is Air Pollution? Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere. These pollutants can be classified into two main categories: primary and secondary pollutants. Primary pollutants are directly emitted from sources such as vehicles, factories, and power plants. Common examples include particulate matter (PM), nitrogen oxides (NOx), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and volatile organic compounds (VOCs). Secondary pollutants, on the other hand, are formed when primary pollutants undergo chemical reactions in the atmosphere. Ground-level ozone is a well-known example of a secondary pollutant that poses significant health risks. ...
