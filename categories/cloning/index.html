Dolly the Sheep: A Landmark in Cloning History

In the realm of biotechnology and genetics, few names evoke as much fascination and intrigue as Dolly the Sheep. Born on July 5, 1996, Dolly made history as the first mammal to be cloned from an adult somatic cell using a technique called somatic cell nuclear transfer (SCNT). Her birth marked a significant milestone in cloning technology and sparked widespread debate and ethical considerations surrounding the implications of cloning. Dolly was created by a team of scientists at the Roslin Institute in Scotland, led by Dr. Ian Wilmut. The researchers used a technique called somatic cell nuclear transfer to clone Dolly from an adult somatic cell. In this process, the nucleus of a donor cell, taken from the udder of a six-year-old sheep, was transferred into an enucleated egg cell. The egg cell was then stimulated to develop into an embryo using electrical pulses before being implanted into the uterus of a surrogate mother sheep. ...
