This damage is linked to a range of chronic diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders. Incorporating antioxidant-rich foods into our diet is a proactive way to combat oxidative damage and support overall health. Before delving into the role of antioxidants, it’s important to understand the process of oxidative damage. Free radicals are produced as a natural byproduct of metabolic processes within the body, and they can also be generated by exposure to environmental factors such as pollution, radiation, and cigarette smoke.
