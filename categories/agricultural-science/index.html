As the world grapples with the challenges of food security, environmental sustainability, and resource scarcity, aquaponics emerges as a promising solution that exemplifies the convergence of agricultural science and technology. At the heart of aquaponics lies the concept of symbiosis, where the waste produced by fish serves as a nutrient source for plants, and in turn, the plants purify the water for the fish. In a typical aquaponics system, fish are raised in a tank, and their waste-rich water is circulated into a hydroponic grow bed where plants, such as lettuce, herbs, or tomatoes, are cultivated. Beneficial bacteria in the grow bed convert the fish waste (ammonia) into nitrates, which serve as vital nutrients for the plants.
