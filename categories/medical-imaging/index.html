Radiation safety is a critical aspect of medical imaging that cannot be overlooked. As technology continues to advance, medical imaging techniques such as X-rays, computed tomography (CT) scans, and nuclear medicine have become indispensable tools in diagnosing and treating various medical conditions. However, it is essential to recognize that these imaging modalities utilize ionizing radiation, which can pose potential risks if not properly managed and controlled. Understanding Ionizing Radiation Ionizing radiation refers to high-energy electromagnetic waves or particles that have enough energy to remove tightly bound electrons from atoms, resulting in the formation of charged ions. These ions can interact with biological tissues, potentially causing damage to cells and DNA.
