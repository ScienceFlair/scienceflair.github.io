The application of forensic ballistic imaging has proven to be instrumental in criminal investigations, providing critical evidence that can link suspects to crime scenes and aid in the pursuit of justice. At the core of forensic ballistic imaging is the concept that every firearm leaves unique markings on the bullets and cartridge cases it fires. These markings are a result of the firearm’s individual characteristics, such as the barrel’s rifling pattern, firing pin impression, and breech face marks. When a bullet is fired, it travels down the barrel of the firearm, and the rifling leaves distinct marks on the projectile. Similarly, the firing pin and breech face leave identifiable impressions on the cartridge case. These markings are distinctive to each firearm and can be used to establish a connection between a bullet or cartridge case and the gun that discharged it. ...
