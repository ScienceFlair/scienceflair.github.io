With just one exception, which we will address later, questions in English always begin with an auxiliary verb (e.g., is, are, does, etc.), followed by the subject and finally the main verb. This structure typically takes the form of an Auxiliary-Subject-Verb (ASV) pattern. For example, “Do you like apples?” follows this structure: do-you-like-apples.
