The Diverse Forms of Carnivorous Plants Carnivorous plants come in a wide array of forms, each uniquely adapted to capture and digest prey. One of the most iconic carnivorous plants is the Venus flytrap (Dionaea muscipula), renowned for its hinged trap that snaps shut when triggered by unsuspecting insects. Another remarkable species is the pitcher plant (Nepenthes), which features a fluid-filled pitfall trap that lures insects to their demise. The sundew (Drosera) is adorned with glistening, sticky tentacles that ensnare insects, while the bladderwort (Utricularia) boasts tiny bladder-like traps that suck in small aquatic organisms.
