From mountains to bodies of water, the features of the land significantly impact the weather we experience on a daily basis. Mountain Ranges and Weather One of the most prominent examples of how landscapes influence weather is the impact of mountain ranges. As air is forced to rise over a mountain range, it cools and condenses, leading to cloud formation and precipitation on the windward side of the mountain. This phenomenon, known as orographic lift, can result in significantly higher precipitation levels on the windward side of the mountains, creating lush, green environments. Conversely, the leeward side, or rain shadow, experiences drier conditions as the air descends and warms, leading to less precipitation. This stark contrast in weather patterns on opposite sides of a mountain range is a prime example of how landscapes shape local weather. ...
