The Role of Cognitive Load in Human Computer Interaction

Cognitive load plays a crucial role in shaping the effectiveness of human-computer interaction (HCI). It refers to the mental effort required by individuals to process information and perform tasks when using computer systems. Understanding cognitive load is essential for designing user-friendly interfaces that optimize user experience and productivity. What is Cognitive Load? Cognitive load can be divided into three types: intrinsic, extraneous, and germane. Intrinsic cognitive load is the inherent difficulty of a task and is determined by its complexity. It depends on the individual’s prior knowledge and the nature of the task itself. Extraneous cognitive load refers to the unnecessary mental effort imposed by the design or presentation of information. On the other hand, germane cognitive load refers to the mental effort required for learning and problem-solving. ...
