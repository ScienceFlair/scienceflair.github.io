Gardens of the Gods: Exploring Earth's Lush, Otherworldly Labyrinths of Green


Nature has the power to leave us breathless, and it does so often with just a simple glance at its boundless beauty. Perhaps one of the most awe-inspiring wonders of Mother Nature is the breath-taking sight of gardens that seem to have been transported straight out of a fairytale, creating kingdoms filled with verdant virtues and natural marvels. From the breathtakingly surreal lavender fields of France to the dramatic landscapes of Iceland’s Golden Circle, Earth’s Gardens of the Gods captivate the imagination and inspire wanderlust in the hearts and minds of even the most seasoned travelers. By embarking on a journey through these lush, otherworldly cathedrals of flora, these enchanting botanical playgrounds will leave travellers enchanted, beckoning them to step away from the mundane routine and reconnect with the awe-inspiring beauty imbibed within nature herself.

One of the most famous Gardens of the Gods is the colourful, otherworldly Field of Light in the Australian outback. Installed by internationally-renowned artist Bruce Munro, this incredible art installation stretches across 7 hectares of the breathtaking desert landscape, featuring over 50,000 individual stems topped with colorful sphere-shaped flower blooms, lighting up the desert with the soft glow of Dunelight and creating magnificent, mesmerising patterns in the cool desert air. Unable to comprehend the size of this glowing, enchanted garden, one feels like a tiny speck of sand in this vast, desert wonderland, grasping the enormity and majesty of the natural world.

On the other hand, the Gardens of Babylon in Germany are a more conventional garden, yet they are still not short of awe-inspiring. Surrounded by lush, green gardens filled with a beautiful collection of flora from across the world, the central structure of this paradise - Baylon’s Tower - is a magnificent architectural marvel, towering above the botanical beauty and dominating the sky. The visual harmony created by the fusion of the humbling grandeur and the verdant serenity is a sight for sore eyes, creating a surreal, almost magical environment in the heart of Germany.

France too has its own hidden Garden of the Gods, nestled in the picturesque alpine village of Eguisheim. The picturesque village road is lined with perfectly manicured rows of colorful roses, carnations, and hibiscus, creating an idyllic and romantic Old World atmosphere. The delicate scents that waft through the air create a dreamy atmosphere, pulling the visitor through these exceptionally beautiful streets reminiscent of a fairy tale.

Nature can indeed play tricks with our perception of reality, creating surreal landscapes in every corner of the world. Iceland’s Seljalandsfoss and Skogafoss waterfalls are not only magnificent due to their thunderous water crashes, but also the verdant cliffsides that they cascaded down. Reaching the foot of the waterfalls exposes travelers to a secret garden of moss-covered basalt columns, the structure of which resembles the Chrysler Building’s Googie-styled façade.

On the other end of the world lies Tianzi Mountain in China’s Hunan Province, where lush green forests cover jagged karst peaks, creating yet another fantastical landscape. With an environment that stubbornly defies gravity and logic, the verdant maze gives the impression of towering skyscrapers emerging from the clouds, a phenomenon further accentuated by the intermittent mist that swirls and wraps around the peaks.

For garden enthusiasts as well as eco-aware travelers, the Gardens of the Gods offer unparalleled opportunities to venture into the most mesmerising, beautiful, and lush parts of the world. From the majestic beauty of lavender-filled French fields to the breathtaking magnificence of Iceland’s Golden Circle, these Gardens of the Gods provide a humbling reminder of the wonders bestowed upon us by Mother Nature. As we tread lightly on these botanical playgrounds, it’s a call to preserve and protect such extraordinary gifts that give even the jaded traveler a chance to be enchanted all over again.
