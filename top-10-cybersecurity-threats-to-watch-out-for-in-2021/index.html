Top 10 Cybersecurity Threats to Watch Out For in 2021


With the rise in remote work due to the pandemic, the attack surface for cyber threats has expanded significantly. abril2020 This opens up numerous opportunities for cyber attackers to exploit vulnerabilities in home networks, public Wi-Fi, and other unsecured connections.

  1. Phishing Attacks

Phishing remains one of the most widespread and profitable attacks on the internet. Cybercriminals continue to refine their techniques and devise ever more sophisticated lures to steal valuable information such as login credentials, credit card details, and sensitive data.

  1. Ransomware Attacks

Ransomware attacks have grown increasingly frequent and sophisticated. These attacks encrypt data and demand payment in exchange for the decryption key. In 2021, expect ransomware attacks to target critical infrastructure, government entities, and healthcare systems with enormous sums of money demanded as ransom.

  1. Supply Chain Attacks

Supply chain attacks are a significant threat to businesses in 2021. Attackers target third-party vendors and suppliers to gain access to sensitive information, steal trade secrets, or disrupt operations. It is crucial to vet suppliers and vendors thoroughly to reduce the risk of supply chain attacks.

  1. IAST (Intrusion As Detection) Attacks

IAST tools continuously monitor an application’s runtime environment for potential threats by inspects and analyzing both the application and the underlying system, looking for signs of malicious activity.

  1. DDoS Attacks

Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks can quickly overwhelm a company’s web servers and networks, causing downtime and significant damage to a company’s reputation. Advances in DDoS attack tools and techniques make it necessary for businesses to adopt robust DDoS protection mechanisms.

  1. -cloud

The cloud presents new opportunities for cyber attackers to target businesses. As organizations migrate to the cloud, they must be mindful of the new attack vectors that emerge. Cloud security strategies should involve a combination of encryption, access controls, and threat intelligence.

  1. Industrial Control Systems

Industrial Control Systems (ICS) are the lifeline of critical infrastructure and manufacturing facilities. Cyber attackers are increasingly targeting these systems to disrupt operations, steal intellectual property, or gain a strategic advantage. ICS security must be a top priority in 2021.

  1. Electromagnetic Spectrum

The electromagnetic spectrum is a critical component of modern communication systems, and its security must not be overlooked. Attackers can disrupt GPS signals, cellphone networks, and other critical infrastructure using spectrum capabilities. To mitigate these risks, robust electromagnetic spectrum management strategies must be devised.

  1. Deepfake Technology

Deepfake technology allows cyber attackers to create realistic media, such as videos and audio files, to deceive their targets. This technology poses a significant threat to individuals, businesses, and governments. It is essential to increase awareness of deepfake technology and its implications to minimize its impact on cybersecurity.

In conclusion, cybersecurity threats are constantly evolving, and businesses must remain vigilant and proactive in their defense against cyber attackers. By incorporating robust cybersecurity strategies that address the top 10 threats outlined above, businesses can safeguard their operations, reputation, and intellectual property.
