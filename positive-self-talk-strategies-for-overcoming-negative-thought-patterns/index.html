Positive Self-Talk: Strategies for Overcoming Negative Thought Patterns


We all have that inner voice deep within our minds that loves to disrupt our everyday life. Sometimes, it suggests doubts, compares us to others, and even makes us question our own existence. In psychology, this is referred to as ’negative self-talk.’ Although it may seem harmless initially, negative self-talk can successfully derail any progress or happiness we strive to achieve. But worry not, because today we’ll explore some strategies that can help conquer these negative thought patterns, thus paving the way for a more inner-dialogue-driven positive self-talk.

1. Identify the Inner Critic

The first step to overcoming negative self-talk is to identify who or what your inner critic is. Are they based on past experiences, society’s expectations, or your subconscious fears? The more you understand where these negative thoughts come from, the easier it will be to challenge and dismantle them.

2. Challenge Negative Thoughts

It’s essential to remember that not every thought you think is true. Negative self-talk feeds on the fear of failure or inadequacy, which isn’t always rooted in reality. By challenging these negative thoughts, you’re planting the seeds for newfound resilience and self-confidence. Ask yourself if there’s evidence to support these thoughts, or if they’re stemming from ungrounded fears.

3. Reframe the Narrative

Our minds often feel too small to contain the colossal chorus of negativity. Reframing the narrative surrounding negative thoughts means changing the way you perceive them. Instead of seeing them as facts, decide to view them as just opinions to be asserted. Using assertive language and reframing your thoughts can change your perspective and give you more control over your thought patterns.

4. Strengthen Your Self-Love

Replace negative thoughts with positive affirmations. This will help foster a deeper self-love towards yourself. Self-love expands in three ways; self-compassion, self-appreciation and self-acceptance. When you replace negative self-talk with affirmations, you’re laying the foundation of a strong and healthy self-esteem. Some examples of positive affirmations include, “I am capable of overcoming any obstacle that comes my way” or “I am deserving of happiness and success.”

5. Cultivate Positivity

Surround yourself with positivity. This will give your mindset a surrounding support system. Spend time with people that uplift and motivate your growth, read books that inspire you and fill your environment with positivity so your mind begins to believe that your thoughts can be changed for the better.

6. Practice Mindfulness

Mindfulness allows us to be fully present in the moment, observing our thoughts without judgment. This practice enables us to see our negative self-talk as just thoughts that come and go, rather than getting consumed by them.

7. Take Control of Your Thoughts

Lastly, remember that you have the power to control your thoughts. The responsibility lies within your reach to reject the hold negative self-talk has on you. Practice replacing these adversarial thoughts with affirmative ones. Recognize that these negative thoughts aren’t part of your true essence. They are fleeting thoughts that won’t define you if you don’t let them.

Identifying and dismantling negative self-talk patterns can take time and effort. But when you come out of this journey, the reward you get in return will be a life filled with self-confidence, resilience and happiness. Take small steps every day to hone the skill of effective and positive self-talk, and soon, you’ll be freeing yourself from the chains that held you back. After all, who doesn’t want a little more power in their corner, right?
