Essential Resources for Veterinary Students and Professionals


Embarking on a journey in the veterinary profession can be an exciting and daunting experience. As veterinary students and professionals, it is crucial to stay up-to-date with the latest technology, techniques, and research to provide the highest level of care to our animal patients. In this post, we will explore essential resources to enhance your veterinary career and encourage continuous learning.

Veterinary Textbooks and Online Learning Platforms

1. Faber & Katz’s Animal Nutrition: A Handbook for Veterinarians and Animal Science Students

This comprehensive textbook provides a deep understanding of animal nutrition, covering various species, nutritional disorders, dietary requirements, and feeding strategies. It is an excellent resource for veterinary students and professionals seeking to enhance their knowledge in nutrition science.

2. Small Animal Internal Medicine, 6th Edition

Written by experts in the field, this robust textbook offers in-depth information on internal medicine. It covers a wide range of diseases, diagnostic procedures, and treatments for small animals, making it an invaluable resource for veterinary professionals.

3. Clinical Veterinary Advisor: The Horse, 2nd Edition

Devoted exclusively to equine healthcare, this resource covers a broad range of topics, from wellness and nutrition to diseases and emergencies. It proves to be a useful reference for veterinarians specializing in equine medicine.

4. Learning Online: What Works Best for Students (VetEducate)

VetEducate is an online learning portal specifically designed for veterinary education. It offers a range of tutorials, workshops, and resources for veterinary students and professionals, focusing on improving student engagement and practical skills.

5. MasterClasses

MasterClasses is an online learning platform offering courses taught by experts in various fields, including Veterinary Medicine. You can find courses on topics such as zoonotic diseases, wildlife conservation, and animal behavior, delivered by experienced professionals. This platform allows for flexible learning and self-paced study.

Veterinary Professional Organizations

1. American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA)

AVMA is a professional organization for veterinarians with resources on continuing education, public policy, and career development. AVMA’s membership provides access to publications like the Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association.

2. World Small Animal Veterinary Association (WSAVA)

WSAVA is another reputable organization that supports and connects veterinarians globally. They offer guidelines, publications, and tools for veterinary professionals. Membership grants access to their quarterly journal, the “World Veterinary Journal.”

3. Association of American Veterinary Medical Colleges (AAVMC)

AAVMC represents veterinary medical schools and colleges in the United States and Canada. They have resources on research, education, and student development. They also maintain the Veterinary Information Network (VIN), an online platform for veterinary professionals.

Veterinary Journals and Publications

1. Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association (JAVMA)

JAVMA is the official scientific publication of the AVMA. It covers a wide range of topics related to veterinary medicine, including research, case reports, and clinical reviews.

2. Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine (JVIM)

JVIM is a peer-reviewed veterinary journal focusing on small animal internal medicine. This journal offers up-to-date research and resources for veterinarians looking to enhance their clinical expertise.

3. The Veterinary Journal

This is the oldest continuously published English-language veterinary journal. A broad range of topics is covered, making it suitable for all veterinary professionals.

Online Veterinary Communities

1. Forum for Veterinary Schools (FVS)

FVS is an online community for veterinary students, professionals, and academics. This network enables collaboration, resource sharing, and discussion on various topics in veterinary medicine.

2. Veterinary Medicines Directorate (VMD)

The VMD is the United Kingdom’s veterinary medicines regulator. Besides providing regulatory updates, their website offers free resources on veterinary medicine, legislation, and access to their online database of veterinary medicines.

3. Veterinary Information Network (VIN)

VIN is an online platform for veterinary professionals, providing access to a vast range of resources, continuing education courses, and a community dedicated to exchanging knowledge and experience.

Affordable Access to Veterinary Resources

1. AccessVet

AccessVet is a nonprofit organization offering free access to a wide range of veterinary textbooks and journals. They collaborate with publishers to provide a constantly updated library of invaluable resources for veterinary professionals.

2. Open Access – Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ)

DOAJ maintains a directory of high-quality, peer-reviewed open access journals, including veterinary science publications. It’s an excellent source of free, scholarly research articles for veterinary professionals.

As veterinary students and professionals, seeking knowledge and embracing a culture of continuous learning is mandatory to provide optimal care for our patients. By leveraging these resources, you will not only remain up-to-date in your field but also grow as an invaluable asset to your practice and the veterinary profession. Let’s make a commitment to lifelong learning.
