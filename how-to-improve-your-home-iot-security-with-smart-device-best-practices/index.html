How to Improve Your Home IoT Security with Smart Device Best Practices


Smart home devices and the Internet of Things (IoT) have officially taken over our homes. They help us manage our lives in the most convenient ways possible, transforming everything from lights, blinds, and thermostats to security systems and home appliances. We’re now anticipating a future where all our tasks will be performed with a single, effortless touch. However, with great convenience comes the risk of potential security vulnerabilities. Hackers can exploit unprotected devices to gain access to your entire network, compromising your privacy and safety. Fear not, for there are many ways you can strengthen your IoT security to keep your home safe. Here is a comprehensive guide on smart device best practices to boost your home IoT security.

1. Use Strong Passwords and Encryption for All Devices

First, ensure every device you connect to the internet has a unique, strong and complex password. Two-factor authentication adds an additional layer of security on devices that support it. Also, ensure you’re using the latest cybersecurity protocols such as WPA3 and HTTPS when setting up your device - these protocols will encrypt and protect information transmitted between devices.

2. Regularly Update Firmware

KeepIoT devices up to date with the latest firmware, as manufacturers continually find and fix vulnerabilities in their software. Many devices lack the ability to do updates directly, forcing you to manually check for updates on the manufacturer’s site. Make this a regular habit to guard against potential vulnerabilities or threats.

3. Secure the Network

Set up Smart Home devices on a separate network segment of your router with its unique SSID. Change the router admin password to something hard to guess and enable MAC address filtering. This step will prevent unauthorized access to smart devices, minimizing the attack surface that would-be hackers can exploit.

4. Disable Factory-Default and Guest Accounts

A majority of smart devices come pre-configured with factory-default usernames and passwords. To secure your device, change these default credentials when setting up your device or when prompted by the manufacturer’s update. Guest accounts also need to be disabled unless they provide added security utility in your particular setup.

5. Use Secure Apps and Channels When Accessing Your Smart Devices

Only use official mobile and web applications for controlling smart devices to avoid malicious actors accessing your devices. Official app stores take measures to vet developers, reducing the risk of rogue apps.

6. Segment Your Network

When it comes to securing your IoT devices, segmenting the hardware from your computer network can decrease the exposure of downloads, emails, or even IoT device-related scanning tools. Devices with weak security oregasus’s should reside in their isolated network with access only to their primary function server.

7. Monitor Activity Regularly

Becoming familiar with daily and weekly typical operation behaviors of your network and IoT devices can reduce the chance of an intrusion. If the usual traffic patterns suddenly change or devices start behaving irregularly, you should investigate further.

8. Limit Physical Access

Supervise children, housekeepers, or anyone with mundane access to your IoT devices. Always remember to lock doors and windows to the declared IoT network room or closet.

9. Restrict Device Capabilities

Limit the range and capability of your IoT devices as much as possible - only provide a device with the access rights it needs to perform its designated task. For example, a smart lock should only need to release the bolt, and not tell you what particular show is on TV.

10. Regularly Delete Unused or Unnecessary Connected Accounts

If you are selling or disposing of the connected device, be sure to delete any accounts that were connected to this device. This can prevent potential hackers from using this data to access your information.

11. Physical Access Equals Root Access

Always remember that unauthorized physical access to a device may lead to it being rooted and compromised since IoT devices have relatively open access for mobile devices. Hence, always be cautious when handling IoT devices.

As much as IoT technology increases our home convenience, it also comes with extra responsibilities in managing a secure network. Overlooking security measures can lead to system instability, data breaches, and other damaging consequences. Follow the smart device best practices outlined above to optimize your home’s IoT security, and enjoy your smart home devices worry-free!
