Material Science and the Popular Culture: Analyzing the Science behind Fiction


Material science, often validated by its lesser-known moniker “the science of stuff,” is a fascinating arena where physical science, engineering, and chemistry converge. It delves into the properties, composition, and interactions of different materials, explicating their roles across an extensive spectrum, from structural engineering to electronics and medicine. In the age of cinema, television, and literature, it’s intriguing to analyze how material science finds its reflection in popular culture – from fictional metals and alloys that possess extraordinary properties, to the generation of lifelike artificial skin. Let’s embark on this journey together, exploring what material science has contributed to the lore of popular culture while deciphering the science behind the magic we witness on screen and in print.

Steampunk Alchemy: Where Material Science Meets Fictional Metamorphosis

Steampunk, a subgenre that blends alternate history, futuristic technology, and Victorian-era milieu, is where material science cements its place among fan-favorite elements. Writers revel in the concoction of futuristic machines powered by steam and operated by complex materials that bend the laws of physics with ease. These narratives often dwell on the illusion of a bygone era juxtaposed with cutting-edge innovation. Consider the iconic H.G. Wells’ ‘The Time Machine,’ wherein the Time Traveller constructs a vehicle that defies the fabric of time, thanks to material properties that are yet to be harnessed or fully understood.

Another celebrated example is ‘The Difference Engine’ by William Gibson and Bruce Sterling, where the invention of a more advanced form of steam engine, combined with mechanical and digital computing, changes the course of history. Material science plays an important part here, as it would entail understanding the functionality of intricate gears, engines, and materials capable of supporting such technology.

In the film ‘Wild Wild West,’ the antagonist, Dr. Arliss Loveless, harnesses materials to fabricate impenetrable, artificial limbs, betraying no signs of their artificial nature. This scene alludes to the underlying theme of biocompatible materials that mimic the human body’s natural functions, a field of study in current material science that has potential applications in prosthetics and bionic implants.

The Marvelous Metamaterials

Metamaterials represent another domain where popular culture and material science join hands, boasting extraordinary properties owing to their artificially structured components. In fiction, they materialize as bouncing wave guides (perfect for cloaking devices), superbly efficient solar panels capable of revolutionizing energy harnessing, and artificial muscles that mimic natural progressions.

One could trace these concepts back to Michael Caine’s character, Dr. John Hammond, in the 1993 science fiction film, ‘Jurassic Park.’ He fantasizes about creating a world teeming with cloned dinosaurs with the aid of genetic engineering and his “reptile exhibitors.” Yet his creations are held captive and controlled by whatever material science we then possessed. Had he conjured the skill of metamaterials, his command over the prehistoric creatures might have expanded beyond imagination.

Similarly, ‘Iron Man’ Edgar Wright’s ‘Scott Pilgrim vs. the World’ collaborated with material science through the concept of the ’expon.bl’, a device capable of generating sound in any material without the need for air. This mechanistic riff echoes the underlying principles of acoustic metamaterials – fascinating structures that manipulate sound waves in an eerily life-like manner.

Faster, Harder, Stronger: Unyielding Materials in Films

In the world of action and sci-fi movies, one cannot overlook the prominence of unyielding materials, such as unbreakable glass, shapes-shifting metals, and super-strength alloys. Blockbuster films like ‘Inception’ have given rise to visually stimulating props like ’narwhals’ – ice picks embedded within the user’s gloves, devoid of handles – requiring unyielding materials that can withstand the force of being driven through human bone.

Superheroes on the big screen wouldn’t be right without their strong, resilient materials. The likes of ‘The Matrix,’ ‘Terminator 2: Judgment Day,’ and ‘Aliens’ have adeptly exploited the power of nanomaterials, polymers, and metals with exceptional strength, transparency, flexibility, and magnetic shielding.

A Unified Field Theory for Popular Culture and Material Science

It’s astonishing to witness the marriage of material science’s scientific credibility with the whimsy and invention found prolifically in popular culture. These congruencies between the realm of science and the sanctum of fiction unravel a truly engaging narrative – a unification of artistic expression with the pragmatics of material science. Each crossover warrants a keen scrutiny of the propulsion behind these fictional feasts, and the insights we derive might just reveal how truly boundless the applicability of material science is.

As we continue to push the frontiers of knowledge in the domain of material science, it’s intriguing to contemplate how these advances will further fertilize the minds of story-tellers, conjuring once-imagined marvels, concealed beneath the guise of fiction, into realms of reality.
