The Economic Impact of Material Science: Investment, Jobs, and Global Competitiveness


Material science is a fascinating field that continues to change the way we live, work, and interact with the world around us. With new materials being discovered and developed at an astonishing rate, material science is having an incredible effect on the global economy, creating new investment opportunities, increasing job growth, and helping countries become more competitive in a rapidly changing world.

Investment Surge in Material Science

Material science is considered an instrumental driving force behind technological innovation, as new materials enable breakthroughs in renewable technology, communication, manufacturing, and even medicine. Investors are taking notice, with research and development spending by material science companies hitting $147 billion globally in 2020. Major players in the industry include:

  • DowDuPont ($43 billion), which focuses on materials essential to everyday life
  • Mitsubishi Chemical Holdings ($23 billion) with operations in resin and plastic, electronic materials, and coal and basic chemicals
  • Solvay ($21 billion) specializing in performance materials, polymers and their components, and specialty polymers

The surge in investment will inevitably lead to improvements in existing products, the creation of entirely new industries, and a higher standard of living for people worldwide.

Job Creation

Material science is an engineers’ paradise, offering a broad range of diverse opportunities and fostering incredible growth in various industries. Each year, new discoveries and advancements could be the impetus for myriad job openings:

  • Manufacturing: The shift towards lightweight materials like carbon fiber and advanced composites can help reduce emissions, lower the weight of vehicles, and create more energy-efficient airplanes and trains. This leads to increased demand for skilled machine operators, engineers, and technicians.
  • Healthcare: Medical materials, such as biodegradable implants, prosthetics derived from 3D-printing and nanomaterials for targeted drug delivery, drive innovation and demand in the healthcare industry for biomedical engineers and research scientists.
  • Renewable Energy: Continued advancements in photovoltaic solar panels and high-strength, lightweight batteries for energy storage create opportunities in the renewable energy sector, including mechanical engineers, battery chemists, and research scientists.

Job growth in material science is not limited to the engineers and technicians, as sales, finance, and other support roles grow in response to industry expansion.

Global Competitiveness

Material science is increasingly a driver of global competitiveness. Countries at the forefront of material science research and development, such as the United States, China, Japan, and Germany, recognize the importance of fostering a dynamic materials-leaning economy. To stay ahead in this rapidly evolving sector, governments are investing in research institutions, providing funding for companies involved in the field, and giving tax incentives to make sure their homegrown talent remains competitive within the global market.

In particular, the United States boasts a distinctive advantage due to its high-skilled workforce, the perseverance of its industries, and research institutions collaborating to tackle challenging problems. Meanwhile, China has been rapidly expanding its material science sectors, investing heavily in R&D to boost its high-tech industries. Japan’s focus On increased strength and durability within materials has elevated its global standing in the field. Germany, often referred to as the “Land of Engineers,” remains a leader in chemical and automotive industries with a more efficient and production-focused approach.

In conclusion, the economic impact of material science transcends the realm of traditional boundaries, with the investment in cutting-edge research, the creation of new job opportunities, and the fostering of global competitiveness, providing a strong push towards a prosperous future. With continuous societal needs and challenges, material science acts as the enabler, driving the world towards sustainable and advanced solutions.
