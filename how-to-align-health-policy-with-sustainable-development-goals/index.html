How to Align Health Policy with Sustainable Development Goals


In recent years, the world has witnessed unprecedented progress in the field of healthcare, with significant improvements in life expectancy, reduction in infant mortality, and increase in access to healthcare services. However, these gains are being threatened by a growing number of health challenges, including the COVID-19 pandemic, increasing antimicrobial resistance, and unintended consequences of environmental degradation. To address these complex health challenges, policymakers must rethink their approaches to health policy and align them with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which aim to create a more just, sustainable, and equitable world. The Interconnectedness of Health and Sustainability The interdependence of health and sustainability was evident during the COVID-19 pandemic, when the virus spread rapidly due to factors like environmental degradation, climate change, and global connectivity. The pandemic also highlighted the importance of collaboration among governments, international organizations, and healthcare systems to respond to and mitigate the impact of health crises. These examples underscore the need to integrate health policy with broader development goals, such as the SDGs, which cover a range of issues including poverty reduction, climate action, and good governance. Understanding the Sustainable Development Goals The SDGs, adopted by world leaders in 2015, aim to achieve a wide range of objectives by 2030, including:

Eradicating poverty and hunger Ensuring quality education for all Promoting sustainable and equitable economic growth Implementing climate action and environmental sustainability Strengthening institutions and promoting good governance Ensuring universal access to healthcare Ensuring peaceful and inclusive societies

These goals recognize that health and well-being are intrinsically linked to economic and environmental development. Poor health and illness can act as significant poverty traps, reducing economic opportunity and perpetuating the intergenerational transmission of poverty. Conversely, investing in health can also drive economic development by increasing productivity, reducing absenteeism, and promoting healthier and more resilient populations. Aligning Health Policy with Sustainable Development Goals Policymakers must recognize and adapt to these interconnectedness in health and sustainability. To achieve this, we propose a three-pronged strategy:

  1. Integrate Health into All Policies

Health professionals will need to interface with policymakers across sectors, from finance and agriculture to education and urban planning, to align decisions that underpin deliverables across the twin latticework of UHC and the SDGs.
