Network Virtualization Deployment Strategies for Engineers


Network virtualization has gained considerable importance in modern-day network architecture. Incorporating virtualization technology brings about robust network operations, centralizes management, and reduces operational costs. Having evolved from physical networks, organizations are now seeking efficient deployment strategies to fully utilize their network virtualization capabilities. Here, we explore some deployment strategies that can make a difference in your network operations.

Strategy #1: Gradual Migration

A gradual migration approach is essential for large-scale enterprises, where a sudden change can significantly impact the business continuity. This method entails implementing new virtualized services while keeping legacy networks intact. This approach allows analysis of the network’s stability and performance before complete virtualization and minimizing network downtime.

Why choose this strategy?

  1. Risk Minimization: Gradual migration reduces the risk of unforeseen network outages or service disruptions.
  2. Validation: It provides you with a real-world validation of your network performance and stability in a virtualized environment, enabling you to make critical choices.
  3. Business Continuity: An essential aspect of network virtualization is business continuity assurance. Gradual migration helps maintain the business’s smooth operation.

Strategy #2: Multi-hypervisor Environment

Deploying a multi-hypervisor environment allows running VMware ESXi, Microsoft Hyper-V, and other hypervisors simultaneously. A multi-hypervisor solution empowers your business with flexibility and scalability in managing virtual network resources.

Why choose this strategy?

  1. Flexibility: Multi-hypervisor environments allow the freedom to choose the right hypervisor for each virtual machine, ensuring optimal resource utilization.
  2. Reduced Costs: They reduce the risk of vendor-lock-in and associated costs by providing multiple hypervisor options.
  3. Industry Standards: These environments adhere to industry standards like VMM (Virtual Machine Manager) and VHD (Virtual Hard Disk), promoting widespread interoperability.

Strategy #3: Hybrid IT Model

The hybrid IT model combines cloud and on-premises infrastructure, providing a flexible and cost-effective approach to infrastructure management. Incorporating network virtualization with a hybrid IT model allows efficient resource allocation and consumption.

Why choose this strategy?

  1. Flexibility: A hybrid approach allows utilizing cloud resources when necessary while retaining on-premises infrastructure resources for critical business processes.
  2. Disaster Recovery: It provides an effective disaster recovery solution with on-premises and cloud resources.
  3. Reduced Costs: With a hybrid approach, you can avoid costly over-provisioning or under-provisioning of resources.

Strategy #4: Automation and Orchestration

Automating network virtualization allows for centralized management, improving resource utilization and increasing operational efficiency. Automation and orchestration ensure that the network maintains optimal performance while reducing human error.

Why choose this strategy?

  1. Cost-efficiency: Automation reduces operational costs and ensures resource utilization optimization.
  2. Stability: It guarantees network stability by minimizing human error.
  3. Scalability: With automation and orchestration, your network can scale seamlessly, ensuring network agility.

In conclusion, deploying network virtualization comes with vast opportunities, provided you select the most appropriate deployment strategy for your business. Selecting the right strategy relies on your business’s unique needs, network infrastructure, and long-term goals. The above methods, when implemented correctly, can substantially improve network performance, ensuring efficiency, scalability, and cost-saving.
