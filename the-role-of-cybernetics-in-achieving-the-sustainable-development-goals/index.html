The Role of Cybernetics in Achieving the Sustainable Development Goals


Cybernetics, originating from the Greek word “cybernetikos,” meaning “skilled in steering” or “pertaining to the art of navigation,” has evolved significantly since its inception in the mid-20th century. Today, it encompasses an array of disciplines, including computer science, information theory, mathematics, and biology, among others. At its core, cybernetics studies how communication and the exchange of information influence living, thinking, and operating systems.

The sweet-spot between technology and human-centric aspirations is critical to consider, especially in the context of the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Launched in 2015, the SDGs create a comprehensive, interconnected roadmap for global development over the next three decades. Ranging from eliminating poverty to ensuring quality education for all, the SDGs aim to unmistakably leave no one behind.

In this context, cybernetics provides a pathway to achieving these broad global aspirations. By acting as a digital compass, guiding the navigation between technology and humanity, cybernetics can create innovative solutions to complex challenges. The web of interconnected objectives could be effectively managed using cybernetics principles, transforming the way in which the SDGs are fulfilled.

Cybernetics has a role in achieving the SDGs through the process of building intelligent systems. Cognitive systems, capable of learning, adapting, and communicating, could provide the much-needed personalized solutions addressing both global and individual challenges.

Firstly, Intelligent System Development can direct and encourage technological implementation for good. By investing in cutting-edge technologies, such as Artificial Intelligence (AI), machine learning, and robotics, we can optimize resource usage and speed up execution, thus facilitating the transformation to greener, sustainable economies.

Secondly, Cybernetics can enhance our understanding of complex systems, better addressing the Systemic or Interconnected nature of the SDGs. Cybernetics exemplifies the essence of interconnectedness through its communication concepts, suggesting that the exchange of information is paramount to the survival and adaptability of any system. This notion resonates with the SDGs, where the peaceful coexistence of humanity, the environment, and technology relies significantly on this notion of interconnectedness.

Thirdly, Cybernetics has the potential to provide Global Actors with better Decision Support. Understanding the interwoven nature of the SDG goals, the scheme is inherently complex and rapidly changing. Cybernetics, with its emphasis on an information-sharing and feedback system, can provide the much-needed clarity to streamlining decision-making processes for both government and private institutions.

Lastly, Cybernetics can enhance the way we monitor, track, and evaluate the success of the SDGs. The UN by itself cannot execute such a far-reaching plan. To monitor whether we’re on pace to achieve the SDGs by the deadline, a collaborative, informed approach to data analysis and data management on a global scale is vital. Cybernetics can lend its methodologies to these goals, creating robust systems of monitoring and evaluation leading us to a better-mapped path to the future.

While spectacular progress has already been made, ensuring the success of the SDGs remains a formidable challenge. Consequently, innovations in cybernetics offer unique solutions in navigating and managing human activities towards the collective endeavor of steering humanity to a better future. With capabilities that stretch from Artificial Intelligence to connected systems, cybernetics is and will remain a key player in the grand, global effort towards growth, sustainability, and progress.
