The Role of Personality in Negotiation and Persuasion


Personality plays a crucial role in various aspects of our lives, including negotiation and persuasion. Each individual possesses unique traits and tendencies that can significantly impact their effectiveness in these areas. In this blog post, we will explore how personality influences negotiation and persuasion, and how understanding these dynamics can lead to more successful outcomes.

Understanding Personality Types

To comprehend the role of personality in negotiation and persuasion, it is essential to familiarize ourselves with different personality types. While there are various personality frameworks, let’s focus on the widely recognized Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) as an example.

The MBTI identifies sixteen different personality types, each characterized by specific traits and preferences. For the purpose of this discussion, we will highlight two contrasting personality dimensions: extraversion-introversion and thinking-feeling.

  • Extraversion (E) vs. Introversion (I): Extraverts are outgoing, sociable, and energized by social interactions. Introverts, on the other hand, are more reserved, introspective, and prefer solitude.

  • Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F): Thinkers base their decisions on logical analysis and objective criteria, while feelers prioritize personal values and emotions when making choices.

Understanding these dimensions will help us delve into the impact of personality on negotiation and persuasion.

Influence on Negotiation

Negotiation involves a process of give-and-take, where parties aim to reach a mutually beneficial agreement. The role of personality becomes evident during the different stages of negotiation.

  1. Preparation: During the preparation phase, individuals with extraverted personalities may excel in gathering information and building relationships with the other party. Their outgoing nature allows them to establish rapport and establish a positive atmosphere for negotiation.

  2. Opening: Extraverts tend to be more comfortable with initiating conversations and expressing their thoughts openly. This can be advantageous in negotiation, as they can set the tone and direction of the discussion.

  3. Bargaining: Thinkers, with their logical approach, may excel in analyzing data and making rational arguments. They may focus on the facts and figures, presenting a compelling case for their position. Feelers, on the other hand, can appeal to emotions and build relationships based on trust and empathy.

  4. Closing: Extraverts may have an advantage in closing negotiations, as they are often skilled at bringing parties together and finding common ground. They can be persuasive in influencing the final outcome.

Impact on Persuasion

Persuasion, the art of influencing others’ thoughts and behaviors, is heavily influenced by personality. Here’s how different personality traits can impact persuasion:

  1. Communication Style: Extraverts may excel in persuasive communication due to their natural ability to engage and captivate an audience. They are skilled at conveying their message with enthusiasm, confidence, and energy. Introverts, on the other hand, may rely on their thoughtful and introspective nature to deliver well-reasoned arguments.

  2. Appeal to Emotions: Feelers, with their empathetic and compassionate nature, may have an advantage in appealing to others’ emotions. They can connect with individuals on a deeper level and evoke emotional responses that can lead to persuasion.

  3. Adaptability: Adapting to different audiences is crucial in persuasion. Thinkers, with their logical and analytical mindset, may struggle to connect with individuals who prioritize personal values and emotions. Feelers, on the other hand, can adapt their persuasive strategies based on the specific needs and preferences of their audience.

Harnessing Personality for Success

Understanding the role of personality in negotiation and persuasion allows us to capitalize on our strengths and adapt our approaches to different situations. Here are some tips to harness personality for success:

  • Self-awareness: Reflect on your own personality traits and preferences. Identify your strengths and weaknesses in negotiation and persuasion.

  • Flexibility: Be open to adapting your style to accommodate different personality types. Recognize that what works for one person may not work for another.

  • Active Listening: Regardless of personality, active listening is a crucial skill in negotiation and persuasion. Pay attention to verbal and non-verbal cues, and demonstrate genuine interest in the other person’s perspective.

  • Collaboration: Foster a collaborative mindset, aiming for win-win outcomes rather than a zero-sum game. This approach can be particularly effective in negotiation and persuasion.

In conclusion, personality plays a significant role in negotiation and persuasion. By understanding our own personality traits and recognizing the preferences of others, we can enhance our effectiveness in these areas. So, embrace your unique personality and leverage it to achieve successful negotiations and persuasive outcomes.
