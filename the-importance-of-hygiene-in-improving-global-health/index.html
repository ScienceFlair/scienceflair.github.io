The Importance of Hygiene in Improving Global Health


As human beings continue to extend the boundaries of technology, science, and medicine, it is staggering to think that one of the most basic human habits could be the most crucial aspect of improving global health: hygiene.

Wash Your Hands Often

Handwashing is typically overlooked as a hygiene ritual, yet it is one of the most scientifically proven techniques for reducing the spread of illnesses. Multiple studies show that proper and consistent hand washing can decrease illnesses from 400,000 to 1500 cases each year per 100,000 people. With populations’ increased awareness of viruses like COVID-19, this narrative rings truer today than ever before.

Hygiene and Children

Hygiene awareness begins at an early age, and forming positive habits from childhood can have far-reaching impacts in later life. Ensuring that children understand and practice proper hygiene techniques, including hand washing, oral hygiene, and bathing, can prevent the spread of many childhood diseases.

Environmental Hygiene

Although humans drive hygiene, it transcends the human experience. We often overlook the environmental hygiene standards, which can exponentially increase our vulnerability to diseases. Improper waste management, contaminated water supplies, inadequate sewage management, and other ecological degradation signify a less sanitary environment, which will inevitably lead to increased disease transmission, especially among vulnerable populations.

Clean Air, Clean Water

The air we breathe and the water we drink play pivotal roles in our overall health. Ensuring the cleanliness and safety of our air and water can significantly improve global health. Clean air and water provide a competent foundation for our immune systems to function optimally.

Improving Global Health Through Education

Education plays a massive role in identifying, understanding, and spreading awareness on hygiene practices. Infectious diseases such as cholera, tuberculosis, and syphilis enter populations when hygiene education lacks the emphasis it deserves. Educating individuals on proper hygiene practices helps mitigate the spread of these diseases and drastically improves global health.

Global Hygiene Initiatives

World organizations put forth substantial resources into hygiene enhancement programs, emphasizing solid hygiene practices in developing nations. Efforts such as the United Nations World Health Organization’s (UN-WHO) Initiative for Vaccine-Preventable Diseases, with programs that immunize children against vectors like polio, measles, and rubella, showcase the emphasis that can be placed on hygiene programs to save lives.

Through continued emphasis on hygiene practices, global health outlooks can improve immensely. Thorough hand washing, responsible air and water hygiene, and childhood education programs can mitigate the spread of disease and give nations a new lease of health. Conclusively, recognizing hygiene as vital in improving global health can remind us that sometimes, the solution to complex human problems resides in simple, albeit often overlooked, human habits.
