The Best Plants for Attracting Hummingbirds


A vibrant garden filled with the gentle hum of wings is a symphony of nature that many gardeners hope to create. Hummingbirds, with their iridescent feathers and darting flight patterns, are more than just delightful visitors. They are crucial pollinators, and attracting them to your garden can not only enhance its beauty but also promote biodiversity. One of the most effective ways to invite these tiny aerial acrobats is by growing the right plants. Below, we’ll explore the best plants for attracting hummingbirds and how to design a thriving hummingbird-friendly garden.

Why Do Plants Attract Hummingbirds?

Hummingbirds are naturally drawn to certain plants that provide their primary source of energy: nectar. These plants usually have specific characteristics, such as tubular flowers, bright colors (especially red, orange, and pink), and blooms that are rich in nectar. Additionally, some native plants are particularly effective at attracting local hummingbird species, as they have evolved together over time.

By selecting plants that meet these criteria, you’ll increase the chances of creating a magical space where hummingbirds feel at home. Beyond providing a food source, your garden can become a safe habitat, offering shade, protection, and sometimes nesting spots.

Top Flowering Plants to Attract Hummingbirds

1. Salvia

Salvia, or sage, is a hummingbird favorite thanks to its vibrant tubular blooms and continuous flowering throughout the season. There are many varieties of salvia, ranging in color from deep reds and brilliant purples to soft blues and whites. Varieties such as Scarlet Sage (Salvia coccinea) and Autumn Sage (Salvia greggii) are particularly attractive. Salvia thrives in sunny conditions, and its drought tolerance makes it an excellent low-maintenance plant choice.

2. Bee Balm (Monarda)

Bee balm is a classic hummingbird magnet. Its unique, spiky flower heads provide both beauty and nourishment. Available in shades of red, pink, purple, and white, bee balm is not only irresistible to hummingbirds but also to bees and butterflies. It grows well in full sun to partial shade and adds a splash of color to borders and flower beds.

3. Trumpet Vine (Campsis radicans)

True to its name, the trumpet vine features trumpet-shaped flowers in hues of orange, red, and yellow—perfectly crafted to accommodate a hummingbird’s long bill and tongue. While this plant can be a vigorous grower, with proper management, it can quickly transform trellises or fences into a spectacular display of color. The trumpet vine blooms in summer and provides continuous nectar during the warmer months.

4. Honeysuckle

The sweet scent of honeysuckle is an additional lure for hummingbirds beyond its tasty nectar. Varieties such as Coral Honeysuckle (Lonicera sempervirens) offer tubular blooms in shades of red and orange. Honeysuckle can be trained to grow along walls, fences, or arbors, adding vertical interest to your garden.

5. Cardinal Flower (Lobelia cardinalis)

The cardinal flower is a native North American plant with striking red blooms and a high nectar output, making it a gourmet treat for hummingbirds. It’s perfect for gardeners looking to add bold color to their space. Cardinal flowers prefer moist conditions and thrive near ponds, streams, or rain gardens.

Shrubs That Hummingbirds Adore

6. Butterfly Bush (Buddleia)

While often touted for its butterfly-attracting properties, the butterfly bush is equally popular with hummingbirds. Its arching clusters of flowers are full of nectar, and this shrub blooms continuously throughout the growing season. To control its tendency to spread in some regions, look for sterile or non-invasive varieties.

7. Azalea

Azaleas are spring-blooming shrubs that provide early-season nectar for migrating hummingbirds. Their funnel-shaped flowers come in a range of colors, from pale pink to bright orange to deep crimson. Native azalea species are particularly beneficial for attracting local hummingbirds.

Perennials and Annuals Hummingbirds Can’t Resist

8. Fuschia

Fuschia plants, with their delicate hanging flowers, are well-suited for hummingbirds, especially in shaded or semi-shaded areas. The red and purple color combination is a favorite, and their blooms persist through summer and fall.

9. Lantana

Known for its clusters of brightly colored flowers, lantana is a magnet for hummingbirds. This plant thrives in hot, sunny climates and is both heat- and drought-tolerant. It’s also a wonderful way to add tropical flair to your garden.

10. Petunias

An easy-to-grow favorite, petunias bloom in myriad colors and patterns, making them a versatile option for gardeners. Choose trumpet-shaped varieties and plant them in pots, hanging baskets, or flowerbeds to invite hummingbirds closer to outdoor seating areas.

Native Plants: Go Local for Success

Native plants are some of the best options for attracting hummingbirds because they are acclimated to the region’s climate and soils, requiring less maintenance. They also often supply the perfect ecological balance needed for local wildlife. Examples include:

  • Fireweed (Chamerion angustifolium)
  • California Fuchsia (Epilobium canum)
  • Red Columbine (Aquilegia canadensis)

When selecting native plants, research what grows naturally in your area and consult local gardening resources to ensure success.

Designing a Hummingbird-Friendly Garden

Simply planting the right flowers isn’t enough. The layout and design of your garden can also play a crucial role in attracting and retaining hummingbirds. Here are some essential tips:

Provide Continuous Blooms

Different plants have distinct flowering seasons. To keep your garden appealing throughout the year, incorporate a mix of plants with staggered bloom times. This ensures a consistent supply of nectar from spring to fall.

Plant in Clusters

Hummingbirds are more likely to notice clusters of flowers than scattered blooms. Group three or more of the same plant species together to create splashes of color that will draw their attention.

Offer Shelter

In addition to food, hummingbirds need safe spaces for perching and nesting. Incorporate small trees, shrubs, or even hummingbird-specific nesting boxes to provide shelter close to feeding areas.

Avoid Pesticides

Pesticides can contaminate the nectar hummingbirds consume and harm the insects they rely on for protein. Maintain a pesticide-free garden to ensure the health and safety of these tiny visitors.

Beyond Plants: Extra Tips for Success

While plants are the foundation of a hummingbird-friendly garden, you can take additional steps to enhance the habitat:

  1. Install Feeders: A feeder filled with a homemade sugar-water solution (1 part sugar to 4 parts water) can supplement natural nectar sources.
  2. Provide Water: Hummingbirds love shallow fountains, misters, or even birdbaths with gently moving water where they can drink and bathe.
  3. Encourage Insects: Hummingbirds eat small insects and spiders, so a garden rich in biodiversity will naturally support their diet.

Conclusion

Attracting hummingbirds to your garden starts with selecting the right plants. Vibrant, nectar-rich flowers, combined with thoughtful garden design, can transform your outdoor space into a haven for these fascinating creatures. By incorporating a variety of flowering shrubs, perennials, and native plants, and by providing an inviting habitat, you’ll ensure your garden becomes a favorite stopover for hummingbirds year after year. With a bit of planning and care, you can experience the joy of watching these mesmerizing birds up close as they flit from bloom to bloom in your very own backyard.
