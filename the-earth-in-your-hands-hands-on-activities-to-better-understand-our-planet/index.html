The Earth in your hands: Hands-on activities to better understand our planet


As we all grow up, we learn about the world around us. However, the true grasp of the Earth and its complexities often feels elusive. It’s one thing to read about the continents, the layers of the Earth’s crust, and the diverse ecosystems that blanket our planet. However, the deeper understanding comes from direct experience, from “hands-on” activities that connect us physically to the world that we share. Let us explore some activities you can engage in to truly grasp the nature of our home.

Building a Model Earth

Creating a model Earth is an enjoyable and fulfilling undertaking. With some simple materials such as a styrofoam ball, paints, and a world map at your disposal, you can set about creating your personal representation of the Earth. Painting and detailing the continents, oceans, and even the geographical features can provide a very tactile understanding of our planet. As a bonus, you can have fun customizing it for different countries, climates, and terrains.

Exploring with Map Games

Finding an old-fashioned map and using it to navigate to your favorite destinations can bring a sense of adventure, while helping you understand the map layouts of various geographic areas. You can also create a treasure hunt using clues spread across a map, giving you a sense of geolocation within a familiar indoor space.

Creating Your Own Geological Layers

The Earth is made up of layers, each with its own properties, special features, and effects on our planet. Kids can discover these layers by sampling materials and placing them in a container in the order they would appear in real life. They can further enhance their understanding by labeling each layer and drawing how the layers might look inside the Earth.

Eco-Friendly Activities

Through planting a simple garden or maintaining a vegetarian household, we can support our environment and further understand the interconnectedness between us and the Earth. Experimenting with composting and taking part in recycling initiatives can help children learn about sustainability and the importance of preserving our planet.

Simulating Weather

home science experiment of creating their own weather system. Use heat sources such as lamps, and a variety of thermometers to conduct temperature experiments. Children can predict the weather based on the temperature settings, thereby getting a sense of weather patterns and how they work.

Weather Mosaics

Creating weather mosaics is a fun way to envision various weather conditions. Kids can choose to reproduce a sunny day, a rainy day, a snowfall, or any weather pattern they prefer. The mosaics can be created from different materials such as colored papers, aerosol paint, or clay. This activity also helps children learn about color, texture and effectively brings out the weather engineer in them.

As we grow knowledgeable about the Earth, we become more appreciative and responsible citizens. It’s exciting and empowering - even as adults - to learn about our planet in such tangible ways. Experience these hands-on activities and watch your understanding of the Earth evolve, as you deepen your bond and responsibility with nature. Remember to share your experiences with your friends and family to inspire others to join this experiential learning process.
