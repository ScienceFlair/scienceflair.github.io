The Ancient Chinese: Terracotta Army and the Great Wall


The ancient Chinese civilization is known for its rich history and remarkable achievements. Two of the most iconic symbols of this civilization are the Terracotta Army and the Great Wall. These ancient wonders continue to captivate the imagination of people worldwide, offering a glimpse into the grandeur and ingenuity of the ancient Chinese.

The Terracotta Army

The Terracotta Army is a collection of life-sized terracotta sculptures depicting the army of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. This incredible archaeological find was discovered in 1974 by local farmers in Xi’an, Shaanxi province. The army consists of thousands of soldiers, along with chariots and horses, all carefully crafted with remarkable attention to detail.

Each soldier in the Terracotta Army has unique facial features, hairstyles, and armor, reflecting the diversity of the ancient Chinese military. The sculptures were originally painted with bright colors, but over time, the paint has faded. Nevertheless, the intricate craftsmanship and realistic portrayal of the soldiers still leave visitors in awe.

The purpose of the Terracotta Army was to accompany the Emperor in the afterlife. Qin Shi Huang believed in the concept of an afterlife and wanted to ensure his protection and dominance even in death. The army was buried near his mausoleum, which remains a mystery yet to be fully explored.

The Great Wall

The Great Wall of China is a monumental feat of engineering and one of the most famous landmarks in the world. Stretching over 13,000 miles, this ancient defensive structure was built to protect the Chinese empire from invasions and raids from nomadic tribes.

Construction of the Great Wall began around the 7th century BC, but the most well-known sections were built during the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644 AD). The wall is made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, and it winds its way across mountains, deserts, and plains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

The Great Wall is not a single continuous structure but rather a series of walls, watchtowers, and fortifications. It served as a military defense system, allowing soldiers to patrol and send warning signals if enemies were approaching. The wall also facilitated trade and communication along the Silk Road, an ancient network of trade routes connecting China with the rest of the world.

Legacy and Significance

The Terracotta Army and the Great Wall are both UNESCO World Heritage sites, recognized for their historical and cultural significance. These marvels of ancient Chinese civilization attract millions of visitors each year, who come to marvel at the ingenuity, craftsmanship, and historical importance of these structures.

The Terracotta Army offers a unique insight into the military prowess and beliefs of the ancient Chinese, while the Great Wall stands as a testament to their determination and resourcefulness. Both sites are reminders of the rich heritage and cultural legacy that the ancient Chinese have left for future generations.

As we explore the history and wonder of the Terracotta Army and the Great Wall, we gain a deeper appreciation for the achievements of the ancient Chinese and the lasting impact they have had on our world. These remarkable structures continue to inspire awe and admiration, reminding us of the power of human ingenuity and the importance of preserving our cultural heritage.

So, if you ever find yourself in China, make sure to visit the Terracotta Army and the Great Wall. Immerse yourself in the history and marvel at the scale and beauty of these ancient wonders. It’s an experience you won’t soon forget.
