The Top 3 Most Common Cybersecurity Failures and How to Avoid Them


In today’s digital era, cyber threats are on the rise, with cybercriminals becoming more sophisticated and innovative. Over 6 billion records were exposed worldwide in 2019 alone, impacting millions of users. Unfortunately, many of these incidents could have been easily prevented with appropriate cybersecurity measures. In this blog post, we’ll explore the top three most common cybersecurity failures and share tips on how to avoid them.

  1. Failure to Update Software

One of the most prevalent cybersecurity issues is failing to update software and operating systems. Many people neglect updates or postpone them because they fear system interruptions, loss of data, or an awkward user experience. Meanwhile, not updating software can make your system highly vulnerable to attacks.

To avoid this common failure, follow these best practices:

a. Always keep your software, operating systems, and applications up to date with the latest patches and updates.

b. Set automatic updates to ensure timely installation of security patches. Remember that built-in settings offer the simplest and most reliable way to receive updates.

c. Change your passwords regularly, especially any time you suspect a data breach has occurred.

d. Anti-virus software should be installed to provide an additional layer of protection against malicious software.

  1. Insecure Passwords

Another common cybersecurity mistake is choosing weak and easy-to-guess passwords. Passwords are like keys to your digital life, and weak passwords can easily unlock your sensitive data and property to cybercriminals.

To create stronger passwords, consider the following suggestions:

a. Eliminate common words, phrases, or patterns in your passwords, such as “123456,” “password123,” or “letmein.”

b. Make sure your passwords are at least 12 characters long and contain a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

c. Utilize a password manager like LastPass, Dashlane, or 1Password to generate unique and complex passwords for each account.

d. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible. This adds an essential layer of security, making it more difficult for attackers to access your online accounts.

  1. Poor Email Hygiene

Email remains a popular, preferred medium for attackers to propagate malware and steal sensitive data. According to Verizon’s Data Breach Investigations Report, 94% of malware is delivered by email.

To protect against social engineering attacks, follow these best practices:

a. Be cautious about clicking links or downloading attachments in unsolicited emails. Always double-check the sender’s information and verify their request or link through a secondary communication channel before taking any action.

b. Implement spam filters on your email accounts, and learn how to identify phishing emails by watching out for suspicious sender addresses, urgent or threatening language, and cryptic attachments.

c. Keep your software current and use reputable security software to scan your emails for malicious content, malware, and viruses.

d. Train yourself and your team to recognize and avoid falling for social engineering tactics, like phishing scams, pretexting, baiting, and tailgating.

Conclusion

Cybersecurity is a shared responsibility. While individuals and companies must be proactive in protecting their data from potential cyber threats, anyone can inadvertently fall victim to a cyberattack. By acknowledging the three common cybersecurity failures mentioned in this blog post and implementing the suggested best practices, you’ll be well on your way to establishing and maintaining a secure digital environment. Remember, staying ahead of cyber threats requires continuous vigilance, ongoing training, and adapting to the ever-changing landscape of cyber security.
