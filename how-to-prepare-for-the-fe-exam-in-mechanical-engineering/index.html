How to Prepare for the FE Exam in Mechanical Engineering


The Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam is one of the first major milestones for aspiring engineers. It serves as a gateway for professional licensure, and for mechanical engineering students, it is an essential step toward building a successful career. Preparing for the FE exam can be a challenging yet rewarding experience, as it tests your fundamental knowledge in various engineering disciplines.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the steps necessary to effectively prepare for the FE exam in mechanical engineering. From understanding the exam format to organizing your study schedule and using the right resources, we’ll cover everything you need to know to ensure that you’re fully prepared for exam day.

What is the FE Exam?

The FE exam is a computer-based test designed to assess a candidate’s knowledge of fundamental engineering concepts. The exam is typically taken shortly after graduation from an engineering program and is the first step toward obtaining the Professional Engineer (PE) license. Mechanical engineering candidates are required to have a solid understanding of both general engineering principles and specialized knowledge in mechanical engineering.

The FE exam is divided into two sections:

  1. The Morning Section – This consists of questions covering general engineering topics, including mathematics, ethics, probability, and mechanics of materials.
  2. The Afternoon Section – This focuses on more specialized topics specific to mechanical engineering, such as thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, heat transfer, and mechanical design.

The exam is designed to test your ability to apply your engineering knowledge to real-world problems, so it is essential to thoroughly prepare for all the topics covered.

Step-by-Step Guide to Preparing for the FE Exam in Mechanical Engineering

Step 1: Understand the FE Exam Structure

Before diving into study materials, it’s crucial to have a clear understanding of the exam format. The FE exam is a computer-based test that consists of 110 multiple-choice questions. You have a total of 6 hours to complete the exam, divided as follows:

  • 5 hours and 20 minutes for answering the multiple-choice questions.
  • 40 minutes for a scheduled break.

The questions are divided into two sections:

  • Morning Section: General engineering topics (about 50 questions)
  • Afternoon Section: Mechanical engineering-specific topics (about 60 questions)

The morning section covers general topics, such as:

  • Mathematics
  • Engineering ethics
  • Statics and dynamics
  • Mechanics of materials
  • Probability and statistics

The afternoon section covers topics specific to mechanical engineering, including:

  • Thermodynamics
  • Fluid mechanics
  • Heat transfer
  • Mechanical design
  • Manufacturing processes
  • Materials science

Each section is weighted according to its difficulty, and a good score requires thorough preparation across all subjects.

Step 2: Obtain the Right Study Materials

Selecting the best study materials is a crucial part of your preparation process. For the FE exam, you’ll need resources that cover both the general engineering topics and the mechanical engineering topics.

FE Exam Review Books

There are a number of review books specifically designed for the FE exam in mechanical engineering. These books are comprehensive and include both practice problems and detailed explanations of concepts. Look for books that are up to date with the current exam format and syllabus.

Some popular FE exam preparation books include:

  • FE Mechanical Review Manual by Michael R. Lindeburg
  • FE Mechanical Engineering Exam Guide by Joseph A. L. Rencis

These books provide a good mix of theory, practice questions, and solutions, which will help solidify your understanding of key topics.

FE Practice Exams

Taking practice exams is an essential part of your preparation. Practice exams will help you familiarize yourself with the types of questions you will encounter, and they will also allow you to track your progress over time. Additionally, they help you practice time management during the exam.

Online platforms like NCEES (National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying) offer official FE practice exams. These practice exams are closely modeled after the actual FE exam and can provide you with a realistic exam experience.

Online Courses and Tutorials

If you prefer interactive learning, online courses can be very helpful. Many platforms offer study materials and video lectures for the FE exam, and some even provide personalized study plans. Websites like PPI2Pass and School of PE offer online courses that cover both general engineering and mechanical engineering-specific topics.

Online tutorials and video lessons can help explain complicated topics in a more digestible format, which is useful if you struggle with certain concepts. Look for courses that have practice questions integrated into the lessons.

Step 3: Create a Detailed Study Schedule

A study schedule is essential for staying on track as you prepare for the FE exam. The exam covers a wide range of topics, so it’s easy to become overwhelmed if you don’t organize your study time effectively. Here’s how you can create a study plan that works for you:

Evaluate Your Current Knowledge

Begin by assessing your strengths and weaknesses in the various topics. This will allow you to focus more on areas that need improvement. Spend more time on subjects you find challenging, but don’t neglect your stronger subjects. It’s important to have a balanced approach.

Set a Realistic Timeline

The time you need to prepare for the FE exam depends on how much knowledge you already have and how much time you can dedicate to studying each week. Ideally, you should allow at least 3 to 4 months of study time if you are preparing while still in school or working full-time.

If possible, set aside dedicated study time each day. Aiming for 2 to 3 hours of study each day can help you stay consistent. Plan your study sessions so that they cover both review material and practice questions.

Break Down the Topics

Break down your study schedule into specific subjects and topics. For instance, if you plan to study thermodynamics for one week, allocate specific days to studying concepts like the laws of thermodynamics, heat engines, and cycles. Then, the following week, move on to topics such as fluid mechanics.

It’s important to stick to your schedule, but be flexible enough to adjust if needed. If you find that a particular subject takes more time than anticipated, extend your study sessions for that topic.

Step 4: Use Effective Study Techniques

The effectiveness of your study time depends on the techniques you use. Below are some strategies that will help you study efficiently and retain information:

Active Learning

Rather than passively reading through your study materials, engage with the content by summarizing key concepts, writing notes, and solving practice problems. Active learning improves retention and helps you develop problem-solving skills, which are crucial for the FE exam.

Practice with Real Exam Questions

Make sure to complete as many practice problems as possible. Working through sample problems will give you insight into the types of questions on the exam and help you identify any gaps in your knowledge.

Use both textbook questions and practice exams to simulate real test conditions. Time yourself while completing practice problems to improve your time management skills.

Use Flashcards

Flashcards are an excellent tool for memorizing key formulas, definitions, and concepts. You can create your own flashcards or use pre-made ones available in various study resources. Review the flashcards regularly to reinforce your memory and recall.

Join Study Groups

Studying with peers can be highly beneficial, especially if you are preparing for a challenging exam like the FE. Join a study group where you can discuss complex topics and share different perspectives on solving problems. Teaching others can also solidify your understanding.

Step 5: Take Care of Your Health

While it’s important to focus on studying, don’t forget to take care of your physical and mental well-being during the preparation process. It’s easy to get burned out, so make sure to balance your study sessions with rest and relaxation.

Maintain a Healthy Routine

Eat nutritious meals, get enough sleep, and incorporate physical activity into your daily routine. Exercise has been shown to improve concentration and reduce stress, which can be beneficial as you prepare for the exam.

Stay Calm and Confident

The FE exam can be stressful, but staying calm is essential. Practice mindfulness techniques, such as deep breathing, to manage stress levels. Believe in your preparation and approach the exam with confidence.

Step 6: Review and Take the Exam

As the exam date approaches, review your study materials one final time. Focus on areas where you feel less confident, and complete additional practice questions.

On the day of the exam, make sure to arrive early, bring your ID, and have all the necessary materials ready. Be mindful of your time during the exam and don’t linger too long on difficult questions. If you’re unsure of an answer, move on and return to it later if time permits.

Final Thoughts

Preparing for the FE exam in mechanical engineering requires hard work, dedication, and a strategic approach. By understanding the exam structure, selecting the right study materials, sticking to a solid study plan, and practicing consistently, you can increase your chances of success. With the right preparation, you will be well-equipped to tackle the FE exam and take the first step toward becoming a licensed Professional Engineer.

Good luck with your preparation!
