10 Online Resources Every Epidemiologist Should Know


Epidemiology, the study of how diseases spread and affect populations, plays a critical role in public health. As an epidemiologist, staying informed with the latest data, methodologies, and resources is essential for conducting meaningful research and making informed decisions that can have a significant impact on public health outcomes. Thankfully, the internet provides a wealth of online resources that make it easier for epidemiologists to access key data, research tools, and networking opportunities. Whether you’re a seasoned professional or just starting your career in epidemiology, these 10 online resources are invaluable to enhancing your knowledge and practice.

1. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) - Data & Statistics

The CDC is one of the most authoritative sources of epidemiological data in the United States. It offers a wide range of online resources for epidemiologists, including public health datasets, research reports, and publications on various diseases and health conditions. The CDC’s Data & Statistics section is an essential resource for epidemiologists, providing access to health indicators, mortality data, surveillance data, and health disparity information. The CDC also offers detailed reports and guidelines for conducting epidemiological research and analysis.

Key Features:

  • Access to health data, disease trends, and surveillance systems
  • Disease-specific information, including outbreaks and prevention strategies
  • Research tools for understanding epidemiological patterns and causal relationships
  • Public health guidelines for data collection and analysis

2. World Health Organization (WHO) - Global Health Observatory

The WHO’s Global Health Observatory is a treasure trove of global health data, offering information on health policies, disease burden, and healthcare infrastructure worldwide. For epidemiologists working on global health issues, the WHO Global Health Observatory is an indispensable resource. It provides statistics and country-specific reports that can inform comparative analyses and research on health disparities and global health trends.

Key Features:

  • Global health data across a wide range of topics
  • Interactive dashboards to visualize health trends
  • Reports on emerging diseases and health priorities
  • Technical and methodological guidance for epidemiological studies

3. PubMed

PubMed, a resource from the National Library of Medicine (NLM), is one of the largest and most reliable databases for peer-reviewed research in the field of health and medicine. With millions of articles, studies, and reports on a variety of health topics, PubMed allows epidemiologists to stay up to date with the latest research findings, clinical trials, and systematic reviews. PubMed also provides links to full-text articles and additional resources for more in-depth exploration.

Key Features:

  • Access to millions of peer-reviewed research articles
  • Search filters for refining searches based on study types and publication dates
  • Regular updates on clinical trials, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses
  • Links to free full-text resources and related publications

4. The Cochrane Library

The Cochrane Library is an excellent resource for evidence-based epidemiology. It is known for its high-quality systematic reviews that summarize the best available evidence on health interventions. Cochrane’s database allows epidemiologists to find evidence on the effectiveness of public health interventions, making it easier to draw conclusions based on solid research evidence. The Cochrane Library is especially valuable for those working on public health policy, preventive medicine, and health intervention strategies.

Key Features:

  • High-quality systematic reviews and meta-analyses
  • Evidence on health interventions, treatments, and prevention strategies
  • Regularly updated to reflect new research findings
  • Provides resources for conducting systematic reviews

5. Google Scholar

Google Scholar is a freely accessible web search engine that indexes scholarly articles across various disciplines, including epidemiology. It allows epidemiologists to search for academic publications, conference papers, theses, books, and patents related to their area of expertise. The service also provides citation metrics, which can be useful for understanding the impact of specific research articles or authors in the field.

Key Features:

  • Easy access to scholarly publications across various fields
  • Citation tracking and metrics for assessing article impact
  • Alerts for new publications on specific topics or authors
  • Links to full-text versions of articles and research papers

6. Epidemiology Resources from the National Institutes of Health (NIH)

The NIH provides a wide range of online resources for epidemiologists, particularly those working in disease prevention and health promotion. The NIH’s various institutes, such as the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID) and the National Cancer Institute (NCI), offer valuable epidemiological data, research findings, and clinical trials that can inform epidemiological studies and public health strategies.

Key Features:

  • Data and publications from NIH-funded research on diseases
  • Access to clinical trials and study findings
  • Health policy guidelines based on current research
  • Research funding opportunities for epidemiologists

7. The Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results (SEER) Program

The SEER Program, managed by the National Cancer Institute, offers a comprehensive collection of data on cancer statistics in the United States. For epidemiologists focusing on cancer epidemiology, this resource is invaluable, providing detailed information on cancer incidence, survival rates, and mortality statistics. SEER also offers tools for conducting statistical analyses and modeling trends in cancer research.

Key Features:

  • Cancer incidence, survival, and mortality data for the United States
  • Interactive data visualization tools for analyzing trends
  • Access to long-term datasets on cancer epidemiology
  • Detailed information on cancer risk factors and demographics

8. Epi Info™

Developed by the CDC, Epi Info™ is a free software tool that provides epidemiologists with a user-friendly platform for designing surveys, conducting statistical analysis, and visualizing data. Epi Info™ allows users to easily collect data, analyze it using advanced statistical tools, and create reports and charts. The software is especially useful for those working in field epidemiology, outbreak investigations, or data collection in public health settings.

Key Features:

  • Data collection and survey design tools
  • Statistical analysis features for epidemiological data
  • Customizable reports and data visualizations
  • Open-source and available for free download

9. HealthData.gov

HealthData.gov is an open data platform that provides access to public health data collected by federal agencies in the United States. The website offers datasets on a wide range of topics, including healthcare quality, disease prevalence, and health services utilization. Epidemiologists can use HealthData.gov to find national, state, and local health data that can inform research, policy decisions, and public health interventions.

Key Features:

  • Access to public health datasets from U.S. government agencies
  • Data on healthcare delivery, outcomes, and health disparities
  • Interactive data tools for exploring health trends
  • Resources for understanding and utilizing public health data

10. OpenEpi

OpenEpi is an open-source web-based tool designed for epidemiological analysis and statistical calculations. It provides a range of statistical methods, including descriptive statistics, hypothesis testing, and survival analysis, among others. OpenEpi is especially useful for epidemiologists working in resource-limited settings where access to expensive software may not be available. It’s an excellent tool for quickly analyzing data and generating results for research studies.

Key Features:

  • Free, web-based statistical analysis tools
  • Easy-to-use interface for conducting basic and advanced analyses
  • Access to a variety of epidemiological statistical methods
  • Regular updates with new features and tools

Conclusion

Epidemiologists play a crucial role in understanding and combating health threats at local, national, and global levels. The resources listed above offer a wealth of data, research tools, and analysis platforms that can enhance an epidemiologist’s ability to conduct high-quality research, make informed decisions, and drive public health initiatives forward. Whether you’re conducting field investigations, analyzing trends in health data, or reviewing the latest scientific studies, these 10 online resources are invaluable tools for advancing the field of epidemiology and improving global health outcomes. Staying connected with these resources ensures that you have access to the most current information, tools, and data to make a meaningful impact on public health.
