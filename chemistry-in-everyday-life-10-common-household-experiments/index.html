Chemistry in Everyday Life: 10 Common Household Experiments


We often cherish our kitchens for their appetizing aromas and appetizing culinary creations. But let’s not forget that each ingredient possesses its secret chemical reactions, lending enchanting flavors and textures to our delicacies. Dive in and peel the layers of science from some everyday household items with these fascinating experiments.

  1. Baking Soda and Vinegar Volcanoes

Ah, the classic! Gather two cups of baking soda for your “volcano” base, and two tablespoons of vinegar for the eruptive catalyst. Shape the soda into a cone or mountain shape and surround it with a moat of water. Now, pour the vinegar into the moat to touch the baking soda mountain and watch your household puffer fish imitation in action!

  1. Lava Lamps

For a more mesmerizing spectacle, combine 1/4 cup of water, a drop of liquid dish soap, and a few drops of food coloring in a cup. Slowly add vegetable oil until it’s three-quarters full. Drop a bright-colored jelly bean or flat candy into the cup and watch the mesmerizing, slowly floating blobs of color.

  1. Penny Plunge

Display the pennies’ buoyancy and how dissolved salt affects it. Fill a 2-cup mezuzah with water, stir in a few tablespoons of salt, and notice how dissolved particles affect the density of water. Add a penny to this saline solution, and it’ll sink! Now, return it to the plain water to see it float again; cool, huh?

  1. Glowing Milk

Banish the spooky mood with this fascinating glow-in-the-dark science experiment. Pour a glass of milk, add a few drops of gel food coloring, and mix it thoroughly. Float a drop of bright green glow stick fluid onto the milk’s surface to marvel at its glowing mystery.

  1. Elephant Toothpaste

Create a foaming, volcanic eruption of “toothpaste” by activating the powerful enzyme, peroxidase in hydrogen peroxide. You need a 1/2 cup of hydrogen peroxide, two drops of dish soap, and a teaspoon of dental activator cream. Pour the mixture into a cup, and watch as yeast (the secret catalyst) activates the eruption in just minutes!

  1. Magic Coloring Eggs

Give the Easter bunny a run for its money with this creatively simple experiment. Boil a batch of eggs, then dye them with uncooked rice mixed with food coloring in a cup of water. Remove the eggs, blot dry with a paper towel, and rub paint brushes or cotton swabs to create “artistic” patterns that magically appear on the boiled eggs.

  1. Candy Chromatography

Separate artificial colorants using paper chromatography technique by dipping paper chromatography strips in water mixed with crushed candy and suspended particles. Dry the strips, then allow them to climb up a jar of water mixed with sugar. The resulting, separation pattern will beguile your senses.

  1. Iron and Lime Water

Peer into the world of rust by witnessing the chemical reaction between iron and lime water. Drop an iron nail onto a solution of lime water (dissolve half a teaspoon of calcium hydroxide in water), and watch it transform into the rusty brown ferric hydroxide.

  1. Oobleck

Participate in ‘Barefoot in the Oobleck’ experiment for the perfect sensory play. Mix two cups of cornstarch with a cup of water, creating a non-Newtonian fluid. It’ll look like flour, act like a liquid, but feel like a solid when pressed firmly. You’d love the diverse properties this unique compound possesses!

  1. Magnetic Slime

Combine one cup of glue, one cup of water, 1/2 cup of baking soda, 1/2 cup of contact solution containing boric acid, and two tablespoons of cornstarch in a bowl. Knead in small handfuls of iron filings to create magnetic slime that demonstrates the interplay between magnetism and chemistry almost magically!

The Chemistry of everyday life is an intriguing amalgamation that bridges our knowledge of the scientific world with a daily dose of captivating innovation. These experiments so easily conducted at home highlight the enigmatic interplay of chemicals in the mundane realm. Harness the ‘science’ in your kitchen and unleash the kid in you!

We hope these fun activities inspire both kids and adults alike to explore the mind-bending world of chemistry step by step. So, grab your kid’s goggles and aprons, and welcome the hands-on approach to chemistry in your homes. Remember, science is as fun and colorful as we make it!

Happy experimenting!
