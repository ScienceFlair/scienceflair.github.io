The Sounds of Silence: Understanding the Role of Music and Sound in Antiquity


usic and sound have been integral to human societies since the beginning of recorded history. While music occupies a prominent place in our understanding of antiquity, sound also holds a fascinating role in the lives of our ancestors. In this blog post, we will explore the sounds of silence and uncover the importance of music and sound in ancient societies.

Music in Ancient Societies

Music played a significant role in ancient cultures. From Homer’s epics to the courtly accomplishments of the Ming Dynasty, it provided a conduit for storytelling, ceremony, and self-expression. Understanding the role of music allows us to gain a deeper insight into these cultures.

In ancient Greece, music held an essential place in education and social life. Music theory was an integral part of the curriculum, and Plato even prescribed specific musical modes for various purposes like war and love. Greek tragedies, powered by music, were a form of catharsis for society, offering a platform for human emotions like joy, fear, and empathy.

Similarly, ancient Egypt revered music and deemed it a universal language. Hieroglyphics depict musicians using various instruments, and temples employed music to affirm divine presence and create a sacred atmosphere. Music was also vital for mourning rituals; bereaved Egyptians believed that music sokar—sending the spirit of the departed to join Osiris in the underworld.

Sound and the Sounds of Silence

Ancient societies didn’t just exploit music as a creative outlet; they were also captivated by the effects of silence. The Greeks discussed the absence of music—the sounds of silence—as a concept to evoke deep thought and contemplation.

In Plato’s “Republic,” he envisioned an ideal city where musicians would play solely to move listeners towards virtue. In this utopia, music would maintain a proper register, thus inspiring appropriate emotions. Consequently, silence would teach restraint and induce seriousness in the souls of citizens.

The ancient Chinese philosophy of Taoism also underscored the significance of silence. As in Plato’s Republic, the absence of sound served as a conduit for introspection. The Tao Te Ching mentions, “The sound of the earth is constant; the sound of the water is constant. Inside the earth and the water is transparency, and transparency is constant.”

The Role of Sound in Antiquity

Sounds of every kind held significance in antiquity. Rennaissance artist Leon Battista Alberti discussed how echoes could bring an abundant variety to language and make one’s voice resonate with the loves of long-dead movers and shakers. He observed that echoes provided a means to surpass the present reality and reach into the realm of the ancestral.

Sound continues to reverberate in our modern world, although often in ways not entirely appreciated. For instance, sound artists today study the sonic effects of natural landscapes, revealing the properties of locations and unlocking a profound auditory experience. We may not recognize these sounds as music per se, but the influence of antiquity still resonates in our understanding of sound.

Conclusion: Harmonizing with the Past

Today, we marvel at the ubiquity of technology, and it’s reasonable to wonder whether the sounds of silence will hold as much significance in the future. Still, understanding the importance of sound and music in antiquity helps us maintain a perspective on the role of silence in our lives.

The literature of the past, whether in the form of Greek tragedies, Taoist philosophy, or accounts of Egyptian rituals, reminds us of the acute awareness our ancestors had about the power of sound and the sounds of silence. As we continue to evolve as a society, it’s crucial to be aware that our earwitnesses to the past can still offer invaluable insights into life’s essential sounds and silences.

Through this understanding, we can harmonize with the past as we compose the future, ensuring that we never lose sight of the vital role sound and silence played in shaping the world we’ve inherited. Soon, we’ll be able to use this knowledge not just in enjoying and creating music but in recognizing that the power of sound resonates in aspects beyond traditional musical composition—perhaps ushering in a new era of awareness for our species.
