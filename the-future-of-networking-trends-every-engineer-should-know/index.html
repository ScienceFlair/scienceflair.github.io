The Future of Networking: Trends Every Engineer Should Know


Networking has evolved significantly since its inception, and the future holds even more exciting innovations. As a network engineer or enthusiast, it’s essential to stay ahead of the curve and understand these trends to excel in your field. This piece will explore emerging networking concepts and trends, delving into the SOC, NFV, and SD-WAN.

Software-Defined Networking (SDN)

SDN has become a game-changer in the realm of networking. It separates the network’s control plane from the data plane, enabling better management and configuration. SDN allows programmability and flexibility, making it easier to manage complex networks. Engineers should understand how to implement SDN, the benefits it provides, and the challenges that come with it.

Network Functions Virtualization (NFV)

NFV has revolutionized how network services work. By virtualizing network functions, the reliance on physical appliances and devices is reduced. This virtualization allows for newer options in deploying networks. NFV also lowers costs, increases agility, and provides scalability. Network engineers should be familiar with NFV, its benefits, and how to make it work.

Software-Defined via Wireless Access Points (SD-WAP)

SD-WAP brings SDN to Wi-Fi networks, allowing for greater control and management. With the ability to segment traffic, troubleshoot, and diagnose issues quickly, SD-WAP offers improved granularity and increased agility in wireless networks.

Multi-Cloud Networking

The shift to multi-cloud environments means that network engineers need to adapt. Ordinary cloud computing is expanding into multiple private and public cloud systems, creating a hybrid model. This management of multi-cloud networks demands a deeper understanding of cloud services and their implications.

The Emergence of the Smart Edge

The smart edge takes advantage of SD-WAN and NFV technologies, bringing services from the core to the edge of the network. This processing at the edge provides efficiencies and performance-enhancing capabilities, reducing bottlenecks and latency. This trend will underpin the way network engineers manage networks in the future.

Automation and Orchestration

Networking automation and orchestration go hand in hand. Automation involves configuring and managing networks programmatically, while orchestration synchronizes the various components of a network. Both are necessary for agility and efficiency. Engineers need to understand the principles of automation and orchestration, and be adept at implementing and managing them in their network.

Network Analytics and Monitoring

Analyzing data is becoming increasingly important in the realm of networking. By collecting, interpreting, and acting on the data, engineers help keep their networks efficient and secure. Network monitoring should be a priority for engineers, as it can help predict issues before they become significant problems. Additionally, network engineers should have a solid understanding of analytics and the tools used to harness network data for insights.

In conclusion, the networking landscape is continuously evolving, and staying current with emerging trends is crucial. As a network engineer, grasping the concepts of SDN, NFV, and SD-WAN is essential. Moreover, understanding multi-cloud environments, the smart edge, automation, and network monitoring and analytics is vital in the future of networking.

Keep learning and stay ahead of the curve – the future of networking relies on it.
