Solar Eclipse Chasing: Traveling to Witness Totality


Living on the East Coast of the United States, solar eclipses are an enchanting phenomenon that I’ve been fortunate to experience a few times in my life. The first time I witnessed a total solar eclipse, I didn’t realize the complexity and intricacy of this celestial ballet until I was well into my journey to view the phenomenon. Thus, I’ve dedicated this journey to understanding and exploring America’s Southwest and its beauty.

A solar eclipse occurs when the moon aligns itself directly between the Earth and the sun, causing a temporary blockage of sunlight on specific places on our planet. This magical event attracts solar eclipse chasers, a diverse group of people from all walks of life, with their unique adventures and experiences. These intrepid travelers view the event as an opportunity to explore the world and experience this rare event while learning about the cosmos, our place in the universe, and what we can do as individuals to appreciate these fleeting moments.

I set off on this adventure earlier this summer and started my journey at a meteorological observatory in New Mexico. One would think that witnessing the solar eclipse would be an awe-inspiring event that would garner attention globally. However, witnessing a total eclipse requires specialized equipment and a group that has dedicated themselves to this pursuit, hence my trek to New Mexico.

While at the observatory, I learned that the scientific community relies on solar eclipses to gain insights into the cosmos and the Earth’s atmosphere. They also use this phenomenon to study the behavior of the sun, our very life source, and its links to our planet’s weather.

Leaving the observatory, our intrepid group crossed the country towards Oregon. This leg of the journey was an exploration of the beauty and grandeur of America’s Southwest. We stopped along the banks of the Colorado River, witnessing majestic panoramas of towering red sandstone and natural bridges.

Finally, on the morning of the great eclipse, we set up on the barren, dry open land with my fellow eclipse enthusiasts, armed with specialized glasses and cameras. As the eclipse approached, we gathered in the collective experience, invoking the same peer pressure and shared anticipation typical of a New Year’s Eve countdown, except a few orders of magnitude more intense.

There, in the quiet expanse of a desolate field, silence fell upon us, broken only by the deep american voice of the local weatherman describing the phenomenon we were about to witness. Then, as the moon gradually slid into place, I could see a shadow falling across the landscape, turning day into twilight faster than any sunset I’ve ever experienced.

As totality set in, the atmosphere transcended any Earthly description. For six interminable minutes, we were witnesses to a revelation, to a show of colors we would rarely get to see again. The sun, transformed into a shimmering, fiery gem, glowing against a backdrop of pure darkness. Our cameras clicked incessantly, trying to document the incredible scene.

Though we’re all different, in one split moment we felt a shared unity - a tiny segment of the Earth’s population bearing witness to this incredible event. We recognized and accepted that, for each individual, our place in the world was just as critical as another’s. We reveled in this unique and beautiful experience that brought us together as a group and, in a small way, as a global community.

To this day, I still can’t fully describe my feelings toward the eclipse. However, it is my journey and the experiences I’ve had that drives me to continue exploring the world and experiencing the astronomique and the seemingly infinite beauty of our universe.
