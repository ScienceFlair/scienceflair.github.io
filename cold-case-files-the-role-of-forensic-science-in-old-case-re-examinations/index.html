Cold Case Files: The Role of Forensic Science in Old Case Re-examinations


You may have heard about cold case files and their fascinating re-examinations in the world of crime and forensic science. Over the years, advancements in technology and the meticulous pursuit of justice have led investigators to revisit some of the oldest unsolved cases. This article aims to unravel the role that forensic science plays in these resurrections of cold cases, along with some of the most famous cases illuminated by these modern advancements.

The Internet’s Help

In the digital age, the internet has become an invaluable resource for investigators. Decades-old analog data can now be converted into digital formats, making extensive databases search-able. Forums, online communities, and social media networks have become vital tools for sharing information and uncovering new leads within cold cases. With a click of a button, a piece of evidence that had been tucked away for years suddenly becomes accessible to anyone with a connection.

Enhanced DNA Analysis

In its early stages, forensic DNA analysis could take weeks to conclude with results, and it could only compare samples with known suspects who had already been processed into a database. Today, the improvements in genetic technology have allowed for mixture deconvolution – a process that can distinguish between multiple contributors’ DNA from a single evidentiary sample. Moreover, this technology can now analyze minute quantities of DNA and effectively compare them against the complete human genome database, identifying unknown suspects.

Advancements in Trace Evidence Analysis

Trace evidence often appears minuscule and inconspicuous yet can often be crucial in connecting or discarding potential suspects with a crime scene. The evolution of this forensic science has not only enhanced the ability to identify and compare trace materials with unprecedented precision, but it has given investigators new tools such as Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) - enabling the detailed analysis of materials at a microscopic scale.

Criminology and Profiling

Criminologists have devised complex theories to understand psychological motives and offender behaviors. With the advent of modern profiling techniques – such as the FBI’s Behavioral Analysis Unit – investigators now have the capacity to make educated connections between seemingly disparate pieces of evidence scattered across multiple cases. This profiling has been instrumental in cold case investigations, especially in identifying links between several cold cases in which a single criminal appears to be operating.

Autopsy Techniques and Technology

Autopsy procedures have drastically changed over the years, with the introduction of innovative diagnostic tools that can yield more information than ever before. These include techniques such as Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), advanced 3D imaging, Digital Radiography (DR), and more. The results pivot around precision, allowing investigators to reach better conclusions about the circumstances surrounding a death.

The Quest for Justice

Re-examining old cases involves not just the application of cutting-edge technology, but also the exercise of extensive, patient detective work. Advanced forensic techniques may garner new evidence that could lead to the conviction of the guilty party. However, sometimes, the truth buried in the cold case files remains elusive. Regardless of the outcome, the quest for justice endures against the testament of time, aided by the cat-like agility of modern forensic science.

Overall, the re-examination of cold cases has been a vivid depiction of mankind’s tireless chase after truth and its formidable execution through ingenious forensic science methodologies. While some cases may remain unsolved, the spirit of relentless enquiry and determination is an undying force that will continue to unravel, with the cooperation of forensic science, our pursuit of justice for victims of old crimes.
