Top Network Engineering Trends to Watch in 2021


Network engineering has been continuously evolving under the influence of emerging technologies, business demands, and shifting market trends. As we approach 2021, network engineers are expected to embrace these trends to remain relevant and maintain a competitive edge in the field. Let’s delve into the top network engineering trends to watch in 2021.

1. Software-Defined Networking (SDN)

SDN is all set to take the networking world by storm in 2021. Its core idea is the decoupling of the control plane from the data plane, enabling programmability and automation throughout the network. By using open APIs, network administrators can more easily customize and manage traffic flows. This trend will continue to gain momentum as enterprises emphasize the need for greater network flexibility, agility, and cost-saving measures.

2. Network Function Virtualization (NFV)

As we move closer to the adoption of 5G networks, the need for network function virtualization intensifies. NFV allows for the deployment and management of network functions on industry-standard servers running standard hardware and software platforms. This virtualization of network functions helps reduce costs, increase flexibility, and create opportunities for software-defined networking. Network engineers should familiarize themselves with NFV to assist in implementing this beneficial technology.

3. Virtualization of Networks

Virtualization is extending its reach into the realm of networking. With virtualization technology becoming cheaper and more efficient, it has become increasingly popular in the networking world. By replicating physical network devices into virtual machines, engineers can simplify management, save on expenses, and increase scalability. This trend signifies that virtual networks will continue to disrupt traditional network configurations.

4. Expanding Usage of Hybrid Networks

Hybrid networks integrate both legacy systems and modern technologies such as SDN and NFV. They provide a seamless transition from the existing setup to the software-defined world of network engineering. In 2021, network engineers will beWatch diluting hybrid networks to blend multiple technologies and ensure a smooth migration to a more advanced network strategy.

5. Increased Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI technologies are becoming increasingly prevalent in the networking industry, forming an integral part of modern networks. Network engineers should keep an eye on the integration of AI to optimize network performance, equality, and data analysis. AI algorithms can be utilized for predicting network congestion, detecting anomalies, and managing security threats.

6. Emergence of Edge Networks

Edge networks are the future of the Internet, as they promise faster service, lower latency, and utmost reliability. These compact networks will empower engineers to manage and control traffic directly from the edge, cutting through the problems associated with traditional centralized network management. As we move into 2021, engineers should prepare for edge networks to take center stage.

7. 5G Transformation

5G will dramatically change the network engineering landscape in 2021. With incredible possibilities, like up to 20 Gbps speeds and an 99.99% availability, this transformation will usher in new applications and services such as the Internet of Things (IoT) and autonomous vehicles. Engineers must be proactive in preparing for this mighty transformation.

The trends mentioned above demonstrate how rapidly the network engineering landscape is evolving. As we step into 2021, network engineers must be ready to embrace these changes to stay ahead in the game. By staying informed and adapting to the latest trends, they can, ultimately, support the significant digital transformation that awaits us.
