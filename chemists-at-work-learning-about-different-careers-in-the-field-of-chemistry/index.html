Chemists at Work: Learning About Different Careers in the Field of Chemistry


Chemistry, the study of matter and its interactions, has broad applications and numerous career opportunities. From developing innovative pharmaceuticals to analyzing environmental samples, chemists play a crucial role in modern society. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the diverse careers available for those with a passion for chemistry.

Analytical Chemists

Analytical chemists focus on the identification, separation, and quantification of the elements and compounds in a sample. They use advanced tools such as mass spectrometers, and chromatography systems to analyze chemical structures, reactions, and properties. Medical, forensic, and environmental fields rely heavily on analytical chemists. A prime example is the use of Liquid Chromatography-MS (LC-MS) to detect, identify, and quantify drugs and their metabolites in biological samples.

Inorganic Chemists

Inorganic chemists study the properties, reactivity, and synthesis of elements, compounds, and minerals. They research areas like materials science, coordination chemistry, geochemistry, and bioinorganic chemistry. Those working in the aerospace industry might develop alloys for spacecraft components, or a metallurgist may research ways to strengthen steel. Some inorganic chemists work in sustainable energy research, developing new materials for capturing CO2 or harnessing solar power.

Organic Chemists

Organic chemists analyze the composition, reactions, and synthesis of Carbon-based compounds. They might work in pharmaceuticals, designing new drugs for treating illnesses; food science, developing healthier, more sustainable products; or materials science, creating novel polymers and plastics. Famously, Friedrich Wöhler synthesized urea from inorganic starting materials in 1828, kickstarting the field of organic chemistry.

Physical Chemists

Comparatively, physical chemists examine the fundamental properties of chemical systems at a molecular level. They can study thermodynamics, kinetics, spectroscopy, and electrophysics, among other subjects. Physical chemists contribute to numerous fields, including the study of green chemistry, heterogeneous catalysis for fuel cells, and nano-materials research.

Polymer Chemists

Polymer chemists specialize in the synthesis, properties, and applications of polymers. They design materials ranging from common plastics to biocompatible substances for medical treatments. This field is rapidly growing, as the demand for sustainable and biodegradable materials increases. An example of this kind of research is the development of new biopolymers derived from plant cellulose, as eco-friendly alternatives to petroleum-based plastic.

Industrial Chemists

Industrial chemists work in manufacturing, ensuring products meet the required specifications and are produced cost-effectively. They perform research and development, quality control, and troubleshooting on production systems, and collaborate closely with engineers and other professionals. The work of an industrial chemist might involve optimizing the production of fertilizers, additives, or other chemical compounds.

Forensic Chemists

Forensic chemists investigate crime scenes, contributing crucial evidence for the legal system. They may use their knowledge of chemical methods, tools, and techniques to analyze trace evidence, explosive residues, or drug samples. Their work often requires expert testimony in court, making excellent communication skills an asset for professionals in this area.

Environmental Chemists

Environmental chemists study how chemical compounds affect the environment and human health, and they work toward finding solutions for pollution and environmental degradation. Examples of work in this field include developing methods for removing contaminants from water or evaluating the impact of chemical emissions on global warming.

In conclusion, the field of chemistry offers a multitude of fascinating and indispensable career paths. Chemists contribute to the development of new materials, medicines, energy sources, and environmental solutions. By exploring these diverse career opportunities, we gain a deeper appreciation for the importance of chemistry in our everyday lives. So if you’ve ever been curious about the endless possibilities that await in the world of chemistry, now is your chance to become a chemist at work.
