Industrial Engineering: Quality Function Deployment


Quality Function Deployment (QFD) is a powerful tool used in Industrial Engineering to transform customer requirements into the design and development of a product or service. It is a systematic approach that helps ensure customer satisfaction by aligning various functions within a company to meet customer needs effectively.

What is Quality Function Deployment?

Quality Function Deployment is a methodology that originated in Japan in the 1960s and has since gained popularity worldwide. It is often referred to as “House of Quality” due to its distinctive matrix-like structure. QFD acts as a bridge between customer desires and the company’s ability to fulfill those desires.

The main objective of QFD is to convert customer needs into specific technical requirements and ensure that these requirements are met throughout the product development process. By considering customer expectations right from the beginning, companies can develop products that truly satisfy their target market.

How Does Quality Function Deployment Work?

QFD involves a series of steps that guide engineers and product developers in understanding customer needs and translating them into actionable design and development requirements. Let’s take a closer look at these steps:

Step 1: Voice of the Customer (VOC)

The first step in QFD is to gather the Voice of the Customer (VOC). This involves conducting surveys, interviews, and focus groups to understand customer preferences, expectations, and pain points. The information collected during this phase forms the basis for the subsequent steps.

Step 2: House of Quality

The House of Quality is a matrix that visually represents the relationships between customer requirements and the technical requirements necessary to fulfill those requirements. It serves as a communication tool that links customer needs to design and manufacturing functions within a company.

Step 3: Technical Requirements

In this step, the technical requirements necessary to fulfill the customer’s desires are identified. These requirements are often specific to the industry or product being developed. For example, if the customer desires a smartphone with a longer battery life, the technical requirement could be a higher capacity battery or energy-efficient components.

Step 4: Competitive Assessment

Competitive assessment involves analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of the company’s competitors. This helps identify areas where the company can differentiate itself and gain a competitive advantage. By understanding what competitors are doing well and where they fall short, companies can position themselves strategically in the market.

Step 5: Product Development

Once the technical requirements and competitive assessment are complete, companies can begin the product development process. This involves designing, prototyping, and testing the product to ensure it meets the identified customer needs and technical requirements.

Step 6: Continuous Improvement

Quality Function Deployment is not a one-time process. It is an ongoing effort to continuously improve products and services based on customer feedback and changing market trends. By monitoring customer satisfaction and addressing any issues that arise, companies can stay ahead of the competition and maintain customer loyalty.

Benefits of Quality Function Deployment

Quality Function Deployment offers several benefits to companies that implement it effectively:

  • Enhanced customer satisfaction: By understanding and meeting customer needs, companies can deliver products that exceed customer expectations, resulting in higher satisfaction levels.

  • Reduced development time and cost: QFD helps streamline the product development process by providing a clear roadmap and eliminating unnecessary steps. This leads to faster time to market and cost savings.

  • Improved communication and collaboration: QFD encourages cross-functional collaboration by bringing together different departments within a company to work towards a common goal. This improves communication and ensures everyone is aligned with the customer’s needs.

  • Competitive advantage: By consistently delivering high-quality products that meet customer expectations, companies can gain a competitive advantage in the market. QFD helps identify areas where the company can differentiate itself from competitors.

  • Continuous improvement: QFD promotes a culture of continuous improvement by encouraging companies to listen to customer feedback and make necessary adjustments. This allows companies to evolve with changing market demands and stay ahead of the competition.

In conclusion, Quality Function Deployment is a valuable tool for Industrial Engineers to ensure customer satisfaction and develop products that meet customer needs effectively. By systematically translating customer requirements into design and development requirements, companies can enhance customer satisfaction, reduce development time and cost, and gain a competitive advantage in the market. Implementing QFD can lead to improved communication, collaboration, and continuous improvement within an organization.
