Top 5 Fast-Growing Hedge Plants


When it comes to creating a lush, green barrier around your property or adding a touch of elegance and structure to your outdoor space, hedge plants are a timeless solution. They offer privacy, reduce noise, shield against wind, and can even increase property value. The best part? Choosing fast-growing hedge plants means you won’t have to wait decades for your landscape vision to come to life.

To help you make an informed choice, here’s a list of the top five fast-growing hedge plants that perfectly balance beauty, ease of care, and rapid growth. Whether you’re looking for height, density, or year-round greenery, these plants are guaranteed to deliver.

1. Leyland Cypress (Cupressus × leylandii)

If you’re searching for a hedging plant that grows at lightning speed, the Leyland Cypress is hard to beat. With its ability to put on as much as three to four feet of growth per year, it’s a favorite among homeowners and landscapers for creating tall, dense privacy screens in minimal time.

Characteristics and Growth:
The Leyland Cypress features soft, feathery foliage in rich shades of green. It is an evergreen conifer, which means it maintains its dense foliage all year round. This makes it a top choice for those looking for uninterrupted privacy and wind protection. Leyland Cypresses can grow up to 50 feet tall if left untrimmed, but with regular pruning, they can be shaped into a formal hedge at your desired height.

Care Tips:
These plants thrive in well-draining soil and a location with full sun to partial shade. They are fairly low maintenance once established, although regular pruning is necessary to keep their fast growth in check. Additionally, they are resistant to most pests and diseases, making them a hardy choice for various climates.

2. Privet (Ligustrum spp.)

Privet hedges have been a staple of classic landscapes for centuries, and for good reason—they are fast-growing, versatile, and easy to manage. With significant growth potential, privets are perfect for creating dense hedges ready to transform your garden into a green sanctuary.

Characteristics and Growth:
Privet plants can grow up to two to three feet annually, depending on the species. Their small, glossy leaves grow tightly together, providing excellent privacy. Some species, like the evergreen privet, keep their foliage year-round, while others, like the common privet, may lose their leaves in winter in colder climates.

Care Tips:
Privets adapt to a wide range of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade. They respond exceptionally well to pruning and can be shaped into clean, geometric forms or allowed to grow more freely for a natural look. Privets can withstand harsh conditions, although periodic trimming will ensure they don’t become overgrown.

3. Bamboo (Clumping Varieties)

Bamboo is a unique and contemporary option for creating hedge-like screens in your garden. Fast-growing and eye-catching, clumping bamboo varieties are particularly popular because they are non-invasive and easier to manage than running bamboo.

Characteristics and Growth:
Clumping bamboo species like Bambusa textilis ‘Gracilis’ or Fargesia robusta can grow several feet per year under ideal conditions. They are prized for their upright growth habit and lush, feathery leaves. Unlike traditional hedge plants, bamboo adds a tropical and modern vibe to exterior spaces, perfect for those looking for something a little different.

Care Tips:
Bamboo thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to part shade. Regular watering and fertilizing will help maximize its growth rate. Although clumping varieties are not invasive, occasional thinning ensures that your bamboo hedge stays tidy. Additionally, their ability to thrive in containers makes them an excellent option for smaller yards or patios.

4. Red-Tipped Photinia (Photinia × fraseri)

For those who want a fast-growing hedge with a splash of color, the Red-Tipped Photinia is a standout choice. Not only does it create a dense hedge, but its vibrant red new growth adds visual interest to your garden.

Characteristics and Growth:
The Red-Tipped Photinia grows at an average rate of two to three feet per year. Its glossy green leaves are contrasted by bright red tips of new growth, providing a striking appearance especially in spring and early summer. Once mature, it can grow up to 10 to 15 feet tall, making it an excellent choice for privacy screens or windbreaks.

Care Tips:
This plant prefers full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil. Regular pruning will promote the colorful new growth and prevent it from becoming leggy. Photinia is relatively low maintenance once established and is resistant to drought, though occasional deep watering is recommended in hot weather.

5. Boxwood (Buxus spp.)

While boxwood is often associated with slow growth, certain varieties like Japanese Boxwood (Buxus microphylla var. japonica) grow much faster, making them ideal for hedges that establish quickly. Boxwood is a classic choice thanks to its compact growth habit and timeless aesthetic.

Characteristics and Growth:
Fast-growing boxwood species can achieve annual growth rates of 12-24 inches, allowing you to enjoy a dense hedge in just a few years. Boxwoods have small, leathery, green leaves that are evergreen, making them perfect for year-round greenery. Their dense foliage makes them an excellent choice for formal hedges or topiaries.

Care Tips:
Boxwood prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil and benefits from regular watering, particularly during droughts. It grows well in full sun or partial shade and responds beautifully to pruning, which helps maintain its shape and encourages dense new growth. To preserve its health, avoid planting boxwood in areas with poor air circulation, which can lead to fungal issues.

Choosing the Best Hedge Plant for Your Landscape

When selecting the ideal hedge plant, consider your climate, soil conditions, and the purpose of the hedge. Do you need a tall privacy screen, or are you looking for a more decorative element in your garden? Do you live in a warmer region, or are you dealing with freezing winters?

Additionally, take into account your maintenance preferences. While some plants require minimal care, others benefit from regular pruning to look their best. Ensuring the right conditions for growing and being realistic about the time you can dedicate to upkeep will help you make a choice that’s both practical and satisfying.

Benefits of Fast-Growing Hedge Plants

Fast-growing hedge plants offer multiple benefits beyond just quick results. They are an eco-friendly alternative to fencing, providing habitats for birds, pollinators, and other wildlife. Their ability to absorb noise and filter air makes them ideal for urban gardens. Beyond that, dense hedges can serve as natural windbreaks, creating a more pleasant outdoor space.

With the right selection and care, these fast-growing hedge plants can transform your garden into a vibrant oasis in no time, providing privacy, beauty, and environmental benefits for years to come.
