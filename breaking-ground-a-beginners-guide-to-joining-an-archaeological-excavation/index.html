Breaking Ground: A Beginner's Guide to Joining an Archaeological Excavation


Having a keen interest in history and unearthing the mysteries of the past, have you ever imagined participating in an archaeological excavation? Here’s a comprehensive guide to embarking on this unparalleled adventure and making a real difference in the world of archaeology.

Before Diving In: Preparing for Your Excavation Experience

Research Potential Excavations: Conduct a thorough search for possible archaeological projects worldwide. Online resources, like DigVentures and Heritrix, are excellent starting points for novice archaeologists. Identify projects that align with your interests and goals, and get in touch with project directors to inquire about opportunities.

Earn Your Stripes: Start by acquiring a basic understanding of archaeology through online courses, workshops, or community college classes. Learning the fundamentals of excavation, such as proper excavation techniques, documentation, and recording methodologies, will help you excel.

Develop Essential Skills: Embrace training in conservation, first aid, and safety procedures, given the rough and tumble nature of excavations. These skills, among others, empower you to contribute significantly to the team’s wellbeing and avoid potential mishaps on-site.

Navigating the Excavation Landscape: Once you’ve established your credentials, secure your spot on an archaeological project. You’ll likely begin as a volunteer, but your commitment and hard work can pave the way for paid opportunities or leadership positions in the future.

On Site: A Day in the Life of an Archaeologist

Arrive Early: Actively participating in team meetings, site reconnaissance, and plotting are vital to kickstarting your day effectively.

Digging In: Begin your work by defining and marking out your grid of squares. This systematic approach enables you to thoroughly examine each unit for artifacts and features.

Recording Findings: Accurately record your observations through meticulous documentation, including sketches, photographs, and narratives. This task is vital for preserving the historical context of your findings and informing future research.

Lifting the Veil: Analyzing the Artifacts

Understand the Importance of Context: Place your artifacts within their historical and cultural framework. Contextual analysis methods, like typology and periodization, aid in identifying patterns within your discoveries.

Working in a Team: Collaborate with fellow archaeologists to discuss findings, engage in dialogue about methodologies, and corroborate data for the most accurate analysis possible.

Advanced Skills Development: As you excel in excavation techniques and research methods, consider pursuing specialized fields, such as osteometry or zooarchaeology. Broadening your knowledge base fortifies your ability to deliver comprehensive insight into your archaeological discoveries.

Conclusion: The invaluable relationships, unparalleled experiences, and enriching discoveries you’ll make on an archaeological excavation will stay with you long after the dust settles. Breaking ground and deciphering the enigmatic layers of human history is a reality you’ll undoubtedly cherish.

Embrace the adventure and start your journey today as an archaeologist. Who knows where your groundbreaking endeavors might lead?
