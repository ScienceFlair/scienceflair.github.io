Top 10 Podcasts on Sustainability You Should Follow


In a world that’s increasingly aware of environmental challenges, sustainability has become a key focus for individuals, businesses, and governments around the world. Staying informed about the latest trends, innovative solutions, and inspiring stories is essential for building a more sustainable future. One of the easiest and most enjoyable ways to stay updated is by listening to podcasts. With their engaging format and expert insights, podcasts offer a wealth of knowledge on sustainability. Here’s a curated list of the top 10 sustainability podcasts you should explore.

1. Sustainababble

Sustainababble is a witty, insightful podcast that explores environmental issues in a way that’s both accessible and entertaining. Hosted by Dave and Ol, this podcast tackles complex topics like renewable energy, corporate responsibility, ocean conservation, and climate policy with humor and clarity. The hosts strike a balance between serious discussion and lighthearted banter, making it an enjoyable listen for newcomers and seasoned environmentalists alike.

Episodes delve into the challenges of balancing corporate greenwashing with genuine action, as well as the ethical dilemmas surrounding everyday choices. Sustainababble’s refreshing tone keeps listeners engaged while encouraging them to reflect critically on sustainability issues.

2. Outrage and Optimism

For those who enjoy thought-provoking conversations, Outrage and Optimism is a must-listen. This podcast is hosted by the team behind the historic Paris Agreement: Christiana Figueres, Tom Rivett-Carnac, and Paul Dickinson. The show explores various aspects of sustainability, from policy changes to grassroots activism, while balancing the anger over environmental injustices with hope for a brighter future.

With high-profile guests like Al Gore, Greta Thunberg, and business leaders, every episode provides valuable insights into what the future of sustainability could look like. The podcast’s unique blend of critical analysis and optimism makes it a perfect fit for listeners seeking inspiration and pragmatic solutions.

3. How to Save a Planet

Hosted by journalist Alex Blumberg and scientist/activist Dr. Ayana Elizabeth Johnson, How to Save a Planet is as much a call to action as it is an exploration of sustainability issues. The podcast addresses critical questions surrounding climate change and offers practical advice for making a tangible difference in your community.

What sets this podcast apart is its solutions-oriented approach. Episodes feature stories about individuals and organizations who are working to combat the climate crisis. It empowers listeners by showing how collective action and systemic change can create real impact in reversing environmental damage.

4. The Circular Economy Podcast

As sustainability increasingly intersects with innovation, The Circular Economy Podcast dives deep into the concept of a regenerative economy. Hosted by Catherine Weetman, this show focuses on how businesses and individuals can minimize waste, design durable products, and prioritize reuse.

The podcast features interviews with entrepreneurs, policymakers, and researchers discussing practical solutions and real-life examples of circular practices. If you’re intrigued by innovative models like sharing economies and closed-loop systems, this podcast provides substantial insights into the transformative potential of circularity.

5. Green Dreamer

Green Dreamer is a podcast that illuminates the interconnectedness of sustainability, social justice, and cultural resilience. Hosted by Kamea Chayne, the podcast has explored topics such as biodiversity loss, regenerative agriculture, ethical consumerism, and decolonizing the environmental movement.

Featuring environmental thought leaders and activists, each episode dives into nuances often overlooked in mainstream discussions. This podcast is especially valuable for those looking to engage with sustainability as a broader multidimensional movement.

6. The Big Switch

The Big Switch focuses on energy – specifically, the global transition from fossil fuels to cleaner, renewable alternatives. Hosted by Dr. Melissa Lott, this podcast breaks down the technical and policy aspects of energy systems in a way that’s easy to understand without oversimplifying the issues.

Listeners will gain a deeper understanding of how energy impacts every aspect of modern life and the changes that are needed to align our systems with a sustainable future. The podcast is rich with expert interviews and practical case studies, making it ideal for anyone curious about the intricate world of energy and climate solutions.

7. Talkin’ Trash

When it comes to hard-hitting environmental conversations, Talkin’ Trash doesn’t hold back. This podcast covers topics related to waste management, plastic pollution, and the true cost of consumption. Hosted by Leigh Lezark and Karen Kinney, the episodes are educational, honest, and surprisingly inspiring.

By highlighting innovative waste-reduction practices and success stories, the podcast challenges listeners to rethink their personal consumption habits and take small yet impactful steps toward sustainability.

8. A Sustainable Mind

For listeners who want to make a difference but don’t know where to start, A Sustainable Mind offers practical advice on how to live a greener life. Host Marjorie Alexander interviews environmental changemakers, including teachers, authors, business owners, and nonprofit leaders.

The podcast covers a wide range of topics, from zero-waste living to the role of technology in sustainability. Its approachable tone and actionable tips make it a fantastic resource for beginners and experts alike who want to deepen their environmental engagement.

9. Sustainable(ish)

Jen Gale hosts Sustainable(ish), a podcast that proves sustainability doesn’t have to be overwhelming or perfect. This practical and relatable show emphasizes small, incremental steps that everyone can take to become more eco-friendly.

Jen’s episodes cover everyday challenges like reducing plastic use, sustainable parenting, and ethical gift-giving. What makes this podcast stand out is its encouragement of imperfection – reminding listeners that every effort matters and that sustainability is about progress, not perfection.

10. Think: Sustainability

Think: Sustainability, produced by 2SER Radio, bridges science, culture, and policy in its exploration of environmental sustainability. This Australian-based podcast examines global issues through a regional lens, offering a mix of scientific rigor and cultural storytelling.

Covering subjects like water resource management, climate change adaptation, and urban innovation, the show equips listeners with a well-rounded perspective on how societies can integrate sustainable practices into governance, urban planning, and individual behavior.

Why These Podcasts Matter

Listening to sustainability podcasts is more than just an entertaining pastime; it’s an opportunity to deepen your knowledge and contribute to broader solutions. These podcasts offer diverse perspectives and actionable insights, whether you’re interested in grassroots activism, systemic reforms, or simply learning how to adopt more eco-friendly habits at home.

By following these top podcasts, you’ll expand your understanding of the pivotal role sustainability plays in our lives and gain the inspiration needed to be part of the solution. Each episode serves as a reminder that the environmental challenges we face today can be addressed through collaboration, innovation, and determination – one conversation at a time.

Start with any show on this list and dive into the stories that are shaping a greener, more sustainable future for everyone.
