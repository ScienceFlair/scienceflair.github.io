The Best Bird Watching Trails in National Forests


Bird watching is one of the most peaceful and rewarding outdoor activities. Whether you are an experienced birder or a novice, there’s something magical about observing birds in their natural habitat. National forests offer some of the best opportunities for bird watching, providing diverse ecosystems that attract a wide range of bird species. From majestic eagles soaring high to colorful warblers hopping through the trees, national forests across the country offer an unparalleled experience for bird enthusiasts.

In this post, we will explore some of the best bird watching trails in the U.S. national forests. These trails not only offer incredible bird watching opportunities but also allow you to connect with nature in a serene and enriching way.

1. Great Smoky Mountains National Park (North Carolina / Tennessee)

Trail: Clingmans Dome Trail

Great Smoky Mountains National Park, one of the most biodiverse parks in the United States, is home to over 200 species of birds. The park’s high elevations, misty valleys, and rich plant life create ideal habitats for various bird species.

Clingmans Dome is the highest point in the park, providing bird watchers with a stunning vantage point to see a variety of species, including warblers, thrushes, and hawks. The Clingmans Dome Trail is a paved path that leads to an observation tower at 6,643 feet, offering panoramic views of the park and beyond. On clear days, you can spot birds like the Black-capped Chickadee, Golden-crowned Kinglet, and the Pileated Woodpecker.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • Abundant species, including migratory birds during spring and fall.
  • High elevation offers views over a large area, making it easy to spot distant birds.
  • The trail is accessible and easy to navigate, suitable for bird watchers of all levels.

2. Yosemite National Park (California)

Trail: Mono Meadow Trail

Yosemite National Park is world-renowned for its dramatic landscapes, from towering granite cliffs to serene meadows and ancient forests. These diverse ecosystems support a wide range of bird species, making it a fantastic destination for bird watchers. The Mono Meadow Trail, a short 2-mile loop, offers a great introduction to birding in Yosemite. This trail winds through a variety of habitats, including meadows, forests, and wetlands, making it perfect for spotting both woodland and water birds.

Bird watchers can see a range of species, such as the Mountain Bluebird, Western Bluebird, and Clark’s Nutcracker. During the spring and summer months, you may also spot the Northern Flicker, and if you’re lucky, you might even glimpse a Peregrine Falcon soaring above the cliffs.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • The trail traverses a variety of habitats, attracting both waterfowl and forest birds.
  • Ideal for both beginners and experienced birders due to its moderate difficulty.
  • Exceptional bird watching opportunities, especially during migration seasons.

3. Everglades National Park (Florida)

Trail: Anhinga Trail

The Everglades, with its expansive wetlands, mangroves, and sawgrass marshes, offers some of the best bird watching in the United States. This UNESCO World Heritage site is a haven for both migratory and resident bird species. One of the best bird watching trails in the Everglades is the Anhinga Trail. This boardwalk trail is less than a mile long, but it offers some of the most up-close views of the diverse birdlife that calls the Everglades home.

The Anhinga Trail is named after the Anhinga, a striking bird with long, slender necks. Along the trail, birders can expect to see a range of species such as Roseate Spoonbills, Great Egrets, White Ibises, and even the elusive Wood Stork. The park is also a great place to spot waterfowl and raptors like Bald Eagles and Ospreys.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • The trail is short and easily accessible, ideal for families and beginner birders.
  • Offers close-up views of birds in their natural wetland habitat.
  • Known for being a prime location to spot unique species, especially during the winter months.

4. Redwood National and State Parks (California)

Trail: Tall Trees Grove Trail

The towering trees of Redwood National and State Parks create an awe-inspiring backdrop for bird watchers. The park is home to an impressive variety of bird species, many of which thrive in the dense, old-growth forests. One of the best trails for bird watching in the park is the Tall Trees Grove Trail. This relatively short 1.5-mile loop takes you deep into the heart of the redwood forest, where you’ll be surrounded by some of the tallest trees on the planet.

Bird enthusiasts can expect to see species such as the Peregrine Falcon, Northern Spotted Owl, and Red-breasted Sapsucker. The park is also a great place to spot woodpeckers and the occasional Swainson’s Thrush as they migrate through the region.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • The dense forest offers unique opportunities to see elusive and rare species.
  • Tall trees and a variety of habitats create diverse birding opportunities.
  • One of the best locations for spotting owls and other forest birds.

5. Shenandoah National Park (Virginia)

Trail: Skyline Drive and Limberlost Trail

Shenandoah National Park, located in the Blue Ridge Mountains, is famous for its scenic views, waterfalls, and rich biodiversity. With over 200 species of birds, it is a paradise for bird watchers. Skyline Drive, which runs through the park, provides access to several great bird watching trails, including the Limberlost Trail.

The Limberlost Trail is a 1.3-mile loop that is easily accessible and winds through a mixed hardwood forest. Here, bird watchers can spot a variety of species, including the Scarlet Tanager, Ovenbird, and Eastern Towhee. If you’re lucky, you might even see a pair of Bald Eagles or a soaring Red-tailed Hawk. The park also offers opportunities for bird watching along the more strenuous trails, where you can encounter species like the Black-throated Blue Warbler.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • The park’s high elevation and diverse habitats make it a great location for birding.
  • Skyline Drive gives easy access to multiple bird watching spots.
  • Great for both beginner and expert birders, with a range of difficulty levels on the trails.

6. Olympic National Park (Washington)

Trail: Hoh River Trail

Olympic National Park is a bird watcher’s dream, with its temperate rainforests, rugged coastline, and alpine peaks. The park’s diversity of ecosystems provides habitats for a variety of birds, making it one of the top bird watching destinations in the Pacific Northwest. The Hoh River Trail, located in the park’s temperate rainforest, is a favorite among birders. This trail takes you through lush, green forests where you can spot a variety of woodland species.

Common sightings include the Chestnut-backed Chickadee, Red-breasted Nuthatch, and the Western Tanager. The area is also a great place to see the Peregrine Falcon and various species of woodpeckers. As you venture deeper into the forest, you may also hear the distinctive calls of owls, including the Northern Pygmy Owl.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • The temperate rainforest provides an ideal environment for spotting a variety of birds.
  • Relatively short trail with easy access, great for families and beginners.
  • Abundant wildlife, with opportunities to see both common and rare species.

7. Acadia National Park (Maine)

Trail: Jordan Pond Path

Acadia National Park offers some of the most spectacular bird watching in the Northeast. Located on the coast of Maine, the park attracts both marine and forest species, making it a fantastic place for birding year-round. The Jordan Pond Path is a popular bird watching trail that offers beautiful views of the pond and surrounding forested areas. The trail is about 3.5 miles long and offers moderate difficulty, making it suitable for bird watchers of all levels.

Bird watchers along this trail can spot a wide variety of species, including Common Loons, Bald Eagles, and various species of warblers. The park is also known for its population of Peregrine Falcons, which nest on the cliffs overlooking the ocean.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • Coastal and forested habitats provide diverse birding opportunities.
  • The trail offers stunning views, making it a peaceful and scenic birding spot.
  • Great for spotting both resident and migratory bird species.

8. Sawtooth National Forest (Idaho)

Trail: Redfish Lake Trail

Sawtooth National Forest in Idaho is a hidden gem for bird watchers, offering rugged mountain landscapes and pristine lakes that are home to a variety of bird species. The Redfish Lake Trail is one of the most popular birding trails in the forest, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding peaks. The area is especially known for its bird diversity, including waterfowl, raptors, and songbirds.

Bird watchers can expect to see species such as the American Dipper, Bald Eagle, and Red-tailed Hawk. The nearby wetlands are perfect for spotting waterfowl like Canada Geese and Mallards. The trail also provides opportunities for spotting more elusive species like the Clark’s Nutcracker, a bird typically found at higher elevations.

Why it’s great for bird watching:

  • Diverse habitats, including lakes and wetlands, attract a wide variety of birds.
  • Scenic and peaceful trail offering excellent opportunities for bird photography.
  • A great place to spot both aquatic and forest species.

Conclusion

National forests offer some of the best bird watching opportunities in the United States, with diverse ecosystems that attract a variety of bird species. Whether you’re hiking through a temperate rainforest, climbing to a mountain peak, or strolling along a peaceful lakeside trail, there’s a bird watching experience waiting for you. The trails listed above provide some of the best opportunities to observe nature and immerse yourself in the beauty of the wild. Make sure to bring your binoculars and a good pair of hiking boots, and enjoy the breathtaking sights and sounds of the avian world.

So, if you’re looking for your next bird watching adventure, consider visiting one of these incredible national forests. With each trail offering a unique experience, you’ll be able to discover new species, explore breathtaking landscapes, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
