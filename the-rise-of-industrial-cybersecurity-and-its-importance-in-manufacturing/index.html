The Rise of Industrial Cybersecurity and its Importance in Manufacturing


Cybersecurity has become an increasingly critical component of modern manufacturing processes. As information technology (IT) and operational technology (OT) continue to converge, the risks associated with network vulnerabilities are on the rise. To maintain the efficiency, safety, and productivity of manufacturing facilities, industrial cybersecurity has emerged as a vital specialty focused on safeguarding these delicate systems. In this post, we’ll explore the significance of industrial cybersecurity, its challenges, best practices, and the future of the industry.

Setting the Stage for Industrial Cybersecurity

As the manufacturing landscape evolves, so do the associated threats. Traditionally, factories were isolated from other networks, making it more difficult for cybercriminals to infiltrate their systems. However, as more connected devices and systems enter the manufacturing environment, the risks of data breaches, system disruptions, and intellectual property theft increase exponentially.

In July 2020, the US Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) issued an emergency directive due to a potentially compromising software vulnerability. This vulnerability could have allowed intruders to blind optical character recognition systems and affect printing operations. This event highlighted the need for robust industrial cybersecurity to protect critical manufacturing infrastructure and equipment.

Importance of Industrial Cybersecurity in Manufacturing

Ensuring Product Safety

With the safety of factory workers paramount, industrial cybersecurity plays a crucial role in preventing accidents due to equipment malfunction or negligence. Properly securing manufacturing systems reduces the risks associated with system vulnerabilities, ensuring a safer working environment for employees.

Protecting Intellectual Property

Sensitive information, such as manufacturing blueprints and designs, is a valuable commodity in the modern manufacturing world. Protecting this information from malicious actors is crucial for ensuring a company’s competitiveness and maintaining its reputation as an industry leader.

Preserving Productivity

The financial consequences of cyberattacks on manufacturing processes can be staggering. Industrial cybersecurity mitigates these risks by safeguarding manufacturing systems and networks, allowing facilities to maintain productivity levels without downtime, loss of data, or potential assaults on intellectual property.

Preventing Damage to Brand Reputation

Reputational damage can have lasting consequences for a brand’s image and market position. Losing consumer trust can significantly impact sales, revenue, and, ultimately, the company’s financial wellbeing. Industrial cybersecurity safeguards against these risks by reducing the potential for cyberattacks that might harm a brand’s reputation.

Addressing Challenges in Industrial Cybersecurity

Manufacturing facilities face unique challenges when it comes to cybersecurity. First, industrial control systems (ICS) have been historically disparate, with different protocols and integration challenges. Second, cyber threats targeting industrial systems differ from traditional IT vulnerabilities. Industrial control systems often run on legacy systems, making them less compatible with contemporary cybersecurity solutions. Third, human error is a perpetual challenge, as bad actors regularly exploit unsuspecting employees to gain entry into manufacturing networks.

Best Practices for Industrial Cybersecurity

  1. Implement a Multi-Layered Approach: Deploy a mix of physical, administrative, and technical safeguards to maintain a strong defense against cyber threats.
  2. Regularly Update and Patch Systems: Ensuring that both hardware and software are updated with the latest patches and firmware can mitigate many potential vulnerabilities.
  3. Train Employees: Providing regular cybersecurity training for employees ensures they are aware of potential risks and threats.
  4. Implement Network Segmentation: Segreating networks into smaller, more secure subnets can help mitigate risks associated with network-wide exploits.
  5. Invest and Focus on OT Cybersecurity: Dedicated resources, attention, and budget allocation are vital to effectively securing manufacturing systems.

The Future of Industrial Cybersecurity

The industry’s future lies in the evolution and adaptation of current cybersecurity solutions to address the unique needs of the manufacturing environment. This will mean further advancements in:

ICS- and OT-specific Threat Intelligence

Pertinent threat intelligence can better equip industrial cybersecurity professionals to recognize and respond to incidents effectively.

The Integration of AI and Machine Learning (ML) for Threat Detection

Artificial intelligence and machine learning hold great potential for anticipating and detecting cyber threats in real-time, enabling quick responses to mitigate risk.

Continued Education and Collaboration

Collaboration between IT and OT professionals and ongoing education will help bridge knowledge gaps and ensure continuity in safeguarding manufacturing systems and networks.

As we continue to witness the technological advancements and convergence within the manufacturing industry, the need for industrial cybersecurity to stay one step ahead of threats will only grow. Embracing new technologies, best practices, and an ongoing commitment to mitigating risks will be key to maintaining a secure and robust manufacturing environment.
