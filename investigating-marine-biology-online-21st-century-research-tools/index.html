Investigating Marine Biology Online: 21st Century Research Tools


Marine biology is a fascinating field that studies the scintillating vastness of marine life and the intriguing environments that they inhabit. With vast tracts of secretive oceans enveloping our Earth, marine biology continues to open a Pandora’s Box of natural secrets ranging from ecological preservation concerns to advancing medical therapies. As students and researchers delve deeper into this alluring world, the traditional tools of investigation are gradually being digitalized and complemented by the online world.

For the contemporary researcher, an astounding range of digital tools and resources are available online for diving into the mesmerizing aquatic universe. Below, a comprehensive overview of these resources and tools is provided to enthuse budding marine biologists and those who want to remotely explore the mysteries held beneath the ocean’s surface.

1. NOAA’s Ocean Explorer

National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s Ocean Explorer (https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov/) is a portal that provides a plethora of resources related to marine exploration. The Ocean Explorer site not only displays real-time data from NOAA vessels but also offers hours of absorbing footage, educational resources, and wall-of-windows into ongoing expeditions.

2. Dive into NEON–The National Ecological Observatory Network

NEON (https://www.neondrive.org/) offers a platform of custom-built measurement tools and methodologies that allow students, educators, and researchers to access and interact with NEON’s train of cutting-edge, environmental data—ranging from underwater photographic surveys to aquatic sensors.

3. WikiSpecies: Marine Life

Wikispecies (https://species.wikimedia.org/wiki/Main_Page) is a renowned database of animal species, housing marine specimens aplenty. With a collaborative network of researchers, nature enthusiasts, and citizenscientists, WikiSpecies serves to identify and explain the taxonomical trees of marine creatures, offering education and insight into marine biology.

4. Marine Cbable

Marine CBCable (https://calacademy.org/ research/marine-cable) is a project spearheaded by the California Academy of Sciences in conjunction with Scripps Institution of Oceanography at the University of California San Diego. The platform compiles data from an international alliance of discovery tools, connecting the dots of various species and expanding the oceanographic atlas.

5. The Global Biotic Interactions (GloBI)

Attempting to provide a more comprehensive level to biodiversity data, GloBI (https://gli.to) is an online platform comprehensively pinpointing the interconnections between species. Incorporating marine organisms in the mix, GloBI is especially instrumental in understanding the dynamics of marine ecosystems.

6. Open Elsey Data

Open Elsey Data (https://openeoceansbiology.org/) is a portal showcasing innovative and open-source datasets from various marine expeditions. Following the philosophy of open science, Open Elsey Data promotes and diffuses scientific inquiry by audience interaction, interoperation, and community participation.

7. Deep Blue

Headquartered at the University of Miami’s Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science, Deep Blue (http://deepblue.miami.edu/) is a data portal that features any research worthy of the name. This web portal houses various reports, posters, theses, articles, maps, books, and a virtual library, opening avenues for an intense study session.

8. Google Arts & Culture

Collaborating with esteemed natural history museums like the Smithsonian and California Academy of Sciences, Google Arts and Culture (https://artsandculture.google.com/) offers a virtual goldmine of categorically ambiguous marine life.

9. Zoomarine 分子分子

Following the COVID-19 protocols, virtual zoos and aquariums have found their way into the horizon of marine biology study tools. Zoomarine 分子分子 (https://www.zoomarine.com.tw/zh_TW/) is one such example that offers an auditory and visual journey through its livestreamed aquatic spectacles.

To culminate, it is abundantly clear that the 21st century brings enhanced opportunities for exploring marine biology online. Thanks to the proliferation of digital platforms, tools, and resources, marine biology researchers now need not be restricted to the shores but can venture out into oceanic expanses from their desks, laptops, or mobile devices.

As the ocean’s realm remains partially uncharted, let us embrace these tools for uncovering its secrets and unravel the mysteries locked in the iridescent depths of the Earth’s seas. For the ocean is the ultimate undiscovered country, waiting to be explored and mapped by the tireless hands of marine biologists.
