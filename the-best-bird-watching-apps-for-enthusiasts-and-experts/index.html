The Best Bird Watching Apps for Enthusiasts and Experts


Bird watching is a thrilling hobby enjoyed by millions around the world. Whether you’re a casual bird enthusiast or a seasoned expert, technology has made it easier than ever to engage with this pastime. Today, bird watching apps provide a powerful tool for identifying species, tracking bird sightings, and learning more about our feathered friends. If you’re passionate about birds and looking to take your bird watching experience to the next level, you’ll find that the right app can make all the difference.

In this post, we’ll take a look at some of the best bird watching apps available to bird lovers of all levels, helping you choose the right one to suit your needs.

1. Merlin Bird ID

Overview:

Merlin Bird ID, developed by the Cornell Lab of Ornithology, is widely regarded as one of the best apps for bird identification. It is particularly well-suited for beginners and intermediate bird watchers, but even experts can benefit from its comprehensive database. Merlin uses artificial intelligence to assist users in identifying birds based on photos, sounds, or descriptions.

Key Features:

  • Photo Identification: Take a photo of a bird, and Merlin will suggest potential species based on the image.
  • Sound Recognition: Merlin can also help you identify birds based on their calls, a feature that’s invaluable for bird watchers who can’t always get a clear view of the bird.
  • Comprehensive Database: The app covers over 650 species in North America, with similar databases available for other regions, including Europe, Asia, and South America.
  • Smart Search: Merlin helps narrow down bird species based on location, size, color, and other physical features.
  • Free to Use: Merlin Bird ID is free, making it an accessible tool for everyone, from casual bird watchers to more serious birding enthusiasts.

Why It’s Great:

Merlin’s ease of use and ability to help you identify birds in real time make it a fantastic app for both beginners and those who want quick identification. Its integration with Cornell Lab’s bird research also ensures that you’re getting reliable and scientifically accurate data.

2. iBird Pro Guide to Birds

Overview:

For bird watching experts, iBird Pro is a comprehensive and feature-rich app that offers in-depth bird identification and tracking tools. It is designed for those who want to go beyond basic identification and explore more advanced features related to bird behavior, range maps, and migration patterns.

Key Features:

  • Extensive Database: iBird Pro boasts over 900 species of North American birds, including detailed information about each bird’s size, shape, habitat, and song.
  • High-Quality Images: The app includes thousands of detailed, high-quality images to help with bird identification.
  • Bird Songs: iBird Pro offers audio recordings of bird calls and songs, making it easier for users to identify birds by sound.
  • Migration Maps: The app shows migration maps to help bird watchers understand where to find specific species during different times of the year.
  • Offline Access: Once downloaded, iBird Pro can be used offline, making it perfect for bird watching in remote locations with limited cell service.

Why It’s Great:

iBird Pro is perfect for advanced bird watchers and experts. Its rich features, including migration maps and detailed bird descriptions, make it an invaluable resource for serious birders. The ability to work offline is also a major advantage for those exploring wilderness areas far from civilization.

3. BirdNet

Overview:

BirdNet is an innovative app developed by the Cornell Lab of Ornithology and Chemnitz University of Technology, focused on bird sound recognition. This app is ideal for bird watchers who want to identify birds based on their songs and calls. BirdNet uses advanced machine learning algorithms to analyze bird sounds and match them to a vast database of bird vocalizations.

Key Features:

  • Sound Recognition: BirdNet allows you to record bird songs and calls, which the app will analyze and match to the correct species.
  • Audio Library: It includes a large collection of bird calls from around the world, making it an excellent tool for both local and international bird watching.
  • Global Database: BirdNet covers thousands of bird species from around the globe, providing a wide variety of bird sounds to learn from.
  • Easy-to-Use Interface: The app is simple to navigate, making it easy to record and identify bird sounds in real time.

Why It’s Great:

For those who enjoy bird watching by sound, BirdNet is a must-have. Its ability to analyze bird calls and match them to the right species can enhance your birding experience and help you connect more deeply with the birds in your area.

4. Audubon Bird Guide

Overview:

The Audubon Bird Guide is a trusted resource for bird watchers across the United States. Developed by the National Audubon Society, this app is designed to help you identify birds, track sightings, and learn more about bird behavior and conservation.

Key Features:

  • Bird Identification: The Audubon Bird Guide includes over 800 species of birds, with detailed descriptions, images, and information on their habitats.
  • Bird Songs: The app provides audio recordings of bird songs and calls, helping you identify birds by sound.
  • Bird Watching Journal: The app includes a journal feature that allows you to track your bird sightings, including date, location, and species.
  • Conservation Information: Audubon is a conservation-focused organization, so the app also provides information on how you can help protect bird habitats and contribute to bird conservation efforts.
  • Offline Functionality: The app works offline, allowing you to use it in remote areas without internet access.

Why It’s Great:

The Audubon Bird Guide is a perfect choice for bird watchers who are passionate about conservation. It not only provides useful tools for identification and tracking but also supports efforts to protect bird populations and their habitats.

5. eBird

Overview:

eBird is another powerful app from the Cornell Lab of Ornithology. It’s primarily a bird tracking and reporting app, but it also includes identification tools. eBird is perfect for birders who want to keep a record of their bird sightings and contribute to global bird data collection.

Key Features:

  • Global Bird Sightings Database: eBird allows users to log their bird sightings and contribute to a global bird observation network. It includes sightings from bird watchers all over the world.
  • Customizable Lists: You can create lists of birds you’ve observed, add photos, and even share your sightings with other bird watchers.
  • Species Search: eBird includes a search function that allows you to look up detailed information about species, including their range, appearance, and behavior.
  • Alerts and Notifications: Set up alerts for specific bird species so you can be notified when someone reports a sighting in your area.
  • Offline Support: eBird works offline, allowing you to log sightings while in remote areas.

Why It’s Great:

eBird is the ultimate app for bird watchers who want to track their sightings and contribute to scientific research. Its massive global database and community-driven platform make it an invaluable tool for enthusiasts and experts alike.

6. Bird Watching Field Guide

Overview:

Bird Watching Field Guide is a well-rounded app for bird enthusiasts, offering identification, tracking, and educational resources in a single package. It’s designed for bird watchers who want a compact yet comprehensive guide to the birds in their region.

Key Features:

  • Local Bird Guide: The app helps you identify birds native to your specific region, providing detailed descriptions and high-quality images.
  • Bird Identification: Use the app’s search filters, including bird size, color, and location, to help identify species.
  • Bird Sounds: Many birds have distinctive calls, and the app includes recordings to help you familiarize yourself with their songs.
  • Tracking and Lists: Keep track of the birds you’ve seen and maintain your own personal bird list.
  • Educational Resources: The app provides additional information on bird migration, behavior, and more.

Why It’s Great:

Bird Watching Field Guide is a versatile app that provides the perfect balance of bird identification tools, tracking features, and educational content. It’s ideal for people who are new to bird watching and want an easy-to-use app with all the essential features.

7. Birds of the World

Overview:

Birds of the World is a high-quality app that provides detailed information about over 10,000 bird species worldwide. It’s the perfect resource for bird enthusiasts and experts who want in-depth knowledge about birds and their behaviors.

Key Features:

  • Global Bird Database: Over 10,000 bird species from all over the world are included, with detailed information about their range, habitat, and behavior.
  • Detailed Images and Sounds: The app features thousands of high-quality images and audio recordings of bird calls.
  • Scientific Information: Birds of the World is backed by rigorous scientific data, offering bird watchers a deeper understanding of species and their behaviors.
  • Bird Life History: Learn about the evolutionary history, ecology, and conservation of various bird species.

Why It’s Great:

If you’re an expert bird watcher or a professional ornithologist, Birds of the World is the app for you. Its comprehensive database and in-depth scientific content make it an invaluable resource for serious birding.

Conclusion

Technology has revolutionized bird watching, making it easier than ever for enthusiasts and experts to identify, track, and learn about birds. The apps listed above are some of the best tools available today, offering everything from identification guides and sound recognition to tracking and community-sharing features.

Whether you’re a casual bird watcher or a seasoned expert, there’s an app on this list that will enhance your birding experience and help you connect with the natural world in new and exciting ways. Happy bird watching!
