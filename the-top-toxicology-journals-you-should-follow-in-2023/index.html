The Top Toxicology Journals You Should Follow in 2023


Toxicology is a dynamic and ever-evolving field, straddling the intersections of biology, chemistry, pharmacology, and environmental science. In 2023, staying updated with the latest research, breakthroughs, and debates in toxicology is more important than ever. Whether you’re a researcher, practitioner, educator, or a student delving into this multidisciplinary field, following the most reputable toxicology journals is critical to staying ahead of emerging trends. These journals not only serve as a gateway to groundbreaking science but also foster a deeper understanding of how chemicals, drugs, and environmental agents affect living systems.

This post explores some of the leading toxicology journals worth your attention, highlighting why they are indispensable resources in the field. With insights from regulatory toxicology to environmental effects and molecular mechanisms, these journals provide a comprehensive view of the discipline. 1. Toxicological Sciences (Oxford University Press)

Toxicological Sciences is a flagship journal in the toxicology domain, widely regarded for its robust editorial standards and high-impact publications. As the official journal of the Society of Toxicology (SOT), it covers a broad spectrum of toxicology research, from basic science to applied toxicology.

Key highlights of this journal include cutting-edge studies on molecular and cellular toxicology, risk assessment, and novel methodologies for toxicological testing. Its peer-reviewed articles are known for their rigorous quality, making it an essential tool for researchers building on fundamental toxicological concepts or seeking practical solutions for industry challenges.

Recent topics featured include innovative work on the development of alternative non-animal testing methods, neurotoxicology research, and the complex interplay between environmental exposures and genetic susceptibility. Toxicological Sciences is an ideal choice for individuals seeking a balance of laboratory-based experiments and translational research.

2. Environmental Health Perspectives (EHP)

Operated by the National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences (NIEHS), Environmental Health Perspectives is a go-to journal for anyone investigating the intersection of environmental factors and human health. While this journal encompasses broader environmental science topics, it holds particular significance for toxicologists.

EHP stands out for its open-access model, ensuring that its high-quality research is accessible to a global audience. It frequently publishes pivotal studies examining the effects of pollutants, endocrine disruptors, and other potentially hazardous chemicals on populations. Systematic reviews, advancements in exposure science, and policy-relevant insights are regularly featured, offering a holistic approach to environmental toxicology.

From studies on air pollution toxicity to emerging contaminants like PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances), the journal frequently tackles the most pressing environmental health issues of our time. For toxicologists focused on public health or industrial impacts, EHP is indispensable.

3. Archives of Toxicology

Among the oldest and most widely respected journals in its field, Archives of Toxicology is an interdisciplinary heavyweight. With roots dating back decades, it consistently publishes innovative research that blends classic toxicology principles with cutting-edge discoveries in genetics, toxicokinetics, and molecular mechanisms.

Known for its global perspective, the journal attracts contributions from researchers worldwide, ensuring diverse approaches to toxicological challenges. Topics range from nanotoxicology and epigenetics to in vitro testing methods and carcinogenicity studies. The journal also emphasizes the development of new toxicological models, often exploring the boundaries of what’s possible in the field.

The high-impact nature of the journal makes it especially useful to academic toxicologists and practitioners interested in applying research findings to real-world toxicology issues. Its content is both deeply scientific and incredibly practical.

4. Food and Chemical Toxicology (Elsevier)

Food and chemical safety remain hot-button topics in toxicology, and Food and Chemical Toxicology is a clear leader in this specialized area. As its name suggests, the journal focuses on toxicological assessments of food substances, chemical contaminants, food additives, and dietary supplements.

A highlight of this publication is its emphasis on regulatory toxicology, offering in-depth analyses of safety evaluations tied to policy-making and public health initiatives. Recent publications delve into issues like pesticide residues, artificial sweetener safety, and food packaging migration. Researchers studying dietary toxicology or working with consumer safety agencies will find this journal particularly relevant.

Food and Chemical Toxicology also frequently publishes papers related to alternative testing methods, reflecting the evolving regulatory landscape surrounding animal testing in safety evaluations. Its reach extends from lab-based findings to guiding worldwide safety indexes for food products.

5. Toxicology Reports (Elsevier)

For those looking for open-access options catering to a broad toxicology audience, Toxicology Reports is a must-read. This journal welcomes a wide array of toxicity-related content, including preclinical analytics, environmental monitoring, biomedical toxicology, and innovative methodologies.

Toxicology Reports has been gaining attention in recent years for its rapid publication model, which ensures timely dissemination of critical findings. Whether it’s new developments in nanoparticle toxicology, pioneering methods for assessing organ-specific toxicity, or biochemical mechanisms, this journal has something to offer all toxicologists. Its open-access structure ensures equitable knowledge sharing and fosters collaboration within the broader scientific community.

6. Critical Reviews in Toxicology (Taylor & Francis)

True to its name, Critical Reviews in Toxicology provides in-depth reviews, critical discussions, and comprehensive surveys of toxicology research. This journal goes beyond merely presenting results, offering readers valuable interpretations and critical analyses of study findings.

A central feature of this journal is its focus on advancements in toxicological sciences. It helps readers identify key takeaways from complex studies, appreciate emerging methodologies, and recognize knowledge gaps that require further research. This makes it an excellent resource for educators, graduate students, and even policymakers who need a broader understanding of toxicology’s evolving landscape.

From systemic toxicology reviews to meta-analyses of chemical risk assessments, this journal ensures that its audience is equipped with well-rounded, evidence-informed insights.

7. Regulatory Toxicology and Pharmacology (Elsevier)

In an age of strict regulatory scrutiny, toxicology operates under constant oversight tied to public safety, drug development, and chemical testing. Regulatory Toxicology and Pharmacology carves out its niche by focusing on the intersection of toxicology, public policy, and law.

This journal frequently covers risk-benefit analyses, toxicological profiles necessary for product registration, and legal frameworks governing toxic substances. With its emphasis on real-world applications, itâ€™s immensely useful for professionals working in industrial and regulatory sectors. The journal often features collaborative work between researchers and policymakers, resulting in papers that steer the direction of regulatory frameworks globally.

Whether youâ€™re in the pharmaceutical industry, an environmental agency, or a governmental role, this journal bridges scientific rigor with practical implications, giving readers insights into the policymaking process.

8. Journal of Applied Toxicology

Applied toxicology is all about translating toxicology research into actionable practices across industries, and the Journal of Applied Toxicology specializes in just that. Itâ€™s an excellent resource for those focused on occupational exposure, industrial chemicals, and product safety.

This journal frequently explores biomarkers of exposure, toxicogenomics, and dermal toxicology, among other areas. Recent focus areas also include artificial intelligence in predictive toxicology and advances in computational modeling for risk analysis. With an emphasis on practical solutions and new technologies, this is a forward-looking publication that appeals to researchers seeking to apply laboratory findings in real-world applications.

Final Thoughts

Toxicology journals play a pivotal role in advancing our understanding of how substances interact with living systems. By keeping up with these publications, you not only gain access to the latest findings in toxicology but also ensure that your knowledge aligns with current methodologies, regulatory standards, and societal needs. Whether your interest lies in pharmaceutical toxicology, environmental hazards, or food safety, the journals highlighted above can serve as your compass in navigating this diverse and rapidly innovating field.

Science evolves at a remarkable pace, and toxicology is no exception. Each of these journals contributes uniquely to the field, encompassing everything from foundational research to applied practices. As the challenges surrounding toxic exposure grow increasingly complex in 2023, staying informed through these top-tier resources is crucial for anyone involved in toxicology. So add these titles to your reading list, and explore the cutting-edge developments shaping the future of health and safety.
