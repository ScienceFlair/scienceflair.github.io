Nature and Nurture: The Influence of Geography and Landscape on Ancient Cultures


Geography and landscape have long been influential forces in shaping human societies. In the case of ancient cultures, the surrounding landscape and geography often played a major role in determining the ways in which they developed, adapted, and thrived. In this blog post, we will delve into the fascinating world of ancient cultures and explore the impact of nature and nurture on their formation and development.

Beginning with the classic example of ancient Egypt, we can see the significant influence of geography on the development of their civilization. The Nile River provided a natural source of water and fertile land, which enabled the growth of crops and the establishment of settlements. As such, Egyptians were able to develop a highly organized and advanced civilization backed by a stable food source and a well-thought-out system of irrigation. Moreover, the desert landscape surrounding the Nile River forced the ancient Egyptians to develop unique methods of dealing with their environment, including the construction of monumental structures such as pyramids to serve as tombs for their pharaohs.

In contrast, let’s consider the ancient Minoan civilization on the island of Crete. This population was significantly impacted by the landscape and the available resources. The abundance of seafood in the Mediterranean Sea, combined with a mild climate and fertile soil, allowed the Minoans to develop a sophisticated culture with advanced skills such as pottery, music, and the arts. Additionally, the presence of multiple natural caves on the island provided them with shelter and opportunities for ritualistic practices.

The landscape of ancient Mesopotamia, located in modern-day Iraq, played a significant role in the formation and development of the Sumerians. Surrounded by mountains and crisscrossed by rivers such as the Tigris and Euphrates, the region provided fertile land for agriculture and a natural resource for water management. This allowed the development of an advanced irrigation system and led to the growth of complex societies with a hierarchical structure. The availability of nearby resources such as timber, metals, and other materials encouraged the development of trade networks that propelled the region’s growth and prosperity.

The landscape of ancient China also had a profound impact on its cultures. The vastness of the country and the variation in geographical regions led to the development of diverse and dynamic cultures. The development of agriculture in the Yellow River Valley provided the foundation for the growth of early Chinese societies. Meanwhile, the rugged terrain of the west prompted the growth of unique and innovative societies, while the coastal regions fostered the establishment of thriving seaports and trade routes.

Geography and landscape continue to have a profound impact on societies, even today. The formation, development, and adaptation of ancient cultures provide an intriguing window into this complex and fascinating aspect of human history, effectively illustrating the intricate relationship between nature and nurture. Thus, understanding this dynamic relationship can provide insights into the trajectories of ancient civilizations and, by extension, contemporary societies as well.
