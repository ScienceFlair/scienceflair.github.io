The Role of Botany in Indigenous Knowledge Systems: Preserving Traditional Plant Wisdom


Indigenous knowledge systems have long served as repositories of invaluable wisdom, passed down through generations and rooted in a deep connection to the land. At the heart of these traditional practices lies botany, the study of plants and their uses, which plays a vital role in preserving and perpetuating indigenous cultures around the world. By understanding the intricate relationships between plants and people, we can unlock a treasure trove of traditional plant knowledge that holds the key to sustainable living, holistic health, and cultural resilience. Botany forms the cornerstone of indigenous knowledge systems, encompassing a rich tapestry of plant lore, medicinal practices, and ecological insights that have sustained communities for centuries. From identifying edible plants to harnessing the healing properties of botanical remedies, indigenous peoples have honed their understanding of plant life through keen observation, experimentation, and storytelling. This profound connection to the plant world not only fulfills practical needs but also nurtures spiritual beliefs, cultural identity, and environmental stewardship.

Keywords: indigenous knowledge systems, traditional plant wisdom, botanical knowledge, plant lore, medicinal practices, ecological insights

Beyond its practical applications, botanical knowledge is deeply intertwined with cultural heritage and identity.
