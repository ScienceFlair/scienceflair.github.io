How to Select Electrical Conduits and Raceways


When it comes to ensuring a safe and efficient electrical installation, selecting the appropriate conduits and raceways is crucial. These components not only protect wiring from physical damage but also aid in managing and organizing electrical systems. With various types of conduits and raceways available, choosing the right one can be overwhelming. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the key considerations, types, and applications to help you make an informed decision. Electrical conduits are protective tubes through which electrical wires are run. They provide physical protection and help route the wiring throughout a building or structure. Raceways, on the other hand, are enclosed conduits or channels designed to hold electrical wires, cables, or busbars, providing both protection and organization.

Key Considerations in Selecting Conduits and Raceways

When selecting the appropriate conduits and raceways for your project, consider the following factors:

  1. Application and Environment: Determine where the conduit or raceway will be installed. Is it indoors or outdoors? Will it be exposed to moisture, chemicals, or extreme temperatures?
  2. Material: Choose a material suitable for the environment and type of installation.
  3. Size and Capacity: Ensure the conduit or raceway can accommodate the number and size of wires or cables you plan to install.
  4. Regulations and Codes: Follow local building codes and regulations, which may dictate specific types of conduits or raceways for certain applications.
  5. Ease of Installation: Consider how easy it is to install the conduit or raceway, especially in tight or complex spaces.

Types of Electrical Conduits

Rigid Metal Conduit (RMC)

Rigid Metal Conduit (RMC) is a heavy-duty conduit made from coated steel or aluminum. It provides excellent protection against physical damage and is typically used in industrial environments.

  • Applications: Suitable for outdoor and indoor use, particularly in industrial settings.
  • Pros: High durability, excellent protection, and grounding capabilities.
  • Cons: Heavy and difficult to install, higher cost.

Intermediate Metal Conduit (IMC)

Intermediate Metal Conduit (IMC) is lighter and thinner than RMC but still offers substantial protection. It is made from galvanized steel.

  • Applications: Ideal for industrial and commercial applications, both indoors and outdoors.
  • Pros: Lighter than RMC, corrosion-resistant, easier to install.
  • Cons: Less robust than RMC, higher cost compared to non-metallic options.

Electrical Metallic Tubing (EMT)

Electrical Metallic Tubing (EMT), often referred to as thin-wall conduit, is a lightweight and easy-to-install conduit made from galvanized steel.

  • Applications: Commonly used in commercial and industrial buildings, ideal for indoor use.
  • Pros: Lightweight, easy to install, cost-effective.
  • Cons: Not suitable for high-stress environments, limited outdoor use without additional protection.

Flexible Metal Conduit (FMC)

Flexible Metal Conduit (FMC), also known as Greenfield, is a spiral-wound conduit that is highly flexible and easy to install in tight spaces.

  • Applications: Ideal for areas requiring flexibility, such as around machinery or equipment.
  • Pros: Highly flexible, easy to install in confined spaces.
  • Cons: Less protection against physical damage, not suitable for all environments.

Liquidtight Flexible Metal Conduit (LFMC)

Liquidtight Flexible Metal Conduit (LFMC) is similar to FMC but features a plastic coating that makes it watertight.

  • Applications: Suitable for environments exposed to moisture, such as outdoor installations or areas with high humidity.
  • Pros: Flexible, waterproof, durable.
  • Cons: More expensive than standard FMC, can be bulky.

Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) Conduit

Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) Conduit is a non-metallic conduit made from plastic. It is lightweight, easy to install, and corrosion-resistant.

  • Applications: Commonly used in underground and wet environments.
  • Pros: Cost-effective, corrosion-resistant, lightweight.
  • Cons: Limited mechanical protection, not suitable for high-temperature environments.

High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) Conduit

High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) Conduit is a flexible and durable non-metallic conduit used in various environments.

  • Applications: Ideal for underground and outdoor installations, often used in telecommunications.
  • Pros: Flexible, resistant to corrosion and chemicals, easy to install.
  • Cons: Limited mechanical protection, requires special fittings for connections.

Types of Electrical Raceways

Surface-Mounted Raceway

Surface-Mounted Raceway is installed on the surface of walls or ceilings, providing an easy way to add or alter electrical wiring without cutting into walls.

  • Applications: Ideal for retrofit projects, commercial, and residential installations.
  • Pros: Easy to install and modify, cost-effective.
  • Cons: Visible on walls, may not be aesthetically pleasing.

Underfloor Raceway

Underfloor Raceway is installed beneath flooring, providing a hidden pathway for electrical wiring and data cables.

  • Applications: Common in office buildings and other commercial settings.
  • Pros: Concealed wiring, protects against physical damage.
  • Cons: More complex installation, higher cost.

Wireway

Wireway is a type of raceway with a hinged or removable cover, making it easy to install and maintain wiring.

  • Applications: Suitable for industrial and commercial environments.
  • Pros: Easy access for maintenance, protects against physical damage.
  • Cons: Limited to shorter runs compared to conduit.

Ladder Tray

Ladder Tray is an open, ladder-like structure used to support and route large volumes of cable.

  • Applications: Ideal for industrial settings with extensive cabling needs.
  • Pros: Supports high cable volume, easy to install and maintain.
  • Cons: Less protection for cables, requires additional support for long spans.

Choosing the Right Conduit or Raceway

To select the right conduit or raceway, follow these steps:

  1. Assess the Environment: Identify environmental factors such as moisture, temperature, and exposure to chemicals or physical damage.
  2. Determine Electrical Load: Calculate the electrical load and determine the size and number of wires or cables.
  3. Consult Regulations: Check local electrical codes and regulations to ensure compliance.
  4. Evaluate Installation Requirements: Consider the ease of installation and maintenance.
  5. Balance Cost and Performance: Weigh the cost against the performance requirements and longevity.

Best Practices for Installation

Planning and Design

  • Plan Ahead: Create a detailed layout plan to identify the best routes for conduits and raceways.
  • Measure Accurately: Measure distances accurately to determine the correct lengths of conduit or raceway.
  • Avoid Obstacles: Plan routes that avoid obstacles and minimize bends.

Installation Tips

  • Use Appropriate Tools: Use tools designed for cutting and bending conduits to ensure clean and precise installations.
  • Secure Properly: Secure conduits and raceways with appropriate clamps and fasteners to prevent movement and damage.
  • Follow Manufacturer Guidelines: Adhere to the manufacturer’s installation instructions for optimal performance.

Safety Considerations

  • Turn Off Power: Always turn off power to the area where you are working to avoid electrical hazards.
  • Wear Protective Gear: Use safety glasses, gloves, and other protective gear to prevent injuries.
  • Check for Compliance: Ensure your installation complies with local codes and standards.

Conclusion

Selecting the right electrical conduits and raceways is vital for ensuring the safety and efficiency of your electrical installations. By considering the application, environment, material, size, and regulatory requirements, you can choose the appropriate conduit or raceway for your needs. Whether you are working on a commercial, industrial, or residential project, following best practices and safety guidelines will help you achieve a successful and compliant installation.
