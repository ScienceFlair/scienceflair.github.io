Time Study Best Practices: A Guide for Industrial Engineers


Time studies are essential tools for industrial engineers and operations managers who want to optimize workflows, improve productivity, and reduce inefficiencies. By closely examining the time it takes to complete specific tasks or operations, engineers can identify areas where performance can be enhanced. However, conducting an effective time study requires more than just observing and recording data; it requires a structured approach that ensures the results are both accurate and actionable.

In this guide, we’ll explore time study best practices for industrial engineers. Whether you’re new to time studies or looking to refine your existing processes, these practices will help you conduct effective, insightful studies that lead to real improvements in productivity.

What Is a Time Study?

A time study involves observing and recording the amount of time required to complete a task or set of tasks. The goal is to understand the time requirements for specific activities, allowing you to identify inefficiencies, improve processes, and determine the best way to allocate resources. Time studies are used to set performance standards, allocate workloads, plan staffing, and even design new workflows.

The process typically involves using a stopwatch or other time-tracking devices to measure the time it takes to complete an activity, followed by analyzing the data to make decisions. It is a systematic and methodical approach, often involving the following steps:

  1. Task Selection: The first step is to identify the task to be studied. It should be well-defined and representative of typical work.
  2. Observation and Data Collection: The next step is to observe the task in action and record the time it takes to complete each step.
  3. Data Analysis: Once the data is collected, it is analyzed to identify trends, bottlenecks, and areas for improvement.
  4. Recommendations and Implementation: The final step involves using the data to make recommendations for improvements and implement changes that lead to better efficiency and productivity.

Best Practices for Conducting Effective Time Studies

Time studies can be incredibly valuable tools when done correctly. However, to get the most out of them, it’s important to follow best practices that ensure accuracy and reliability. Here are the top practices that industrial engineers should keep in mind when conducting time studies.

1. Define Clear Objectives

Before starting any time study, it’s critical to define clear objectives. What do you want to achieve? Are you looking to reduce cycle time, improve throughput, or measure operator performance? Defining your objectives will help guide the study and ensure that you collect relevant data.

Without clear objectives, you risk collecting unnecessary data or focusing on the wrong aspects of a task. Be specific about what you want to achieve, and make sure your time study is designed to address those goals.

2. Select Representative Tasks

Selecting the right tasks to study is another crucial factor. Ensure the tasks you choose are representative of the work being performed. A time study on an outlier task that only occurs occasionally might not provide useful insights for overall operations.

The selected task should also be complex enough to show opportunities for improvement but not too complex to make the study difficult to analyze. If possible, choose tasks that are performed frequently, as they will have a greater impact on overall productivity.

3. Use Standardized Methods and Equipment

When conducting a time study, it’s important to use standardized methods and equipment. Consistency is key to ensuring the accuracy of your results. If you’re using a stopwatch, be sure that it’s calibrated and that everyone involved in the study is using the same method to track time.

In addition, there are software tools available that can help streamline the process. These tools often provide more advanced features like automated data logging, which can save time and reduce human error.

4. Observe the Work in Its Natural Environment

When conducting a time study, it’s crucial to observe the work in its natural setting. This means that workers should perform the task as they normally would, without any artificial changes. While this approach ensures that you are studying real-world conditions, it also means that you need to ensure the environment is as free from disruptions as possible.

Don’t try to “improve” the process while you are studying it. Your goal is to capture how the task is currently performed, not to influence it in real-time. Any changes made during the observation could skew the data and lead to inaccurate conclusions.

5. Account for Variables and Interruptions

Real work environments are rarely perfect. Interruptions, delays, and other variables can all affect the time it takes to complete a task. When conducting a time study, it’s important to account for these interruptions, as they can significantly impact the results.

For instance, you may want to measure the time it takes to perform a task during regular shifts versus periods with more frequent interruptions. Additionally, consider factors such as fatigue, machine breakdowns, or supply chain delays. These variables can skew the data and should be factored into your analysis.

6. Use Multiple Observations for Accurate Results

To ensure the accuracy of your time study, it’s essential to take multiple observations of the task in question. A single observation will not be enough to account for variations in performance or external factors.

Aim for at least three to five separate observations to get a good baseline. You can then calculate the average time for the task and identify any variations that need to be addressed. If the task is highly variable, more observations may be necessary to get a reliable result.

7. Break the Task Into Smaller Elements

When performing a time study, it’s helpful to break the task into smaller, more manageable elements. This will allow you to study the individual components of the task and identify inefficiencies at each stage.

For example, if you are studying the process of assembling a product, break the task down into steps such as retrieving materials, assembling parts, packaging, and moving the completed product. By focusing on individual elements, you can identify where time is being wasted and make targeted improvements.

8. Collect Data Consistently and Accurately

Data accuracy is paramount in a time study. Ensure that you are consistently recording the time for each element of the task and that you are using a reliable system to track the data. This will help you avoid errors that could affect the study’s reliability.

To improve accuracy, it’s helpful to use multiple observers or automated systems that can track time more precisely. Additionally, make sure that the data is recorded promptly after each observation to avoid confusion and mistakes.

9. Analyze the Data Carefully

Once the time study is complete, it’s time to analyze the data. Start by calculating the average time for the task, as well as the standard deviation to understand how much variability exists.

Look for trends or patterns in the data. Are there specific steps that take longer than others? Are there any significant variations that can’t be explained by external factors? Identifying these trends will allow you to pinpoint inefficiencies and prioritize areas for improvement.

It’s also important to compare the results of your time study with industry standards or benchmarks. This can give you a sense of whether the current process is competitive or needs improvement.

10. Use the Data to Drive Improvements

The final step in a time study is to use the data to drive improvements. Once you’ve identified inefficiencies or bottlenecks, you can implement strategies to improve the process.

For example, if a task is taking longer than expected, consider whether automation, training, or changes to the workflow could help reduce the time required. In some cases, it may be necessary to redesign the task entirely to eliminate unnecessary steps or streamline the process.

Make sure to communicate your findings with relevant stakeholders, including workers, managers, and department heads. Share the data and explain how the proposed changes will improve productivity, reduce costs, or enhance efficiency. Collaboration and buy-in are crucial for the successful implementation of time study findings.

11. Follow Up and Monitor Results

After implementing improvements based on your time study, it’s important to monitor the results over time. Is the task consistently completed within the desired time frame? Are there any unexpected challenges that arise? Regular follow-up ensures that the changes made are sustainable and effective.

Sometimes, processes may need further adjustments as conditions change. Continuous improvement is key to maintaining efficiency and staying competitive in an ever-evolving industry.

Conclusion

Time studies are powerful tools for industrial engineers looking to optimize operations and improve productivity. By following best practices, such as defining clear objectives, selecting representative tasks, observing in natural environments, and analyzing data accurately, industrial engineers can gain valuable insights into workflow efficiency.

Remember that time studies are not a one-time task. They should be an ongoing part of your continuous improvement efforts. By consistently refining your processes based on accurate time studies, you can ensure that your operations remain efficient, competitive, and aligned with organizational goals.

Incorporating these best practices into your time study approach will not only improve your efficiency but also create a data-driven culture of continuous improvement within your organization.
