The Ultimate Guide to Workplace Ergonomics: Improving Health & Productivity


Did you know that, on average, adults spend around 90,000 hours of their lives at work? With such a significant amount of time spent in the office, ensuring a comfortable and ergonomic workspace becomes crucial. Adequate workplace ergonomics not only contribute to employee well-being but also increase overall productivity and efficiency.

WHAT IS WORKPLACE ERGONOMICS? Workplace ergonomics is the scientific discipline of designing the workspace and equipment to fit the worker’s physical and psychological needs. It aims to ensure that a workspace is safe, healthy, and comfortable for employees, allowing them to perform efficiently and meet organizational goals. Ergonomics goes beyond mere adjustments of desks, chairs, and computer monitors; it extends to various aspects of the workplace including job design, lighting, and ventilation.

TOP TIPS FOR CREATING AN ERGONOMIC WORKPLACE

  1. Furniture and Posture: The right furniture promotes a good posture while working. Chairs should be adjustable to support the individual’s seated posture, and desks should be at the right height to avoid hunching or slouching over a computer. Additionally, safely positioned monitor stands and Healthy Posture stools for breaks can encourage our sit-stand workstation transition correctly.

  2. Lighting: Proper lighting facilitates better concentration, reduces eye strain, and prevents accidents. Combine natural with ambient lighting to reduce the need for artificial lighting and create a warm environment. Adjust the desktop computer’s screen brightness and contrast to match individual preferences.

  3. Access to water: Drinking water can prevent dehydration and boost productivity. Keeping a water bottle within reach and encouraging regular breaks can ensure employees have plenty of fluids throughout the workday.

  4. Sound and Temperature: A comfortable noise level reduces the risk of hearing damage and contributes to a mental break in a bustling workplace. The ideal temperature should be between 21-22°C (70-72°F) to optimize comfort, and prevent extension of stay in the workplace.

  5. Take frequent breaks: Encourage employees to take regular short breaks to promote blood flow and muscle movement. This can prevent aches and pains associated with extended periods of sitting, hence reducing absenteeism due to illness.

  6. Ergonomic IT equipment: Adjust computer hardware like keyboards, mice, and monitors to an employee’s needs. Keyboard trays that permit switching from sitting to standing positions effectively reduce strain and help avoid repetitive stress injuries.

  7. Reduce physical fatigue: Employees should perform tasks like lifting heavy objects, bending, or squatting with care to reduce physical fatigue and risks associated with manual labor, such as muscle strain.

  8. Cognitive ergonomics: Encourage regular communication and feedback among employees to reduce stress and prevent cognitive fatigue. Training, rotation of duties, provide for mental breaks and ergonomically-designed work schedules can significantly improve productivity and mental health.

In conclusion, a well-designed ergonomic workplace can yield numerous benefits such as improved job satisfaction, overall employee health and wellbeing, and a tangible boost in productivity. As an employer or a team leader, a commitment to ergonomics is a manifestation of your dedication to your employees’ well-being and a nod to improved productivity. Adopt these easy-to-implement ergonomic practices to optimize your workplace environment. Lay the foundation for a healthier, more productive workforce with workplace ergonomics. Let’s begin this journey together!
