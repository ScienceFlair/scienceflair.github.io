The Influence of Leaders: The Psychological Phenomenon of Leadership and Followership


Leadership and followership have long been crucial components of society, shaping the outcome of group dynamics and contributing to individual growth. Understanding the influence of leaders on followers and the subsequent psychological phenomena can lead to better leadership, ultimately benefiting both parties in the long run.

Firstly, leaders possess four main characteristics that can shape the attitudes and approaches of their followers:

  1. Charisma: A magnetic personality that draws people toward their goals and ideas. In this case, the leader’s charisma can make their followers feel motivated and inspired to work towards the same goals. This may lead to improved performance and overall success of the group.

  2. Vision: A clear picture of where the group or organization is heading. This vision creates a sense of purpose and direction, enabling followers to see the bigger picture and strive for progress.

  3. Decisiveness: The ability to make decisions confidently and efficiently. Followers often rely on their leader’s decisiveness to guide their actions and alleviate uncertainty.

  4. Tactful Communication: Leaders who can effectively navigate and resolve conflicts without causing emotional upheaval throughout the group can create a more harmonious and productive environment.

Conversely, followers can influence their leaders through various means, including:

  1. Constructive Feedback: When provided with open-mindedness, constructive criticism fosters opportunities for improvement. A good follower will highlight their leader’s strengths and offer guidance on potential weaknesses.

  2. Determination: The follower’s commitment to their goals and values can inspire their leader to push for progress and drive their vision forward.

  3. Support: Encouraging their leader through tough times and celebrating victories goes a long way in fostering a strong bond. This support boosts the leader’s morale and leads to a more successful outcome.

  4. Perseverance: A follower’s drive to remain dedicated and committed can challenge the leader and ultimately contribute to the group’s achievements.

In the process of leadership and followership, trust is vital. Trust between parties can lead to increased synergy, enabling the group to accomplish more as a cohesive entity than they would independently. Trust in leadership often revolves around the leader’s competence, character, and communication style, while trust in followers depends on their reliability, integrity, and ability to inspire those around them.

However, power struggles can emerge when this balance of trust is disrupted. When followers begin to distrust their leader or vice versa, it can lead to conflicts, stagnation, and the dissolution of the group’s success.

The continuously evolving dynamic nature of leadership and followership demonstrates the complexity of this psychological phenomenon. As understanding of this relationship grows, so does our ability to grasp the intricacies of human behavior and the impact of effective leadership, leading to personal and professional success. By embracing both the role of a leader and a follower, a healthy balance can be achieved, benefiting the individual and the group as a whole.

In conclusion, an understanding and appreciation of the influence of leaders on followers and vice versa is a valuable asset in creating a successful, harmonious outcome. Leaders shape the psychology of followers, while followers provide essential feedback and drive progress in their group. As we navigate this ever-changing landscape, we must remember that leadership and followership are not at odds with each other, but rather, they are interconnected and necessary for a group’s success.
